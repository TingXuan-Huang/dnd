# Background: Ruin Scholar

*Homebrew · Fallowton campaign · Formatted like D&D 2024 Basic Rules backgrounds*

Use this background if your character spent years copying broken inscriptions off field-stones, reading the wrong books in the wrong libraries, or arguing with a tutor who finally told you to leave.

---

## Ruin Scholar

You did not grow up in Fallowton. You came here because the village sits on top of something worth reading. While the farmers watched the north field sink, you watched the lines along the exposed stone — the way one seal repeats every six paces, the way the carvings on the door-jamb are not decorative but instructive. You knew the script before you knew the village. You knew what a Dawnweave seal looked like, in copies in a tutor's notebook, before you ever stood near one that was still warm.

The local clergy calls your work meddling. The wizard calls it long overdue. Your tutor would have called it dangerous, but your tutor is not here.

You believe the seals can be read without being broken. The village hopes you are right.

**Ability Scores:** Intelligence, Wisdom, Dexterity  

**Feat:** [Skilled](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024/feats#Skilled) (you have spent years training your eye for languages, old hands, and the small marks that separate a binding seal from a memorial)

**Tool Proficiency:** [Calligrapher's Supplies](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/450-calligraphers-supplies) (rubbings, transcriptions, and careful copies of what you cannot remove)

## Optional personality prompts

- Which tutor or library cast you out, and what were you reading when they did? Be specific — the title or the room.
- You know one thing about the Dawnweave that you have never written down because writing it down would make you a target. What is it?
- The seal you most want to read is on the north field. There is one you are *afraid* to read. Which one, and why?

## Weave Strain (if you gain magic later)

You may not start as a spellcaster, but you understand the channel better than most who do. If you become one — or if you reach into a seal you should have left alone:

- **Reading** Dawnweave seals, copying their script, or comparing them to your notes does **not** cause Weave Strain and does **not** add Luck debt. Study is safe.
- **Activating** a seal — channeling magic through it, even briefly — does. Treat the activation as casting a leveled spell for strain purposes.
- If you attempt to activate a seal while you are already at strain **2 or higher**, you must make an **Intelligence (Arcana) DC 14** check. On a failure, you trigger an **Overreach Backlash** before the strain cost lands. The seal accepts what you give it and demands a little more.
- When you choose a **memory** strain option (forget a trivial detail, brief flicker, Dawnweave vision), count it as **+1 Luck debt** for tracking purposes. You remember the wrong things on purpose, and the channel notices.

See [Weave Strain Magic — Ruin Scholar](../../../docs/campaign/weave-strain-magic.md#custom-fallowton-backgrounds).
