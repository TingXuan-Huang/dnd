# The Pantheon of the Sealed Age

The gods are real. They are also distant. Every cleric in the world casts on credit drawn through the broken channel ([[weave-strain-magic]]); every prayer goes out past a sealed door. The temples teach this differently, but no priest under sixty has ever been answered out loud.

> See also: [[weave-strain-magic]] (Sealing / Dawnweave / Hollow Star), [[calendar]] (holidays that anchor the religious year).

## How worship works after the Sealing

Before the Sealing, the gods walked. The old fires-of-winter stories — rivers that healed, starlight you could catch — are half-true memories of that age, the same half-true memories that the Dawnweave architects exploited. When the architects tore at the world-magic core and let the **Hollow Star / Nameless Hunger** notice the wound, the gods sealed the core to save what remained. They paid for it. Most of what they were is on the other side of the seal.

What's left on this side is enough to keep clerics in business and Sister Elra in a job, but it isn't enough to argue with. **Prayer in the Sealed Age is a discipline, not a conversation.** The Office of Sealed Practice (see [[crown-and-office]], unbuilt) approves which forms of worship are orthodox in the crown's provinces. Villages keep older shapes.

## The Active Pantheon

Eight gods receive open worship today. Six are state-sanctioned. Two are folk patrons the crown tolerates because no provincial magistrate has ever successfully banned a harvest festival.

---

### Vassa, the Lamp-Bearer [CROWN]

- **Domain:** Light
- **Portfolio:** The seal itself. The light that holds the dark out. Wardens, lighthousekeepers, night watch.
- **Holy symbol:** A single upright flame inside a closed ring. Drawn one-stroke on doorframes during Lampset.
- **Holiday:** The **Lampset Vigil** (Lampset 12) — one candle in the western window, unspoken. The orthodox crown reading is that Vassa lit the first warding lamps when the gods sealed the core.
- **Worshipped in Fallowton by:** Sister Elra (see below). Most villagers light a candle once a year without remembering why.

### Orun the Ash-King [CROWN]

- **Domain:** War, Death
- **Portfolio:** Honorable killing, soldier's last rites, the burial of oathbreakers. Sees both edges of every sword.
- **Holy symbol:** A spear-point laid across an open palm.
- **Holiday:** The **Office's Walk** (Stillmonth 1). Office riders carry Orun's spear-and-palm on the back of their sealed warrants — a reminder that the law cuts both ways and someone has to do the cutting. (Provincial families do not light incense to him that day. Provincial families lock their doors.)
- **Worshipped in Fallowton by:** Bran Orwick keeps a spear-and-palm token in the Elder's Hall desk. He has not taken it out in years.

### Imrel of the Sown Hand [CROWN]

- **Domain:** Life, Nature
- **Portfolio:** Sown grain, lambing, mending. The patron of crown agronomy and the official blessing of the year's first plough.
- **Holy symbol:** A barley sheaf bound with a green cord.
- **Holiday:** **The Crowning** (Sownmonth 14) — state holiday that doubles as Imrel's planting blessing. The first sealed-age monarch was crowned during the planting rite to fuse legitimacy with the grain.
- **Worshipped in Fallowton by:** Goodwife Bessa hangs a green-cord sheaf above the Bent Plow's hearth every Sownmonth. Sister Elra leads the village's short Crowning blessing.

### Talune, the Quiet Door [CROWN]

- **Domain:** Death
- **Portfolio:** Clean death, named graves, the ledger of the dead. The god most invoked by undertakers and shrine-keepers who maintain village burial rolls.
- **Holy symbol:** A door drawn closed, with a key hanging beside (not in) the lock.
- **Holiday:** Honoured quietly during the **Long Hush** (leap-year sixth Sealing Day). Talune is said to walk the rolls and check that every name still has its face.
- **Worshipped in Fallowton by:** Sister Elra maintains the shrine ledger of village dead under Talune's sign. Maerla's brother is in that ledger — a fact Elra does not yet know to be afraid of.

### Hesh the Measurer [CROWN]

- **Domain:** Knowledge
- **Portfolio:** Records, weights, sealed warrants, lawful scribing. The god the Office prays to before riding out.
- **Holy symbol:** A two-pan balance with a single tally-mark above it.
- **Holiday:** Observed across all five **Sealing Days** (First through Fifth Hush) — the orthodox crown calendar treats them as the days Hesh re-balanced the books after the Sealing. Tax assessments are dated from Hesh's reckoning.
- **Worshipped in Fallowton by:** Nobody warmly. The grain-store keeper crosses two fingers (the balance) before opening a fresh tally-book. He would not call it worship. It is.

### Brenn of the Tempest-Yoke [CROWN]

- **Domain:** Tempest
- **Portfolio:** Storms one is supposed to survive. River crossings, sea-roads, the patron of carters and millers and anyone whose work is at the mercy of weather.
- **Holy symbol:** A yoke with a lightning-cut down its centre.
- **Holiday:** No fixed feast — observed on the first storm of each season.
- **Worshipped in Fallowton by:** Garret the miller has Brenn's yoke scratched into the lintel of the mill door. After he saw the blue light come up through the millstone, he scratched a second one beside it. He will not say why.

### Mother Sown-Beneath [FOLK]

- **Domain:** Nature, Life
- **Portfolio:** Fallow ground, the soil that holds the dead, the seed that waits one season longer. The patron of barley specifically — not grain generally.
- **Holy symbol:** Three barley grains arranged in a triangle, point-down.
- **Holiday:** **Barleynight** (Goldfall 27). The youngest binds the last sheaf; the oldest cuts it. The kissing and the bad new beer are hers. The crown tolerates her because the harvest does not happen without her name being said.
- **Worshipped in Fallowton by:** Everyone, on Barleynight, whether or not they would say so the rest of the year. Old Henric is her unofficial priest in the north-field rows.

### Kell the Hedge-Walker [FOLK]

- **Domain:** Trickery, Nature
- **Portfolio:** Crossroads, hedges, things found on the road. The god of poachers, smugglers, midwives who walked a long way to the wrong farmhouse, and any traveller who survived a forest they should not have.
- **Holy symbol:** Two crossed hawthorn twigs tied with red thread.
- **Holiday:** No state-recognised feast. Kell's small shrines sit at three-way crossings; a copper or a crust left there is the only liturgy.
- **Worshipped in Fallowton by:** Tamsin the smith — she ties a hawthorn cross to her doorframe each spring. Her husband Joren did not, the year he went into the western forest.

## The Dawnweave Mythos [MYTH]

These names survive in old verses, shrine murals nobody repaints, and the etymologies of months ([[calendar]]). The crown teaches them as allegory. The Office prefers they not be taught at all.

### Soun-Moon, the First Listener [MYTH]

- **In the verses:** the architect who taught the first priests to hear the world's magic.
- **DM truth (Religion / Arcana DC 17):** Soun-Moon was a Dawnweave **architect**, not a god. The fourth month is named for her. She designed the listening-chambers of the Houses of First Light — the rooms where Converter priests sat with a victim and recorded what was being taken from them. The "first listener" listened to people forgetting their own mothers.

### Bright-Leaf, the Anointed [MYTH]

- **In the verses:** the priest who walked into the first light and was crowned with green flame.
- **DM truth (Religion / Arcana DC 17):** Bright-Leaf was the title — *not the name* — of the first Converter-priest who survived the commissioning rite of the House of First Light. The fifth month is named for the title. The green flame was the visible side-effect of memory being drawn through a freshly-anointed Converter altar. Bright-Leaf himself died ten years later of a hollowing nobody could name.

### Reaph, the Hand That Builds [MYTH]

- **In the verses:** the smith-saint who hammered the lamps of First Light.
- **DM truth (Religion / Arcana DC 17):** Reaph was a Dawnweave architect, an engineer in the same cohort as Soun-Moon. The seventh month is named for him. The "lamps" he built were not lamps. They were the Converter focus-stones whose blue glow Garret the miller saw two months ago. Reaph's reinforcement-pattern is what Tamsin's hands are unconsciously following on the spearhead in her forge.

### The Hollow Star [MYTH — do not invoke]

- **In the verses:** Never named in any sanctioned text. Folk tales call it the *Nameless Hunger* and the *Eye-That-Closes-Last*.
- **DM truth (Religion / Arcana DC 17 *only if a PC asks specifically*):** The Hollow Star is not Dawnweave at all — it is what the Dawnweave's hubris let in. It fed on magic, memory, and the meaning of things. The gods sealed the world-magic core to wall it out, and the seal worked, and that is the only reason any of the active pantheon can still be prayed to. Saying its name three times in a sealed chamber is the kind of thing that ends a campaign.

## Sister Elra prays to Vassa, the Lamp-Bearer

**Canon resolution.** Elra is a sworn folk-priest of **Vassa**. Her shrine in Fallowton is one of the small road-side Vassa shrines that pre-date the crown's province-wide reorganisation; the village calls her faith "the old roadside faith" because the form is older than the current orthodox liturgy, not because the god is different.

The prayer she chants — *Keeper of the first light, do not wake* — is in **Dawnweave**, not because she is praying to a Dawnweave figure, but because the **petition-phrasing was passed down orally through the shrine line and never translated.** She is asking *Vassa* (a modern, post-Sealing god of wardlight) to keep the *Converter* (the House of First Light, under the north field) asleep. The chant works because Vassa's domain *is* the seal itself; the chant is in Dawnweave because the last person who could translate it died eight generations ago.

When a PC translates the chant for Elra (Arcana DC 15 or *comprehend languages*, per [[module-1-npcs]]), her horror is not that she has been praying to the wrong god. It is that she has been praying *the right prayer in the wrong language*, **to the right god**, *about a thing she did not know was real.*

## DM use — pantheon at the table

Six moments where the pantheon should appear without anyone giving a lecture about it.

1. **The candle in the window.** When the party arrives in Fallowton, one upper window of the Bent Plow has a candle in it even at noon. *"Bessa's lit Vassa's lamp early this year."* That's the entire line. (Vassa, [CROWN]; light it during any session set near Lampset.)
2. **The token in Bran's hand.** If the elder is rattled and not showing it, have him fidget with a small iron spear-and-palm token from his desk drawer. PC who recognises it (Religion DC 13) knows Orun. PC who served under crown banners knows what it means that Bran is holding it. (Orun, [CROWN].)
3. **The smith's curse.** When Tamsin burns her hand on the spearhead she's been forging unconsciously to a Dawnweave pattern, she swears *"by Kell's red thread"* — a hedge-walker's oath, the one a smith would not use unless something had genuinely scared her. (Kell, [FOLK]; cues the player that something is wrong.)
4. **The grain-keeper's tally.** When the grain-store keeper opens his fresh ledger to show the chewed sacks, he crosses two fingers over the page before he writes. PC who catches it (passive Insight 12) recognises Hesh's balance. He'd deny he was praying. He was. (Hesh, [CROWN].)
5. **The oath at the rim.** If a PC swears an oath at the sinkhole's edge — to recover a friend, to seal the breach, anything — let them choose **which god they swear by.** That choice is now campaign canon and the DM should track it: a Vassa-oath at the rim is a *different* commitment than an Orun-oath, and the world should answer accordingly in later modules.
6. **The prayer Elra won't translate.** If the party hears Elra's full chant (Religion DC 12 catches a phrase, History DC 14 dates the language as Dawnweave), let them notice that *Vassa's name is in there* — pronounced in the older form. The chant is a Vassa-petition. The fact that Elra doesn't know that is exactly what makes the moment land.
