Pre-session prep · 5 minutes

  • Open module-1-npcs.html in a second window — six NPCs you'll voice today.
  • Open module-1-checks.md if you want the master DC list within reach.
  • Write the Awakening Clock on a sticky note: 0 / 6. Hide it.
  • Write the Day Clock on another note: Sunrise → Forenoon → Midday → Afternoon → Sunset. Tick across as locations are visited.
  • Tell players, before the first read-aloud:
    "You have one day in Fallowton before the village forces a decision about the sinkhole. Use it however you like — but the village will not wait for you forever."
  • Make sure each PC has answered: Why are you in Fallowton? (use d8 hook table in npcs file if stuck).

Act I — A Day in Fallowton

Time: Sunrise → Sunset · Day 1 of the campaign · ~90 minutes table time

Fixed Pivot

Opening · The Bent Plow Inn

Sunrise · ~15 min

清晨，雨终于停了。

The Bent Plow Inn 的窗户结着薄薄一层水汽，木门半开着，让一缕灰白色的晨光斜斜地落在地板上。屋里有炉火残留的烟味、湿羊毛味，还有 Bessa Thorn 正在揉面的声音。她的手腕沾着面粉，头也不抬。

角落里坐着三个农夫，已经在低声争吵。

"那个洞自己会闭上的。"
"它会闭上的吗？我可没听见它闭上。"
"老 Henric 的麦田全毁了——他这把年纪还能再种一季吗？"

窗边那张桌子边，独自坐着一个穿着黑色旅袍的男人。他的手指上戴着几枚银戒，面前摆着一盏不曾点燃的 pale wood staff。他听见你们进门的脚步，抬起头——眼神平静，却让屋里其他几张脸不约而同地转开。

他向你们点了点头。

"早安。我叫 Mordren Vale。坐下吧——如果你们今天有空，我有一份工作给你们。"

What this scene does:
  • Plants Mordren as the job-giver. He is polite, never overbearing.
  • Establishes that the village is afraid of the sinkhole — three farmers' argument seeds the rumor.
  • Lets every PC introduce themselves to each other if they don't yet know one another.
Mordren's offer (deliver in your own words):
  • Descend the sinkhole stairs. Look. Report. Return.
  • Touch nothing you do not understand. Bring back any writing, relic, or "living flame."
  • Pay: 1 sp each in advance, plus a fair share of anything recovered. If they bring back a relic he wants, +5 gp each.
  • He does NOT tell them: he suspects this is a Dawnweave Converter, not a temple. He does NOT tell them: he has royal backing. (Save these for later modules.)
If players ask why him: "I am a scholar of ruins. The village does not want one of its own to die down there. I do not want this to be sealed before I understand what it is."
Open check Insight DC 12 — Mordren is telling part of the truth. Success: "He is hiding more than he is saying, but he believes what he says." Fail: "Seems sincere."
Silent check Passive Perception 13 — Mordren's silver ring flickers blue when the farmers say 'cave.' On a quiet beat, narrate it only to the highest passive PC. No need to call for a roll.
End of scene: Mordren says he'll be at the cottage west of the mill if they need him. He stands, leaves a coin under his mug, and goes. The day is theirs. Clock +0 — nothing in the ruin has noticed yet.

Free Day · Pick 3–4 locations

Forenoon → Afternoon · ~50 min
How the freedom works:
  • The party can split up or stick together. Each location visited burns one "time slot." They have 4 slots before the sunset bell rings.
  • If they try to visit a 5th location, narrate the bell ringing partway through — they only get half of what was there.
  • If they linger at one location ("we spend two hours here"), that's 2 slots. Let them choose.
  • Locations they skip stay available in Module 2 the next morning — but the NPCs will remember being ignored.
Suggested party split: If a Sinkhole Witness PC exists, encourage them to visit the Mill Shed or the actual sinkhole rim alone — Garret will speak more freely to one of their own.

Seven locations. Three to four are realistic. Each has one open check and one silent check; both are catalogued in module-1-checks.md. Quick blurbs below — click "Full scene" for the dedicated location page.

1 · The Bent Plow Inn

Bessa Thorn · innkeeper · rumor hub
Returning lets PCs press Bessa for what she overheard. She knows Mordren mutters "House of First Light" in his sleep — but only if they buy two rounds for the farmers OR pass Persuasion DC 12.
Full scene →

2 · Elder's Hall

Elder Bran Orwick · authority · gatekeeper
Bran will demand the party justify why strangers should be allowed under Fallowton land. He fronts hostile but wants the same thing they do: nobody dies. Bond is 10 gp OR a useful item left as collateral.
Full scene →

3 · Village Shrine

Sister Elra · faith leader
Elra prays in the old tongue. Religion DC 12 catches one phrase: "keeper of the first light, do not wake." She fears the sinkhole is "holy failure" — but she may bless a torch, water, or weapon if asked respectfully.
Full scene →

4 · Smith Shed

Tamsin Bellows · smith
Sells weapons and metalwork only — no general goods. Can sharpen a blade in an hour (+1 to first damage roll in the ruin) for 5 sp. Knows the sinkhole stone is not from local quarries.
Full scene →

5 · Grain Store

Quiet keeper · food pressure
Goods are vanishing at night. Investigation DC 12 finds a small tooth-mark on a bag — goblin sign. The keeper hasn't told Bran; he doesn't want the bond raised.
Full scene →

6 · Mill Shed

Garret · miller · first witness
Garret saw blue light leaking from the ground three nights before the field collapsed. He told no one because his wife thought he was drunk. He will tell a Sinkhole Witness PC freely.
Full scene →

7 · Mordren's Cottage

Wizard's rented room · locked boxes
Only accessible with invitation (Persuasion DC 14 at the inn) OR Stealth DC 14 to slip in. Inside: candles, books, a sealed letter with a royal wax seal — they cannot open it without breaking it. If broken, Mordren knows.
Full scene →
Awakening Clock from the village day:
  • If they visit Mordren's cottage and read the sealed letter — Clock +1 (he reaches the cottage by sunset, knows something is wrong).
  • If a player casts a leveled spell in town — Clock +1, and the caster also rolls Weave Strain (see campaign rules).
  • Otherwise — Clock +0.
What the party should walk away with (any 2 of these means Act II goes smoothly):
  • Bran's permission (or paid bond)
  • A blessing, torch, or sharpened weapon
  • The goblin clue from grain store
  • Garret's blue-light testimony
  • A guess at "House of First Light"
Fixed Pivot

Sunset Bell · Convergence

Sunset · ~15 min

太阳落到树林后面，整个 Fallowton 被染成了一种潮湿的橙红色。

就在天色将暗未暗的那一刻，村中央 Elder's Hall 外面的 old bronze bell 被人撞响了。一声，两声，三声。低沉，缓慢，像有人在敲打整个村子的胸口。

村民们从田里、屋里、谷仓里走出来，沉默地朝那口钟聚拢。他们手里握着叉、铲、灯——不是武器，只是平日的农具，但今天看起来像在等待一个判决。

Elder Bran Orwick 站在钟下，雨水般灰白的胡须贴在颈口。Henric 站在他半步之后，眼眶发红。Sister Elra 已经在低声诵念。Mordren Vale 站在人群最外圈，背着那根 pale wood staff，没有走近。

Bran 没有看你们。他对着整个村子说话——但所有人都知道，这句话是说给你们听的。

Elder Bran Orwick"今晚之前，这件事要有一个回答。要么把它封上，要么有人下去看清楚。Fallowton 不能在每个晚上都听着那个洞呼吸。"

所有人转过头，望向你们。

The pivot: The party must declare one of three choices before the night ends. All three lead to descent within 12 in-fiction hours. Do not let them stall.
  • A · Descend now (torchlit night descent). Most common. Skip to Act II.
  • B · Wait till dawn. Mordren says quietly, "Then I will go tonight alone." If they don't stop him, he descends — and by morning his pale staff is propped against the sinkhole stairs, but he is gone. Skip to Act II at dawn; treat the staff as a found relic (it weighs nothing and pulses faintly when held in the ruin).
  • C · Refuse / try to seal. The sinkhole exhales after midnight — Henric's daughter Lila is missing by sunrise. Forced descent at dawn. Skip to Act II. Clock +1 (the ruin is no longer just listening).
Bran's posture: He prefers seal-or-descend, not delay. If the players want to negotiate the bond, this is the moment — see Bran's check matrix in module-1-checks.md.
Open check Persuasion DC 13 — convince Bran the descent will protect Fallowton. Success: bond waived; the village contributes 2 torches, 50 ft rope, and a small loaf. Fail: pay 10 gp OR leave one useful item as collateral.
Silent check Insight 14 vs Mordren — does he want them to go alone tonight? Success → narrate: "There is something almost relieved in his face when you say you'll wait." (He plans to go alone.)

Act II — Descent

Time: That night (option A) or the next dawn (option B/C) · ~30 min

Fixed Pivot

The Stairs · Down

你们沿着黑色石阶向下走。

雨声逐渐远去，取而代之的是一种低沉、潮湿的寂静。石阶两侧的墙壁上刻着古老的浮雕：农夫托着光，孩子仰望星辰，河流从大地流向天空，树木弯下枝条，好像正在听人说话。

但越往深处走，浮雕越残破。

有些人的脸被刮掉。
有些星星被凿成黑色的空洞。
有些手掌被刻意毁去，仿佛有人不希望后人知道他们曾经握着什么。
还有一些跪下的人没有名字。他们的影子被刻成细长的线，沿着地面流向同一个方向，像溪水流入井中。

Open check Athletics DC 10 — the lower stairs are slick with mud and seepage. Fail: 1d4 bludgeoning, drop one held item one step. Success: keep footing.
Silent check Wisdom DC 12 — does any PC sense the ruin noticing? On highest success, whisper to that one player only: "The stones at your back feel like they are turning their faces toward you." Do not narrate aloud.

最后，石阶结束了。

你们来到一片塌陷的地下大厅。这里不像完整的 temple，更像一座已经死去很久、又被泥土重新吞下的 ruin。断裂的石柱倒在地上，墙壁裂开，树根从天花板垂下。

大厅中央立着那座从地面上看见过的阶梯状建筑。它的上半部分像金字塔，苍白、冷硬、层层收拢；它的下半部分却沉入更深的黑暗，像一根石钉钉进大地。围绕它的地面刻着一圈又一圈的纹路，某些沟槽里积着灰色尘土，仿佛很久以前曾有什么东西在其中流动。

在金字塔前方，有一个干涸的石池。池底没有水，只有薄薄一层灰尘。灰尘中隐约能看见许多重叠的掌印，小的、大的、人的，几乎像人的。

大厅前方，有三条长长的黑暗隧道。

Hand the players a 30-second silence here. Let them look at each other. Let them ask, "Which way?" before you describe the tunnels in detail. That hesitation is the scene. Clock +0 (unless a spell was cast on the way down — then +1)

Act III — The Sampling

In-fiction: ~30 minutes underground · Table: ~45 min

Read this to yourself before the act:
  • No combat this session. The party is sampling — peering, listening, smelling. The boss fights live in Module 2.
  • The party may peer into all three tunnels in any order. Each peek = one in-fiction "minute" + Clock +1.
  • After they've peeked into 2 tunnels, foreshadow the Pool Shudder. After the 3rd, fire it.
  • If a player tries to enter a tunnel beyond the mouth: allow one small room of exploration only, then the Shudder forces retreat. Award the clue, deny the combat.

Left Tunnel · Blue Light

Mouth-peek only

最左边的隧道飘着一些细小的蓝色光点。

它们漂浮在半空中，像萤火虫，又像有人在黑暗中慢慢眨眼。它们既不靠近你，也不远离你。它们就只是看着。

在隧道入口前的地上，半埋在尘土里，有一把 broken dagger。刀身已经断裂，但握柄上还残留着古老的纹路，以及一对几乎被磨平的字母——你看不太清。

Open check Investigation DC 12 — the dagger. Success: initials H.V. or W.V. (DM picks; either reads as "a Voss"). History DC 12 piggyback: local make, 60–100 years old.
Silent check Wisdom 11 — what do the motes feel like? Highest success: whisper to that PC: "Not malice. Vigilance." Do not call for a roll. If no one passes, motes feel cold and indifferent.
Atmosphere clues to drop (1–2 of these):
  • Cold dry air; faint smell of old iron.
  • The motes brighten by half a shade when a PC speaks softly. They dim if a PC raises their voice.
  • Far back in the dark, the outline of a figure that does not move. (Do not describe it as armored or alive yet. Just shape.)
If they take the dagger: note it on the player choice log. Henric will react when shown — see Module 2.
If they leave it: Edras (the guardian, Module 2) will note its absence positively. Clock +1 for the peek itself. +1 more if they touch a mote with bare skin.

Middle Tunnel · Sound

Mouth-peek only

中间的隧道传来奇怪的声音。

像是小东西在石头上爬行。像是骨头被拖过地面。像是一个孩子在很远的地方笑——又像是有人在模仿一个孩子笑。

有什么东西被吃过的金属在咯吱响。从隧道深处的方向，飘出一阵酸臭的烟味——油脂、毛发、和别的不该燃烧的东西。

Open check Perception DC 11 — tracks at the mouth. Success: small clawed footprints in the dust, three-toed, going both directions. Several days of traffic.
Silent check Insight 13 — the laugh. Highest success: whisper: "That is not a child. Something has been listening to children, and learned the shape."
If a PC calls out / shouts: the sounds stop instantly. Then, after three breaths, the laugh returns — closer. Clock +1.
If they cross-reference the grain-store goblin clue (from Act I): reward them — they recognize this as the same intruder. Advantage on first social roll vs goblin captives in Module 2.
Atmosphere drop: a single iron nail rolls out of the tunnel, comes to rest near a PC's boot. (Goblin "currency" — they chew nails.)

Right Tunnel · Empty Wind

Mouth-peek only

最右边的隧道最安静。

没有脚步声。没有低语。没有萤火般的光。只有一阵空洞的冷风从深处缓缓吹来，仿佛某个很大、很深、很久没有呼吸过的胸腔，正在试着记起怎样呼气。

那风经过你们身边时，像是在寻找某个人的名字。

Open check Arcana or Nature DC 13 — the wind. Success: this is not weather. The air pulses at roughly the rhythm of a sleeping heartbeat. Something is breathing, slowly, far below.
Silent check Wisdom 12 — the name-searching. Highest success: whisper to that PC only: "The wind passes your face and seems, for half a heartbeat, to know your name. Then it lets go." No mechanical effect. Just the dread.
What's actually here (DM only): circulating Dawnweave ward magic, the closest thing to the ruin's "mind." It is searching for an owner — a name to bind to. The right tunnel is where the ruin thinks.
Atmosphere drops:
  • A dead goblin half-impaled on a stone needle just inside the mouth. Already dead, days old. No loot but a leather cord with three iron nails.
  • Beyond the goblin, the tunnel turns once and disappears. You cannot see what is past the bend.
Clock +1 for the peek. +2 if any PC speaks their own name aloud in this tunnel mouth.
Fixed Pivot

Pool Shudder · The Ruin Stirs

After the 3rd tunnel peek (or 30 in-fiction minutes)

就在那一刻——

大厅中央那个干涸的石池，里面的灰尘忽然轻轻震动了一下。

不是被风吹动。不是被你们的脚步震动。是从下面，从更深的地方，传来一次低沉的、几乎听不见的呼气。

池底的尘土上，那些重叠的掌印，仿佛在那一瞬间——多了一只。

三条隧道里，所有的声音同时停下了一秒。

然后，左边的蓝光收得更近了。中间的笑声变成了某种警觉的咯咯。右边的风，停了。

This is the exit cue. Mordren (if present) says quietly: "Out. Now. We've seen enough." If Mordren is not present (option B), the senior-Wisdom PC feels it instead — let them say the line.
  • If players obey: Act IV.
  • If players refuse and push into a tunnel: allow them one room only, no boss. Award them one extra clue, then the room shifts (a corridor closes, a ward flickers, the floor groans) and they must retreat. Clock +2.
  • If they insist on full combat — pause the session. Tell them: "Module 2 is the deep run. Tonight we leave alive or we don't have a Module 2 at all." This is OK to say out-of-character; it's a pacing contract, not railroading.
Open check Constitution save DC 10 (group) — climb back up under the ruin's eye. Anyone failing arrives at the top trembling, disadvantage on first Charisma roll back in the village. Narrative only.

Act IV — Return & Cliffhanger

Late night or pre-dawn · ~20 min

Fixed Pivot

Surface · The First Night After

你们从沉睡的村庄边缘爬出来。北边的麦田一片死寂——只有那道塌陷处仍在缓缓呼气，像一头沉睡的兽。

云层被风撕开。月光落在你们身上的时候，你们才意识到——身上的尘土，是银灰色的。它不像泥，也不像石灰。它落在掌心，竟然有一点温度。

Fallowton 还在睡着。

但有什么，跟着你们一起回来了。

Pick ONE ending below. Use the triggers to decide. If two triggers tie, pick whichever feels strongest for your table.

A · Mordren Disappears

Trigger: Party chose option B (wait till dawn), OR broke into Mordren's cottage, OR was hostile to him at the inn.

You sleep in fits. You dream of white towers under a black sun, and of a man kneeling at a stair of pale stone, whispering a word you cannot remember when you wake.

By sunrise, Mordren is gone. His pale wood staff is propped against the sinkhole stairs. It is not warm. It is not cold. It hums, very faintly, when held over the opening.

Bran says only: "He went down. He did not come up."

B · Silver Leaves in the Well

Trigger: Party took anything physical from the ruin (the broken dagger, the iron-nail cord, a chip of stone).

You sleep. You wake. The village is shouting.

The old well at the center of Fallowton has filled overnight with silver leaves. They are from a tree no one knows. They are dry. They do not float — they hang, suspended, in clean cold water.

When a villager pulls one out, it crumbles to the same silver-gray dust on your boots.

Sister Elra refuses to draw water from the well until the leaves are gone. The leaves do not go.

C · The Forgotten Name

Trigger: Party ignored or were rude to an NPC, OR skipped 3+ village locations, OR cast a leveled spell in town.

You sleep. At dawn, someone is crying outside the inn.

It is Henric's wife, Maerla. She has forgotten the name of her own brother, dead twenty years. She remembers his face. She remembers the cart he drove. She does not remember his name.

Later that morning, a child climbing down the sinkhole rim out of curiosity finds something carved fresh into the pale stone, in a hand none of the villagers recognize:

a single name no one alive can read.

Closing line, choose one, end the session here:
  • "Tomorrow you go back down. Bran will not stop you this time."
  • "Mordren is gone. The staff is yours, if you want it."
  • "The well does not stop. The leaves keep coming."
Final clock readout (DM private): Note the Awakening Clock value. Module 2 begins at that stage. If clock ≥ 4, Mordren is gone regardless of which ending you picked.

After the session

Log these in your campaign journal before next session:
  • Which 3–4 village locations the party visited (and which they skipped — those NPCs now feel slighted).
  • Whether they paid the bond, persuaded Bran, snuck in, or refused.
  • Which tunnels they peered into, and in what order.
  • Whether they took the broken dagger / iron-nail cord / any stone.
  • Which ending fired, and the final Awakening Clock value.
  • Any PC personal hook (a Sinkhole Witness's reaction, a wizard's Weave Strain ticks, a name the wind seemed to know).
Hand off to Module 2: The next session opens at sunrise. The cliffhanger image is fact, not dream. They choose a tunnel to enter fully. Bring stat blocks.
