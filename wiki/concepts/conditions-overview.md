# Conditions Overview

A **condition** is a temporary game state. Each condition has specific mechanical effects; various rules and effects define how a condition ends. [BR-2024 §rules-glossary#Condition]

## The 15 official conditions

| Condition | One-line summary |
|-----------|------------------|
| [[blinded]] | Can't see; Disadv on attacks, Adv on attacks against you |
| [[charmed]] | Can't harm charmer; charmer has Adv on social checks |
| [[deafened]] | Can't hear; auto-fail hearing checks |
| [[exhaustion]] | 1–6 levels; −2 per level on d20 tests; −5 ft Speed per level; die at 6 |
| [[frightened]] | Disadv on checks/attacks while source visible; can't approach |
| [[grappled]] | Speed 0; Disadv on attacks against anyone but grappler |
| [[incapacitated]] | No action/bonus/reaction; no concentration; speechless |
| [[invisible]] | Adv on Initiative; concealed; Adv on your attacks, Disadv on attacks against |
| [[paralyzed]] | Incapacitated + Speed 0 + auto-fail Str/Dex + auto-crit within 5 ft |
| [[petrified]] | Turned to stone; Incapacitated; resist all damage; immune to Poisoned |
| [[poisoned]] | Disadv on attacks and ability checks |
| [[prone]] | Half-speed to stand; Disadv on attacks; Adv vs you within 5 ft |
| [[restrained]] | Speed 0; Adv against you; Disadv from you; Disadv on Dex saves |
| [[stunned]] | Incapacitated + auto-fail Str/Dex + Adv against you |
| [[unconscious]] | Incapacitated + Prone + auto-crit within 5 ft + unaware |

## General rules

- **No stacking.** A condition either applies or doesn't — you can't have it twice.
- **Exception:** [[exhaustion]] is cumulative across 1–6 levels.
- **Composition.** Some conditions include others as part of their effect:
  - [[paralyzed]], [[petrified]], [[stunned]], [[unconscious]] all include [[incapacitated]].
  - [[unconscious]] additionally includes [[prone]].
- **Ending a condition.** Each condition lists how it ends (passage of time, saves at end of turn, action to break free, healing to a positive HP, etc.). Many spells and class features can also remove conditions — e.g., *Lesser Restoration* ends one disease or Charmed/Paralyzed/Poisoned/Stunned condition.

[BR-2024 §rules-glossary#Condition]

## Related "condition-like" effects

These are not formal conditions but behave similarly:

- **Surprise** — Disadvantage on Initiative if caught unawares.
- **Bloodied** — A creature at half HP or less; some features key off this.
- **Concentration loss** — A spell ends; not technically a condition but a status change.
- **Difficult Terrain** — Movement cost effect, not a condition.
