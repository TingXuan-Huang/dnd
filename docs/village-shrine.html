The Village Shrine

Tended by Sister Elra · unnamed deity · older than the village

Floor Plan — The Village Shrine · Stone walls, older than the village name

The shrine is made of the same gray stone as the Old Hill above it — placed here long before Fallowton had its current name. Oil lamps burn at the four corners of the bench rows. The altar at the far end is plain, no icons, no face. Sister Elra kneels before it. She does not turn when you enter, but her back stiffens.

Who's Here

Sister Elra
Faith keeper · no formal church · serves "the old unnamed one" · 50s, silver-haired
Elra is not a healer by trade, but she knows which plants reduce fever and how to set a bone. She tends to people who have no one else. Since the cave opened, she has been here at the shrine from dawn until midnight, burning offerings and watching the hill. She does not sleep well. She believes the cave's blue light is not a haunting — it is something older, and that is worse.

What Elra Offers

  • Blessings: +1 to one saving throw of choice for the next 24 hours (once per character per long rest). She will not bless "those going to disturb what's buried."
  • Herbal kit: poultices, fever teas, basic antitoxin equivalent (2 doses, 2sp each).
  • Knowledge: She knows three fragments of the old legend about the Dawnweave — the "world full of light" version. She does not know the rest.
  • The offering bowl currently contains: 12cp, a child's clay animal, three dried flowers, and a pinch of gray ash wrapped in cloth. Elra did not put the ash there.
  • The supply cabinet holds ritual candles, blessed salt, chalk, dried herbs, and a leather-bound copy of prayers in a script the party cannot read.

DM Only

Sister Elra's unreadable prayer script is in proto-Dawnweave — a liturgical language used before the Converter era. She inherited the text and cannot read it herself. If the party has a character with Arcana or History proficiency and can examine it (DC 14), they can identify the script. With a 18+ check, they can partially translate it: it describes the ritual of "memory offering" — the act of giving a personal memory to the altar in exchange for something. It is a Converter activation rite, written as a prayer. Elra does not know what this means. If she finds out, she will spend three days in silence and emerge with a decision the party will not expect. She is not naive — she is steady. She has been waiting for something to confirm her worst fear, and now it has.
