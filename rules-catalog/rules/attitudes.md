# Rules: Attitudes

Source: [D&D Beyond 2024 Rules Glossary](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024/rules-glossary#GlossaryConventions)

Social attitude states used with interaction and Influence checks.

## Terms

- [Attitude](#Attitude)
- [Friendly [Attitude]](#FriendlyAttitude)
- [Hostile [Attitude]](#HostileAttitude)
- [Indifferent [Attitude]](#IndifferentAttitude)

Related action: [Influence](actions.md#InfluenceAction).

<a id="Attitude"></a>
### Attitude

A monster has a starting attitude toward a player character: Friendly, Hostile, or Indifferent. *See also* “ [Friendly](#FriendlyAttitude),” “ [Hostile](#HostileAttitude),” “ [Indifferent](#IndifferentAttitude),” and “ [Influence](actions.md#InfluenceAction).”

---

<a id="FriendlyAttitude"></a>
### Friendly [Attitude]

A Friendly creature views you favorably. You have [Advantage](d20-tests.md#Advantage) on an ability check to influence a Friendly creature. *See also* “ [Influence](actions.md#InfluenceAction).”

---

<a id="HostileAttitude"></a>
### Hostile [Attitude]

A Hostile creature views you unfavorably. You have [Disadvantage](d20-tests.md#Disadvantage) on an ability check to influence a Hostile creature. *See also* “ [Influence](actions.md#InfluenceAction).”

---

<a id="IndifferentAttitude"></a>
### Indifferent [Attitude]

An Indifferent creature has no desire to help or hinder you. Indifferent is the default attitude of a monster. *See also* “ [Influence](actions.md#InfluenceAction).”
