The Seven

Mordren Vale he/him · ~38
Travelling wizard-scholar · arrived three days after the sinkhole opened
Cipher

Snapshot

Black travel robes, road-dusted. Silver rings on three fingers. A pale wood staff he never raises in anger but never sets out of reach. He listens longer than he speaks. When he speaks, the room quiets without anyone asking it to.

Wants

  • To know whether the sinkhole structure is a Dawnweave Converter, not a temple
  • To recover any writing, relic, or "living flame" from below
  • To do this without the village deciding to seal the site

Fears

  • Being right — about what it cost the old world to make free magic
  • The villagers turning him out before he learns
  • His own debt to the seal beneath Fallowton (cast in his sleep, the runes on his rings flicker)

Voice (sample lines)

I am a scholar of ruins. I am not a hero. I will pay you for being braver than I am.

Touch nothing you do not understand. And if you find an altar — do not pray at it.

Free magic was never free. Someone paid. We are still paying.

Speech & posture

Soft register. Pauses before nouns. Uses "perhaps" and "I think" even when certain — a habit, not a hedge. Never sits with his back to a door.

How to play him

  • Polite but never warm. Smiles only at irony.
  • Pays in clean coin, counted twice.
  • If pressed about himself: deflects to the work. "We can discuss me when the village is safe."

Active checks

Insight DC 12 (his pitch) Persuasion DC 14 (cottage invite) Passive Perc 13 (ring flicker) Insight 14 (relief at "wait")

Secrets · what Mordren is hiding

  • Identity: He has royal backing. A sealed letter in his cottage carries the wax seal of the Crown's Office of Recovery — a body the public does not know exists. Reveal only at end of campaign arc 1.
  • Thesis: Dawnweave magic was not "free." Converters consumed living memory. He suspects this ruin is one. He has no proof yet — that's why he hires the party.
  • Personal cost: He owes one Weave Strain debt that has not yet come due. He never casts in front of villagers because the cost is visible (his eyes go grey for an hour).
  • Loyalty: He will not betray the party for gold, but he will betray them for evidence he cannot otherwise obtain. Make him sympathetic, not safe.

If killed in Module 1 (don't let this happen, but if):

The royal seal becomes the campaign's biggest loose thread. Within two weeks of in-fiction time, a Crown agent arrives in Fallowton looking for him. The party's job offer transfers to them — at twice the pay and twice the danger.

Elder Bran Orwick he/him · 64
Village elder · third of his name · de facto magistrate
Gate

Snapshot

Broad-shouldered, narrow-eyed, weathered to the color of cured oak. Wears the village ring of office — a thumb-ring of plain iron, polished by sixty years of worry. Stands with his weight forward, like he's expecting to refuse something.

Wants

  • Fallowton, intact, when his term ends in two springs
  • The sinkhole sealed, not opened — but he knows that may not be possible
  • For strangers to earn the right to walk under his earth

Fears

  • That his refusal to act will kill the village while his permission to act also kills it
  • That the old stories — the ones his grandfather refused to tell him — were true

Voice

Nothing good comes from beneath old earth.

You want to walk under our fields, disturb our dead earth, and call it courage? Then convince me Fallowton is not the one paying your price.

Pay the bond, or give me a reason. I do not need both.

Speech & posture

Slow. Stops mid-sentence to choose the next word. Will not be hurried. Repeats important things twice — once with weight, once with quiet.

How to play him

  • Fronts hostile, isn't. Wants to be persuaded.
  • Respects honesty, even rude honesty. Despises flattery.
  • If Sinkhole Witness PC is present, he will give them one chance to speak as a Fallowton voice. Advantage on that one roll.

Active checks

Persuasion DC 13 (waive bond) Intimidation DC 15 (force pass) History DC 14 (cite Dawnweave) Insight 12 (he is afraid)

Secrets

  • Bran's grandfather Wendel was the one who reburied the ruin sixty years ago. Bran does not know this — but if the H.V./W.V. dagger surfaces and is shown to him, he will sit down hard, and say only: "I thought that was a story."
  • He has already drafted a letter to the regional lord asking for soldiers. He has not sent it. The letter is in the Elder's Hall desk, second drawer down.
  • If the party fails him publicly, he does not retaliate — he withdraws. The village follows him. By Module 3, no door in Fallowton will open to a stranger.

Old Henric Voss he/him · 71
Farmer · owner of the north barley field · found the stairs
Peril

Snapshot

Tall once, stooped now. Hands like old maps — every line a year of frost. Smells of damp wool, wet earth, and the specific bitterness of barley smut. His left eye waters constantly; he does not bother to wipe it.

Wants

  • His field restored — or compensation enough to buy seed for next spring
  • A share of any gold the party recovers ("My field. My share.")
  • To not be the man who broke Fallowton

Fears

  • That his family already knew. (They did.)
  • That his daughter Lila will grow up over the same ground.

Voice

If there's gold down there, some of it is mine. Bran can shake his head all he wants.

My grandfather Wendel Voss — he wouldn't go near the north field after dusk. I never asked why. Now I think I should have.

You bring me back anything from down there, anything at all, you let me see it first.

Speech & posture

Rural cadence — drops articles, leans on "well" and "so." Stops mid-sentence to spit. When upset, does not raise his voice; lowers it.

How to play him

  • Greedy and grieving, at the same time. Both real.
  • Cannot read. Will ask a PC to read any writing he is shown.
  • If the broken dagger with H.V./W.V. is shown to him — he goes silent for a full minute. Then: "That. That is Wendel's. My grandfather. He told me to never dig in the north field."

Active checks

Persuasion DC 11 (share gold) History DC 12 (dagger ID via him) Insight 11 (he already knew)

Secrets · the Voss hook

  • Wendel Voss (grandfather, dec'd ~60 years ago): went into the ruin once, came out changed, told no one what he saw, told his children "never dig the north field." He may be the dagger's owner — initials W.V. He may also be one of Edras's oath-bound dead in Tunnel 1, if you want a haunting payoff later.
  • Henric himself was warned and forgot. He remembers his grandfather's silence, not the words. The forgetting may be magical (Converter residue) — make this DM's choice for the campaign tone.
  • Lila (daughter, ~9): if option C fires, she is the one who goes missing — drawn to the rim by curiosity, not malice from the ruin. She returns in Module 2 with a name carved in her palm she cannot read.
  • Maerla (wife, 64): in ending C, she is the one who forgets her brother's name. The brother is buried in the village shrine plot. Sister Elra knows this and does not yet know to be afraid.

Sister Elra she/her · 52
Keeper of the village shrine · folk-priest of the old roadside faith
Lore

Snapshot

Grey robe, salt-and-pepper braid wrapped twice around her head. Her hands are always stained at the fingertips — from grinding shrine-incense, not from any holier thing. She kneels when she prays, never bows; her people don't bow to anything.

Wants

  • Old magic to go back to sleep
  • To not be the priest who failed during the awakening
  • A clear conscience about Maerla's brother (whose name is in the shrine plot)

Fears

  • That her prayers are in a language she does not understand — and that the language is Dawnweave
  • That blessing the party will count as participation

Voice

If old magic is waking, then something holy has failed.

Keeper of the first light, do not wake. I am old and small and I will not stop you. I only ask you to stay asleep one more season.

I will bless your torch. I will not bless your sword.

Speech & posture

Quiet. Patient. Uses old turns of phrase no one teaches anymore. When she chants, the room cools by a half-degree even in summer — small enough that you might be imagining it.

How to play her

  • Will not be paid for blessings — she will accept a gift of clean salt, fresh bread, or honest service.
  • If asked to bless something, she touches it with her left hand, lips moving silently. The blessing is real (advantage on first save vs. ruin-sourced fear in Module 2).
  • Hostile to leveled spellcasting in her shrine. Will ask the caster to leave.

Active checks

Religion DC 12 (catch prayer phrase) Persuasion DC 13 (request blessing) History 14 (her chant is Dawnweave)

Secrets

  • The prayer she has chanted for thirty years is a Dawnweave invocation — passed down orally through the shrine line. She has been petitioning the Converter to remain dormant, without knowing what she was petitioning.
  • If a PC translates the chant for her — Arcana DC 15 or Comprehend Languages — she sits down, very slowly, and asks them to never tell another soul.
  • She holds the shrine ledger of village dead. Maerla's brother is in there. If the forgotten-name ending fires, she is the one who notices first.

Bessa Thorn she/her · 47
Innkeeper of the Bent Plow · third generation · rumor hub
Ally

Snapshot

Broad woman, flour-dusted arms, eyes that don't miss the second coin in the second pocket. Runs the inn briskly; remembers every face that ever ate her stew twice.

Wants

  • The inn full and the rumors flowing
  • The sinkhole resolved before her trade dries up
  • To not be afraid in her own common room

Fears

  • Mordren — not what he is, what he listens for
  • That she will be the last innkeeper of the Bent Plow if the village empties

Voice

Stew is two coppers, ale is one, room is three silver, and no, I don't take wizard credit.

He mutters in his sleep. "House of First Light," he says. Don't ask me what it means — I just clean the room.

If you mean to die down there, settle your tab first.

Speech & posture

Quick, dry, mid-register. Wipes the bar with one hand while talking with the other. Will laugh — sharp, brief — at the right joke. Looks people in the eye.

How to play her

  • Cheap to bribe with conversation, expensive to bribe with coin (she'll take both).
  • Knows where every other NPC lives, what they eat, who they owe.
  • Will warn the party once if she likes them: "If you go down, take three torches and a friend, in that order."

Active checks

Persuasion DC 12 (Mordren rumor) Bribe 2 rounds (farmer rumors) Insight 13 (she is scared)

Secrets

  • She is keeping a tab for Mordren she doesn't expect to be paid. She lets him have hot water and corner tables because she thinks he is the only person in Fallowton who might fix this. She would deny this to her death.
  • The wanted notice tacked near the door — three counties away, never removed — names a man who matches Mordren's height, build, and a younger face. Probably coincidence.
  • Clem Thorn (late husband, d. 3 winters ago): ran the Bent Plow before her; found the inn maid as an abandoned infant on the doorstep and raised her without making a ceremony of it. Bessa did not choose the arrangement but never undid it. She does not know how to say she's glad the girl stayed. She has not tried. If asked about Clem, she wipes the bar and changes the subject. He is the reason she still lights the lamp in the back room.

Garret he/him · 41
Miller · first to see the blue light · believed by no one
Witness

Snapshot

Short, heavy, perpetually flour-grey. One eye drifts slightly. He laughs nervously at things that aren't jokes. Smells of millstone and yeast and three days without sleep.

Wants

  • To be believed
  • To not be the village fool
  • For his wife Anel to stop looking at him like he's broken

Fears

  • That he saw correctly
  • That the light will come back

Voice

Three nights before the field fell, I saw light come up through the ground. Blue. Not lantern-blue. The blue of a dead thing's eye when the lamp catches it sideways.

I didn't drink that night. I haven't drunk in two years. Tell my wife.

It came up through the mill stone. Came up, and went back down, and the stone was warm in the morning. Warm. Stone shouldn't be warm.

Speech & posture

Fast, urgent, low. Repeats words for emphasis. Looks at hands while speaking, not faces. Cracks knuckles unconsciously.

How to play him

  • Believes immediately that the party will believe him — which is why he opens up so fast.
  • If a Sinkhole Witness PC is present, he speaks to that PC alone, low. Treat as "best friend who has been waiting two months for someone to listen."
  • Will draw, on the mill-shed floor in flour, the exact shape of light he saw. (It's a Dawnweave seal — Arcana DC 13.)

Active checks

Insight DC 10 (he's telling truth) Arcana DC 13 (decipher flour drawing) Perc 12 (mill stone still warm)

Secrets

  • The mill stone is still slightly warm, half a degree above ambient, and has been for two months. If a PC touches it, they pass a Wisdom save DC 12 or feel an irrational urge to stay another minute. (Nothing happens. The urge passes. But the urge is real.)
  • Garret cannot draw the Dawnweave seal anywhere except on his mill floor in flour. He has tried. He doesn't know why. (The mill is on a thin spot of the seal.)

Tamsin Bellows she/her · 36
Village smith · weapons and metalwork only · widowed three winters ago
Ally

Snapshot

Tall, scarred forearms, leather apron that has burned through and been patched twice. Sleeves rolled to the elbow regardless of weather. Soot in the creases at the corners of her eyes — permanent, not new.

Wants

  • The party armed properly — she does not trust adventurers to do it themselves
  • To not have to bury another villager
  • To finish the spearhead she's been forging for two months (it's for nothing in particular; she just needs to finish it)

Fears

  • Her late husband Joren went into the western forest five years ago and came back wrong. He died last winter. She does not say it was the forest. She knows it was the forest.

Voice

I don't sell rations. I don't sell rope. I sell things that cut.

Five silver for the sharpen. One gold for the new edge. Don't haggle — I haggle worse than you do.

That stone you brought me — it's not from here. Not within fifty miles of here. Where did you find it?

Speech & posture

Direct, declarative. Will not look up from the anvil unless the conversation is interesting. Smiles rarely; when she does, it's slow and earned.

How to play her

  • Believes the party will need her, eventually. Doesn't say so.
  • Will sharpen a blade in one hour for 5 sp (first damage roll in the ruin: +2).
  • Can identify the sinkhole stone if shown a chip — "not local, not within fifty miles." History DC — she rolls free if asked.

Active checks

History DC 0 (stone ID, she just knows) Persuasion DC 12 (price haggle) Insight 14 (her grief is fresh)

Secrets

  • The spearhead she's been forging for two months is unconsciously following a Dawnweave reinforcement pattern — Arcana DC 16 from a PC who sees her finish it. She has never been taught the pattern. She does not know she's doing it. If completed and gifted to a PC, it functions as a +1 spear that does an extra 1d4 radiant against undead and aberrations. Recommended payoff for Module 3 or 4.
  • Joren's death certificate, in Sister Elra's ledger, lists "natural causes." It was not natural causes.

Supporting cast

Maerla Voss
Henric's wife · 64 · keeps chickens

Quiet, observant, the village's best baker. If ending C fires, she is the one who forgets her brother's name. Until then she is a comfortable, almost invisible presence — exactly the kind of person whose forgetting matters most.

Henric won't tell you, but the field has been wrong for years. I knew before the rain.

Lila Voss
Henric's daughter · 9 · curious

Bright, undersized, asks questions adults won't answer. If option C (refuse to descend) fires, she sneaks to the rim that night and is found at dawn with no memory of how she got there. Returns in Module 2 with a name carved in her left palm, in a script she cannot read. The wound does not bleed.

Da says I'm not allowed near the hole. But the hole is interested in me. That's not the same thing.

The Grain-Store Keeper
unnamed · ~50 · nervous

Refuses to be named ("just call me keeper"). Has been losing grain at night for two weeks and has not told Bran because Bran will raise the bond on the next traveling merchant, which will hurt the village. Will show the party the chewed sack if Persuasion DC 11.

Don't tell the elder. He has enough to worry over. I'm handling it.

Three Farmers
chorus · always at the Bent Plow

Theo, Jord, and Old Marn. Argue every evening about the sinkhole. They will share everything they have heard for the price of a round of ale (2 cp). Theo thinks it's an omen, Jord thinks it's a mine, Old Marn says nothing and watches.

Theo: It's an omen. My mother dreamed of holes like that the year of the bad winter.

Jord: It's a mine. There's gold in old places. There's always gold.

Marn: ...
