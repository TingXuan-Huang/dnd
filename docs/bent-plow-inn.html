The Bent Plow Inn

The only inn in Fallowton · Est. before living memory

Ground Floor Plan — The Bent Plow Inn

The low door scrapes open and a wall of warm, smoky air hits you — woodsmoke, spilled ale, wet wool, and the faint bitterness of old grease. The common room is wide and dark, lit by candles on rough-hewn tables and a great stone hearth that hungers along the west wall. Several faces turn toward you. Most look away. The barkeep — a broad woman with flour-dusted arms — does not look away.

Who's Here

Bessa Thorn
Innkeeper · rumor hub · sharp-eyed · third generation
Runs the Bent Plow with brisk hands and a sharper memory. Bessa knows every rumor in Fallowton because she feeds the people who make them. She is quietly terrified about the cave, but she would rather overcharge a stranger than admit fear in her own common room.
Mordren Vale
Wizard-scholar · uneasy regular · arrived three days after the sinkhole opened
Does not sleep here; he rents the old west cottage. He comes to the Bent Plow for hot water, a corner table, and quiet questions about the hill. Bessa dislikes him because he listens like a man measuring a grave.
Three Farmers
Regulars · drinking · arguing
Argue every evening about whether the sinkhole is an omen, a mining opportunity, or someone's fault. They will share everything they know for the price of a round.

Notable Details

  • The sign out front shows a plow with a bent blade — the original bent plow, framed above the bar.
  • Bessa can point the party toward Mordren's rented cottage, but warns them not to steal from a wizard unless they enjoy becoming a story.
  • A wanted notice is tacked near the door — old, water-stained, for someone three counties away. Nobody removes it.
  • The east window of the common room faces the Old Hill. Someone has tied black thread and dry wheat over it as a village charm.
  • A chalkboard behind the bar lists prices — ale 1cp, stew 2cp, room 3sp/night.
  • The kitchen has a back door that opens to the north alley behind the inn.

DM Only

Bessa has heard Mordren say "House of First Light" once, under his breath, when someone mentioned the blue glow. She does not know what it means. If the party gains her trust (DC 12 Persuasion) or buys two rounds for the farmers, she says Mordren rents the old west cottage and sometimes returns from the north field with silver-gray dust on his cuffs.
