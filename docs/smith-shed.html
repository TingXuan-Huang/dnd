The Smith Shed

Forge · tools · horseshoes · iron work · the smell of burning coal

Floor Plan — The Smith Shed

Before you see it, you hear it — a rhythmic ring of iron on iron, steady as a heartbeat. Then the heat finds you. The forge dominates the eastern half of the shed, its coals banked high and orange. The smith's back is to you. He does not look up. On the workbench: six iron door bars, half-finished. He has been making them all week.

Who's Here

Tamsin Bellows
Machine-smith · soot-stained · practical · third generation in this shed
Tamsin is not a woman who wastes words. She communicates through what she makes: door bars, window latches, farm repairs, and small crank-driven devices that do exactly one useful thing. Since the sinkhole opened, she has been making iron door bars and one very heavy lock. She sells weapons, tools, and hardware, not comfort.
Pip (apprentice)
Boy of ~14 · curious · talks too much
Sweeps, pumps the bellows, runs errands. Has crept to the edge of the sinkhole twice to look down. Knows more about village gossip than anyone. Will trade gossip for copper pieces or interesting stories.

Notable Details

  • Iron door bars for sale: 2sp each. Currently has 4 finished, 6 in progress.
  • Tamsin can repair armor and weapons. Full repair: 10% of item value, 1 day per item.
  • A crude sketch has been pinned to the south wall — Pip drew the pyramid structure from memory after sneaking to the sinkhole. Accurate enough to be useful.
  • A large locked chest under the workbench contains the smith's savings (22gp) and an iron key on a chain that she never removes from her neck.
  • The forge chimney points north, toward the Old Hill. At night, Tamsin has noticed that the chimney smoke sometimes bends toward the hill instead of rising. She will not explain this unless she trusts the party.

DM Only

The iron key around Tamsin's neck unlocks the storage chest. Inside the chest, beneath the coins, is a fragment of pale gray stone — the same material as the buried structure. Tamsin found it in the collapsed soil when she helped dig out old Henric Voss's fence posts. She has not told anyone. It has not rusted; iron set beside it rusts in hours. Tamsin suspects it is connected to something dangerous and has been trying to decide whether to throw it in the river. Pip's drawing, if the party examines it closely, shows two details Pip does not understand: kneeling figures whose shadows flow upward, and a central shape that looks like a door.
