# Campaign Rule: Weave Strain (The Price of Borrowed Magic)

*Fallowton campaign · DM + players · Matches [01-story.md](../dm-handbook/01-story.md)*

In **Fallowton**, everyone knows magic is real. Kings hire it, colleges study it, and a few gifted or desperate people still use it. But no one pretends it is free.

The old saying in your village is closer to the truth than most wizards admit:

> **Anything borrowed from magic must be repaid.**

**Weave Strain** is how this campaign tracks that repayment when you cast serious magic.

---

## How this fits the world’s history (what you might learn in play)

### What villagers believe

In Fallowton, no one debates whether magic is real. They debate whether it was worth it.

The old people speak in threes when they talk about magic: it hurts the one who uses it; it hurts the ones nearby; and it leaves a debt that doesn’t always announce itself before it collects. They’ve seen exhaustion, sickness, madness, shortened lives. Strange things happening to animals and fields after a wizard passes through. Children who stop sleeping well and can’t say why.

So they don’t say “magic” plainly in the market square. They say **black craft** when they’re afraid. **Old work** when they’re angry. **Wrong tongue** when they want someone to stop.

That is not ignorance. That is the memory of paying someone else’s debt.

The oldest stories — told by fires in winter, when the old people still feel generous — go further. They speak of **the Dawnweave**: a time before memory, when magic moved through the world like weather. Rivers that healed wounds. Trees that bent toward the lost. Starlight that could fall into a child’s open hand and stay there.

The old people always pause before they finish that story.

Because they know: any legend that sounds that clean has only told you half.

They are **partly right about all of it**. The full truth is worse and older than they know.

### What happened (DM truth — reveal slowly)

The Dawnweave was real. Magic did once move through the world freely, like rain and light. The old people remember a truth.

What they do not remember is the other side of the ledger.

The Dawnweave did **not** make magic free. It made the cost **invisible** — and it collected from people who had no say in the matter.

At the height of their civilization, Dawnweave architects built **Converters**: pyramid machines wrapped in the language of temples and holy houses, staffed by people who prayed to something they did not fully understand. The **House of First Light**, buried under Fallowton’s north field, was one of them.

From the outside, Converters looked like healing. Grain grew better near them. Fevers broke. Wounds closed faster.

From the inside — from where the debt was actually taken — they ran on **living memory**. Not simply life force. Memory specifically. Names. The sound of a dead parent’s voice. The shape of a child’s face twenty years later. The grief someone carried for a loss no one else remembered anymore. Victims might die. But often they simply became **unremembered**: erased so completely that their own families could not hold their faces in mind. Statues went blank. Records lost entries. A man might vanish from a village and no one could say whether he had ever been there at all.

The pain of a few, made invisible, became effortless wonder for everyone else.

The old saying is exactly right. The old people just don’t know what the repayment was originally in.

When Dawnweave rulers eventually tried to reach the **world magic core** without the Converters — without the machinery, without the priests, without even the memory of those who had paid — they opened a door they could not close.

Something from outside the world noticed the wound. Working titles, until named in play: **the Nameless Hunger**, **the Hollow Star**. It fed on magic, memory, and the meaning of things.

The gods **sealed the core** to save what remained. The seal worked. But it changed magic forever. Every real spell is now a withdrawal through a **broken, guarded channel**. The price falls on the **caster**, the **land nearby**, a **relic**, or **debts** the old world never paid.

**Weave Strain** is the personal, immediate part of that price.

### Why the sinkhole matters

Weeks of rain, and a failing **earth-seal**, exposed the Converter. The House of First Light has been dreaming under the north field for centuries, held in place by wards built by the same people who buried it.

Those wards are cracking.

Casting magic near the ruin is like striking a bell that was already ringing: the resonance travels inward. The old machine hears. Strain costs bite harder. And unlike the people who built it, the ruin has no opinion about who pays.

## What does “strain” mean at the table?

### In plain English

**Strain** = **stress on a damaged system** — the magical equivalent of drawing water through a cracked pipe. Each **level 1+ spell** adds pressure until you pay a visible cost.

**Weave Strain** is a number on your sheet (**0–3** at low levels; **0–4** after Purification III). It is **not** spell slots, hit points, or exhaustion (unless the DM adds exhaustion as story).

### What Weave Strain is **not**

| Not this | Why |
|----------|-----|
| **Spell slots** | Slots = how many spells you *may* cast. Strain = what casting *costs* you afterward. |
| **Exhaustion (default)** | Strain has its own track and recovery unless the DM ties strain 3 to exhaustion narratively. |
| **Concentration** | One ongoing spell. Strain builds across the session. |
| **Official 2024 rules** | Homebrew for this campaign only. |

---

## What players need to know

1. Magic is **legal and real** in the world; in Fallowton it is **mistrusted** because the **price** is well known.  
2. Each **level 1+ spell** (using a slot) usually adds **+1 Weave Strain** and one **strain cost** from the table.  
3. **Cantrips** are weak **echoes** of the old ambient magic — usually **no strain**.  
4. **Magic Initiate:** cantrip usually no strain; the **once-per-long-rest level-1 spell** usually **does** cause strain.  
5. **Purification** (story milestones) lets you cast more safely — without rebuilding the Converter’s evil.

Track **current strain**: 0, 1, 2, or 3 (max 4 only if DM awards Purification III).

---

## Track it on your character sheet

```text
WEAVE STRAIN (current): 0 / 3
Purification: none | I | II | III | IV  (DM marks)

When I cast a leveled spell:
  1. Cast the spell (use slot as normal)
  2. Strain +1 (max 3, or 4 if Purification III)
  3. Pick one cost from the row for my NEW strain total
  4. Say what villagers would notice (if any)

Luck debt (optional): 0
Class/background hook? (see “Who bears worse luck”)

At strain 3: NO more leveled spells until rest.
Cast at strain 3 anyway? Con save DC 15 (17 in ruin) — fail = 3d10 & 0 HP; fail by 10+ = death.

Short rest: strain −1   |   Long rest: strain 0
```

## Example at the table

**Elara** (Cleric) casts **Cure Wounds** on a ally in the exposed Converter hall.

1. She spends a **1st-level slot** — normal rules.  
2. Strain **0 → 1**. She chooses **1d4 necrotic damage** or a sign villagers would call “black craft.”  
3. She casts **Bless** before a fight. Strain **1 → 2**. She chooses **Weakened** or **disadvantage on next Wis save**.  
4. The DM may rule the **ruin is listening** (Converter proximity): **Ruin Awakening Clock +1** instead of another option.  
5. **Short rest** in the hall: strain **2 → 1**. **Long rest** in Fallowton: strain **0**.

If she cast at strain **2+** inside an awakened chamber, the DM may add **Overreach Backlash** — magic spilling to allies like the old machines did.

## When strain applies

| Triggers strain | Usually no strain |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Casting with a **level 1+ spell slot** | Weapon attacks, skills, tools |
| **Ritual** that consumes a slot | Most **cantrips** |
| **Magic item** that casts a leveled spell (DM) | Lay on Hands, Rage, Sneak Attack (no spell) |
| **Activating** a Dawnweave seal, Converter panel, or relic (DM) | Reading ruins, normal Mechanist machines |
| **Inside the House of First Light** (DM may require Overreach at strain 2+) | |

| Character type | Strain |
|----------------|--------|
| Full casters (Bard, Cleric, Druid, Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard) | Leveled spells → strain |
| Paladin / Ranger | When spellcasting begins |

## Strain cost (after each leveled spell)

**Step 1:** Cast the spell. **Step 2:** Strain +1 (respect max). **Step 3:** Pick **one** cost from the row for your **new** strain total.

| Strain | Cost (choose one unless DM assigns) |
|--------|-------------------------------------|
| **1** | **1d4** necrotic damage (the channel bites back), **or** obvious **black-craft sign** until end of next turn (grey eyes, cold breath, hair lifts, nosebleed), **or** **Weakened** until end of your next turn, **or** disadvantage on next **Charisma** check in Fallowton (“they collect debts”), **or** forget a **trivial detail** until next rest, **or** **ill luck:** disadvantage on your next **attack roll, ability check, or saving throw** (any one d20 — villagers say magic steals fortune) |
| **2** | **1d6** necrotic damage, **or** **Weakened** 1 minute, **or** disadvantage on next **Wisdom** save, **or** **Ruin Awakening Clock +1** if underground in the Converter, **or** brief **memory flicker** (DM), **or** **deep ill luck:** disadvantage on your **next two** d20 rolls (any mix of attacks, checks, saves), **or** until your next rest, **halve** gold found and nonmagical healing you receive (rounded down) — “the debt takes more than blood” |
| **3** | **1d8** necrotic damage **and** you cannot cast **leveled spells** until a **short or long rest** (cantrips still work). You are **burned out** — see **casting past the limit** below. |

**Weakened:** Speed −10 ft; next Str, Dex, or Con check has disadvantage; then effect ends (or when duration ends).

**Constitution save (recommended):** When you would take strain **damage**, DC **10 + strain**; success = half, failure = full.

### Optional: Luck debt on your sheet

Some tables track **Luck debt** when players pick **ill luck** options (DM marks +1 each time).

| Luck debt | Effect (DM) |
|-----------|-------------|
| **1** | Next time you roll a natural **1** on any d20, the DM may add a small complication (trip, broken strap, spoiled ration) |
| **2** | Disadvantage on **one** random d20 this session (DM picks moment after you roll high) |
| **3+** | Villagers avoid you; one **near miss** per session (DM describes — arrow clips ear, stone falls where you stood) |

Luck debt clears on **long rest** or **Sister Elra’s blessing**. Purification II+ may ignore luck debt 1/session (DM).

Track on sheet: `Luck debt: 0`

## Who bears worse luck (and worse death risk)

Not every caster pays the same **kind** of debt. Fallowton gossip says **innate** magic steals luck from the bloodline, **pacts** steal it from the future, and **apprentices** were warned and cast anyway.

These are **optional** modifiers. Use them to make classes and backgrounds feel distinct without extra punishment for every player.

### Classes

| Class | Ill luck (when you pick an ill-luck strain cost, or DM assigns) | Burnout / death at strain 3 |
|-------|----------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Sorcerer** | **+1 Luck debt** automatically (magic in the blood). First natural **1** each day may be strain-themed (nosebleed, chill, animal flinches). | Constitution save **DC +1** (16 in ruin) |
| **Warlock** | **+1 Luck debt**; villagers whisper your patron will **collect twice**. Next **Charisma** check with anyone who saw you cast has **disadvantage** even if you did not pick the Cha strain option. | On burnout **failure by 5+**, DM may describe the patron taking a memory, dream, or name — not always extra damage |
| **Wizard** | Prefer **memory** strain options (forget detail, flicker) when possible — fits study and Dawnweave lore. | Standard DC |
| **Cleric** | Ill luck often hits **Wisdom** saves first (faith tested). If you cast **healing** at strain **2+**, Sister Elra or faithful NPCs may be **cold** until your next rest (roleplay, not mechanical ban). | Burnout DC **−1** (15 → 14) if the leveled spell **only restores HP** to another creature |
| **Bard** | Ill luck on **Performance / Persuasion**; rumor spreads in Fallowton after one visible cast. | Standard DC |
| **Druid** | Ill luck on **Survival / Animal Handling**; beasts shy away for 1 hour. | Standard DC |
| **Bard / Sorcerer / Warlock** (Charisma casters) | Use class row above; social luck debt stacks with village fear. | — |

**Paladin / Ranger:** Use class rules when spellcasting begins; until then, no strain.

### Custom Fallowton backgrounds

| Background | Ill luck | Burnout / death |
|------------|----------|-----------------|
| **Failed Apprentice** | You were taught the price before you paid it. On strain **1–2**, if you cast a leveled spell, the DM may **require** your strain cost to be **ill luck**, **memory**, or **black-craft sign** (not raw damage) unless you have **Purification I**. | Burnout save **DC +2** for you — you knew better |
| **Magic-Touched** (any origin) | Start play with **Luck debt 1** if you have any spellcasting (cantrip or feat). | Standard |
| **Sinkhole Witness** | After casting, **disadvantage** on the next **Wisdom (Survival)** or **Perception** check near the north field or underground. | Burnout DC **+1** underground; villagers say the hole **remembers** you |
| **Ruin Scholar** | **Memory** strain options count as **+1 Luck debt** when tracking. Reading seals without casting: no luck debt. | Activating a seal at strain **2+** triggers **Overreach** on a failed **Intelligence (Arcana)** DC 14 check |
| **Mechanist** | **No** luck debt from mundane machines. If a cast powers **Dawnweave** machinery, treat as Sorcerer for luck (+1 debt) that day. | Standard unless powering Converter — then Overreach table favored |
| **Village Doctor** | You do **not** gain Luck debt from **observing** strain. If **you** cast leveled magic, use normal rules. | **Medicine DC 14** during short rest: one ally who failed a burnout save this session rerolls it **once** |
| **Fieldhand / Guard / Poacher** | No strain until you gain magic; then villagers treat you as “touched” — **+1 Luck debt** the first time you cast in Fallowton. | Standard |

### Species and feats

| Source | Ill luck | Burnout / death |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Tiefling** (and other innate magic traits) | First strain cost each day **shows black-craft sign** unless you pass **Con save DC 12** | Standard |
| **Magic Initiate** | Cantrip: no debt. The **1/day leveled spell**: **+1 Luck debt** the first time you use it each long rest | Standard |
| **Dragonborn breath** etc. | Not spell slots — **no strain** unless DM rules otherwise | — |

### DM quick rule

- **One** extra luck/death hook per character is enough. Do not stack every row.  
- Pick the **class** row **or** the **background** row, whichever fits the story better.  
- **Death** should still be telegraphed; Failed Apprentice and Sorcerer at strain 3 are **more likely to die** only if the player chooses to cast anyway.

### Strain 4 (Purification III only)

Max strain becomes **4**. Reaching 4: **1d10** necrotic damage, no leveled spells until **long rest**, DM may trigger **Overreach** once. Represents tearing too much power through the sealed channel.

---

## Casting past the limit (burnout and death)

Strain **3** means the channel is **full**. The body and soul are not meant to pull more through the seal without rest. Villagers know stories of wizards who **cast once too often** and were found cold in their beds, or mad, or empty-eyed like the Converter’s victims.

### If you are at strain 3 and cast a leveled spell anyway

You may still try in desperation (DM should warn: *“This may kill you.”*):

1. Cast the spell using a slot as normal.  
2. **Do not** increase strain above 3 (unless Purification III allows 4).  
3. Immediately make a **Constitution saving throw**, DC **15** (underground in the Converter: **17**).  
   - **Success:** Take **2d10** necrotic damage; remain conscious.  
   - **Failure:** Take **3d10** necrotic damage and fall **unconscious** (0 hit points).  
   - **Failure by 5 or more:** As failure, and you also suffer **one failed death save**.  
   - **Failure by 10 or more:** You die — heart stopped, soul scraped hollow, or memory erased. No death saves unless the DM rules a miracle intervention.

4. You **cannot** cast leveled spells again until you finish a **long rest**, even if you are healed above 0 HP.

### If you reach strain 3 twice in one day without a long rest

After the **first** burnout, each additional leveled spell that would bring you to strain 3 again uses the same save, but the DC increases by **+2** each time (15, then 17, then 19…).

### If you reach strain 4 (Purification III campaigns)

Reaching strain 4 from a normal cast: apply strain 4 cost, then **Constitution save DC 16** or **drop to 0 hit points**. **Failure by 10+:** death as above. This is the hard ceiling of the broken Weave.

### Healing and revival

- **Spare the Dying**, **Healer’s kit**, and **medicine** work normally on strain damage and burnout.  
- **Raise Dead** and stronger magic are rare at level 1; if used later, the DM may rule a revived character begins with **strain 2** and **luck debt 2** until cleansed — magic left a mark.

### DM tone

Death from overcasting should be **possible but telegraphed**. Always warn before a strain-3 cast. The goal is fear and restraint, not punishing one honest mistake.

## Roleplay: what strain feels like

| Strain | What it feels like | What Fallowton might say |
|--------|--------------------|---------------------------|
| **1** | A sudden cold behind the sternum, like swallowing winter air. The sense of being watched by something that doesn’t blink. | *”The wizard’s work. You can see it on them.”* |
| **2** | Ears ring with a sound that’s almost words. Small things — names, faces, what you ate this morning — slip like wet rope. Animals step away from you first. | *”Old work. Wrong tongue. Don’t stand near them.”* |
| **3** | The chest goes hollow. The world feels thin, like paper held over a hole. Fortune turns its face away. | *”The debt came due. We all saw it coming.”* |
| **Burnout** | Something seizes in the chest like a closing fist. Vision whites at the edges, then all the way through. | *”They cast their life out. The debt took the rest.”* |

**Village Doctor:** Wisdom (Medicine) **DC 10** to recognize strain; **DC 12** + short rest + kit to give ally **advantage on next strain Con save** or **−1 strain once per long rest** (DM).

## Overreach backlash (Converter logic)

Use when magic is **more than the broken channel** should carry — especially inside the **House of First Light**.

Triggers:

- Cast at **strain 2+**  
- Use a **Converter** control, Dawnweave relic, or one-shot power  
- **Upcast** or break normal limits  
- Cast in an **awakened** ruin chamber after DM warning  

Apply normal strain cost, then roll **d4** or choose:

| d4 | Backlash | Lore tie-in |
|----|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Caster **Weakened** 1 minute | Body pays what the machine once took from others |
| 2 | **Companion echo:** allies within 10 ft, DC 10 Con save or 1 necrotic damage per spell level / Weakened until end of next turn | Magic spills to nearby life like Converter runoff |
| 3 | **Ruin attention:** clock +1 underground; fear and animals panic above | The wounded engine notices |
| 4 | **Memory debt:** forget a harmless detail until rest, or DM gives a Dawnweave vision | Living memory stolen or returned as ghost-images |

Use **companion echo** rarely — once per session at most is enough.

| Event | Strain |
|-------|--------|
| **Short rest** | −1 (minimum 0) |
| **Long rest** | 0 |
| **Sister Elra’s blessing** (roleplay, 1/week) | −1 |
| **Cleansed spring** (story) | −2 or clear |
| **Village Doctor** (see above) | Advantage on next Con save vs strain damage, or −1 once/long rest |

Recovery fixes **strain**, not **spell slots**.

## Purification (growing stronger without evil)

**Purification** is not “more Converter power.” It is learning to draw magic through the **seal** more safely — closer to how the House of First Light was *meant* to heal the world, without stealing memory from victims.

The DM awards steps. Do not take them yourself.

| Step | Typical timing | Benefit | Story meaning |
|------|----------------|---------|---------------|
| **I** | Level 2 or first major relic | Strain damage dice −1 step | You learn a gentler grip on the channel |
| **II** | Level 4 or Blue Light / map | First leveled spell each day = **no strain** | One clean draw per day |
| **III** | Level 5 or Black Spring hook | Max strain **4**; once/long rest: +1 spell DC **or** one upcast without strain | You can push without breaking — briefly |
| **IV** | Tier 2 | Strain 1–2 may skip damage option; Fallowton may trust you | You are known as debt-payers, not thieves |

Purification should **never** require sacrificing someone else’s memory. That is the old Converter sin.

## Magic law and the crown

Fallowton's fear of magic is not unique. It is official policy.

The crown restricts magic to approved practitioners — in practice, this means nobility and the scholars they sponsor. The justification is Weave Strain itself: magic costs the caster, spills onto bystanders, and untrained use has caused genuine harm. These costs are real. The crown did not invent the danger.

What the crown did invent is the solution: a branch of enforcement that travels the provinces, identifies unauthorized magic use, and arrests the practitioners. The Office does not advertise itself. Villagers know its agents by the four-person squads and the sealed warrants. The official name for what they do is "public safety." The practical result is that the only people with regular access to magic are the ones who were already powerful.

This pattern should feel familiar. The Converter ran on the same logic: magic for the few, cost borne by the many, the machine justified by the benefit it produced. The crown does not know it is repeating the Dawnweave's mistake. The parallel is not a conspiracy. It is what power does when left without memory.

**For players:** If your character knows the capital, they know the law. Magic is not illegal everywhere — it is legal for the right people. Whether that distinction is just is a question your character may have opinions about.

---

## Social magic in Fallowton

Villagers **do not deny** magic. They **mistrust the bill**.

- Say **black craft**, **old work**, **wrong tongue** — not “arcane studies.”  
- **Mordren** is tolerated as an outsider; locals whisper *“Wizards always collect debts.”*  
- Obvious casting without trust → cold stares, refused service, children hidden.  
- Strain **1** can include disadvantage on the next Charisma check in town — that is the social cost matching the mechanical one.

## DM quick adjudication

- **Healing still strains** — or Clerics spam with no tension.  
- **Mordren** should show a cost (or convincingly hide it).  
- **First leveled spell** for a nervous apprentice: optional **no strain** once for drama.  
- **Converter / House of First Light:** favor **clock +1** and **memory** options on strain 2+.  
- **Lantern of First Light:** ignore strain 1/day when attuned (DM).  
- **Do not** let Purification recreate Converter sacrifice mechanics.

## One-page summary

1. **Magic is real; the price is real.** Fallowton calls it **black craft**.  
2. **Leveled spell** → **strain +1** → pick one cost (damage, luck, memory, ruin, etc.).  
3. **Cantrips** → usually free (echoes of the old world).  
4. **Short rest** −1 strain · **Long rest** 0.  
5. **Converter ruin** → worse costs, Overreach, clock.  
6. **Strain 3** → stop leveled casts until rest; **cast anyway** → Con save or **0 HP / death**.  
7. **Purification** → safer casting **without** stealing memory like the old machines.
