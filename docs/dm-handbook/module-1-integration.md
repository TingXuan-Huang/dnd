# Module 1 — Homebrew Systems Integration Pass

The new homebrew systems (Wounds, Weave Strain, The Notice, Mind-Body bleed, Tolerance, etc.) were designed *after* Module 1. This file maps each new system onto the existing M1 play script. **Read this during 30-min-before prep alongside [`module-1-dm-flow.md`](module-1-dm-flow.md).** It tells you exactly where each system fires so they don't sit dormant.

> **The principle:** systems that never fire become wallpaper. Each one below should fire at least once in M1 so players learn what they mean.

---

## Quick reference — which systems fire in M1?

| System | Module 1 status | First likely fire |
|---|---|---|
| **HP & death saves** | RAW; firing every scene | Act II (descent slip) |
| **Wounds** | Active; low probability in M1 | Act II (wet stone fall — possible Twisted Ankle) |
| **Weave Strain** | Active if anyone casts | First leveled spell |
| **The Notice** (formerly Corruption + Awakening Clock — see [`../../homebrew/the-notice.md`](../../homebrew/the-notice.md)) | **Central tracker for M1.** Replaces the old Awakening Clock and the personal Corruption tracker — one unified 0-10 scale | First Mordren-cottage seal-touch or Blue-mote contact |
| **Death Visions** | Active if any death save rolled | Act II falls; Act III combat (unlikely) |
| **Trauma** | Possible, low probability in M1 | If a PC sees the Hollow Star presence directly |
| **Mind-Body bleed** | Bake into Notice ticks | Notice 3+ in Act III |
| **Load tier** | Active; check at chargen | Pre-game |
| **Hunger/thirst** | Skip in M1 (one-day adventure) | n/a |
| **Sleep deprivation** | Skip in M1 | n/a |
| **Disease/infection** | Skip in M1 (Wounds don't go 3 days before M2) | n/a |
| **Vision/light (Dawnweave script DC bump)** | **Active** | Reading descent inscriptions |
| **Tolerance (potion / magic buff)** | Track but unlikely to trigger | If party blesses each other 5+ times |
| **Joy / Despair** | Possible | NPC moments at the inn |
| **Healer burnout** | Sister Elra's burden = 5 at campaign start — narrate it | She visits / heals |

## Act I — Free village explore (Sunrise → Sunset Bell)

### Bent Plow Inn opening (FIXED)

**Mordren's ring flicker (Passive Perception 13 silent check):**
- The existing M1 rule stands.
- **New:** Mordren wears a Voss-line ring that is faintly Hollow Star-touched. If a PC sees the flicker AND has Voss-Blooded feat or Voss-Family Lore → that PC takes **+1 Notice** (recognition of kin-touched magic).

**Mordren's offer of work:**
- He pays in advance (10 gp / PC). His silver coins are old.
- **New:** if a PC has Lore (Crown Law), Insight DC 12 reveals the coins are pre-Crowning mintage — Mordren is dealing in coin the crown doesn't track.

### Sister Elra at the shrine (FREE)

**Existing:** chanting; if a PC asks for a blessing, she gives it on a torch, weapon, or flask.

**New mechanics:**
- Her chant is in Old Common. Religion DC 12 + Old Common knowledge → auto-translation of the phrase.
- **Sister Elra's burden = 5** at campaign start. If a PC studies her (Insight DC 12): they sense she is **tired**. If they offer help — a chore done, a meal brought, a silence given — her burden drops by 1 and she does the blessing as a **+1 Notice reduction** that fires immediately for the recipient.
- Her blessing in M1 specifically applies:
  - **Torch:** advantage on the first save vs fear or charm in the ruin (M1 existing).
  - **Weapon:** the bearer cannot cause a Hollowed Wound to anyone in the next 24 hours (Concord seal).
  - **Flask:** drinking the contents = 1 day immune to a fresh Hollowed effect.

### Mordren's cottage (FREE or break-in)

**Existing:** royal seal investigation; sealed letter; opportunity to break in.

**New mechanics:**
- **Breaking the seal openly = +1 Notice** to the PC who breaks it (you've touched something the world's ledger noticed).
- **Reading the letter without breaking (Investigation DC 13)**: see only the salutation. The salutation phrase ("To the bearer of the Recovery") is itself in Old Common — Religion DC 12 reveals it as Concord-canonical with a Dawnweave-era loanword (*recovery* originally meant *re-takeing of memory*).
- Inside the cottage, a Sealcrafter PC (feat) can sense the protective seal Mordren has carved on his hearthstone. It's blocking 1 spell level — recently used.

### Smithshed — Tamsin Bellows (FREE)

**Existing:** Tamsin IDs the sinkhole stone; haggles on sharpening.

**New mechanics:**
- Tamsin will NOT sharpen Dawnweave-touched weapons. If a PC has Voss-Touched feat OR carries a Voss-line item, she returns the weapon politely and **will not work with that PC again** until trust is rebuilt (1 visit + a gift from a Fallowton villager).
- A PC with Crebb or Tann hereditary lines (per `survival-realism.md` §A7.2) — Tamsin recognizes the family marks without saying so (one-line tic: she pauses before serving them).

### Grain Store — the tooth marks (FREE)

**Existing:** Investigation/Persuasion for goblin clue.

**New mechanics:**
- The keeper, **Old Marn**, has a Voss-line wife (Henric's cousin) — a backstory detail for any PC who connects the dots later (Lore (Voss-Family) reveal in a later module).
- If a PC has Lore (Hedge), they may identify the trampled grain as having been gnawed by *non-goblin* teeth at one point — a Wronged-tier lingering dead spilled grain here years ago (a hint at H5 ghosts for later).

### Mill — Garret (FREE)

**Existing:** Insight DC 10 confirms Garret's testimony; Arcana DC 13 on the flour drawing.

**New mechanics:**
- The mill stone is "warmer than the air" (existing Silent Perception 12).
- **New:** a PC who *touches* the mill stone with bare skin takes **+1 Notice** silently (the stone resonates with Hollow Star attention). The PC also sees, very briefly, a child's face they don't recognize — they may roleplay it as a Vision flavor moment (zero mechanical effect at level 1, but narratively foreshadows Memory mechanics in later modules).
- Garret's blue-light testimony establishes him as a witness; he is a candidate for becoming an Unfulfilled-tier ghost if he dies in M2-3.

### Elder's Hall — Bran Elderfield (FREE)

**Existing:** Persuasion / Intimidation / History gates for the village bond waiver.

**New mechanics:**
- **The Intimidation gate (per `homebrew/character-design/progression.md`):** disadvantage on the first Intimidation roll against Bran unless the PC presents *concrete proof* of having seen something. Showing him the W.V. dagger (a Voss-line item) counts. A Wound counts. A Vision spoken aloud counts.
- Bran can grant the bond waiver as a Joy moment for the PC who succeeded: a small wooden token (his deceased son's). Carrying this for the rest of M1 grants **+1 to one saving throw of choice** (one-time, narrative).

### Sunset Bell — the discipline pivot (FIXED)

**Existing:** Persuasion DC 13 to convince Bran the descent will protect Fallowton.

**New mechanics:**
- The bell ringing is the moment the village's Notice clicks awake. **All PCs gain +1 Notice automatically** (the descent itself is the moment the world sees you).

## Act II — Descent (FIXED transitions, FREE pacing)

### The stairs

**Existing:** Athletics DC 10 on wet stone; 1d4 bludgeoning on fail; drop one held item one step.

**New mechanics:**
- **A fail by 5+** = the PC takes a **Twisted Ankle Wound** (from `survival.md` §B). This is the first Wound likely to appear in the campaign. Use it to teach players what a Wound is.
- Reading the inscriptions on the descent walls in dim torch light: **DC +3** (per `survival-realism.md` §A4). Arcana / Religion / History to read.

### The Ruin's Eye (silent Wisdom 12)

**Existing:** narrate to highest-Wis PC: the stones feel like they turn toward you.

**New mechanics:**
- This is an active manifestation of Notice. **+1 Notice to the PC who passes the save** (their character became momentarily *known*). On fail, no Notice change.

### Central Hall reveal

**Existing:** three tunnel mouths shown; PCs choose order to peek.

**New mechanics:**
- The carvings on the columns are Architect Script. A PC with Lore (Dawnweave) recognizes them without rolling. The script forms the phrase *"We did not mean for this to happen"* — a player narrative gift.
- If a PC has Voss-Touched feat, they may speak ONE of the carved words aloud → +1 Strain to them + +1 Notice + a brief visual reaction (a candle flame elsewhere goes blue for a heartbeat).

## Act III — Three tunnel mouth peeks (FREE)

### Blue mouth

**Existing:** broken dagger investigation; mote feelings; Wisdom 11 silent check.

**New mechanics:**
- **Touching a blue mote bare-skinned = +1 Notice** to that PC (existing M1 ruling stands).
- **Picking up the W.V. broken dagger:** if a PC has Voss-Blooded feat or Voss-Family Lore, they receive a brief vision the moment they touch it (the dagger's bound soul fragment — per `sanity-and-spirit.md` H3). +1 Notice if accepted; player narrates the vision.
- The kneeling armored skeleton (concession-room): its blue ribs are dark. A PC with Lore (Dawnweave) recognizes this as a Marked-tier corpse — a Voss who died here.

### Sound mouth

**Existing:** track perception; the imitation laugh (silent Insight 13).

**New mechanics:**
- **Shouting in the mouth = +1 Notice** to whoever shouts (existing M1).
- The laughing creature: a Marked-tier echo of a Wronged child — the child died here decades ago, and something is using their shape. If a PC has Lore (Hedge), they sense the *staleness* of the laugh.
- In the concession-room, the 3 goblin minions playing dice: one of them wears a small wooden horse (Sister Elra recognizes it). If recovered: present it to Sister Elra and her burden drops by 1.

### Wind mouth

**Existing:** wind-knows-your-name (silent Wisdom 12); the trap floor.

**New mechanics:**
- **Speaking own name aloud in the Wind mouth = +2 Notice** (existing M1).
- A PC with Marked-by-the-Bell feat: feels the Awakening Clock effect with no save needed; +1 Notice to that PC silently.
- The trap floor: Dex DC 12; on fail, 1d6 piercing. **A fail by 5+** = take a Punctured Lung Wound (per Wound table).

### Pool Shudder (FIXED — fires when Notice ≥ 5 OR when Act III pace demands it)

**Existing:** the pool fills an inch with black water; the exit-cue fires.

**New mechanics:**
- **All PCs gain +1 Notice automatically.** This is the cosmic "the Hollow Star has noticed your visit."
- A PC with Sealcrafter feat: may attempt to **stabilize the pool** with a 1-action seal carve. Arcana DC 14. On success: the Pool Shudder freezes for 10 minutes (party gets an extra exploration window before forced exit). On fail: +1 Notice to the carver.
- The group Constitution save DC 10 (existing): on fail, the PC takes 1 level of exhaustion (from sleep-deprivation-and-stress combined; per `survival-realism.md` §A2 + `sanity-and-spirit.md` §H6).

## Act IV — Surface + cliffhanger (FIXED)

**Existing:** one of three cliffhangers (Mordren disappears / silver leaves / forgotten name).

**New mechanics:**
- **Cliffhanger A — Mordren disappears**: triggers a personal Trauma roll for whichever PC was closest to Mordren (Trigger-NPC; resolves when they find him next module). If no PC was particularly close, skip.
- **Cliffhanger B — Silver leaves in the well**: each PC who took an item from the ruin takes **+1 Notice** automatically (it followed them home).
- **Cliffhanger C — Forgotten name**: the highest-Notice PC loses a small memory the way nat-1 death save losses work (per `survival.md` §C). The DM picks; 24-hour effect; returns at dawn.

**Long-rest in Fallowton after the descent:**
- Standard long rest, no half-rest issues (they're not in armor sleeping in danger).
- **The Awakening Clock from the existing M1** = the world Notice tier; track the highest mark the party reached. Carry it forward to M2.
- Each PC's personal Notice ticks should be recorded for ongoing tracking.

## DM principles for running M1 with these systems

1. **Don't over-load the table with mechanics on session 1.** Fire Wounds, Notice, and Strain — those are the three players need to learn. Let Trauma and Tolerance stay invisible unless a player triggers one organically.
2. **Narrate Notice ticks silently when they happen behind-the-scenes** (the Mordren ring flicker), and audibly when they happen via player action (touching a mote). The mix is the texture.
3. **Sister Elra's burden is real.** If players engage with her humanely, reduce it. If they treat her as a vending machine, increase the cost of her services.
4. **The W.V. dagger is the campaign's seed item.** If a PC carries it forward into M2, the campaign has its narrative engine.
5. **Wounds in M1 should be soft.** A Twisted Ankle is enough. Do not introduce a Bleeding Gash unless a player explicitly does something stupid. M1 is teaching the system, not punishing.
