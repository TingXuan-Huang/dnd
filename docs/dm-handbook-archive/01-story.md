# Module 1 — Story

Simple narrative the DM can read, paraphrase, or split across scenes. **Secrets** are labeled; do not reveal unless play earns it. Table-facing text is written in Chinese read-aloud style; DM-only lore can stay in English for prep clarity.
```text
在这片古老土地的边缘，有一个不起眼的小村庄，名叫 Fallowton。

它坐落在低矮的丘陵之间。北边是一座沉默的小山，东边是辽阔的平原与一条窄河，西边和南边则是阴森的树林。林子很深，村里的老人说，风吹过那些树时，像有人在用忘记的语言低声说话。

Fallowton 只有二三十户人家。木墙、旧篱笆、泥泞田埂、歪斜的谷仓，还有一条向东通往外界的道路。它不在重要的地图上，也没有值得吟游诗人传唱的英雄故事。大多数旅人只是经过这里，喝一碗热汤，听一夜雨声，然后继续上路。

这里的人种 barley、turnips、rye 和 gray wheat。日子缓慢，收成普通，炉火低矮而温暖。村民们畏惧森林，畏惧旧山，也畏惧那些比人类记忆更古老的东西。

这个世界里，魔法确实存在。

它被学院研究，被王侯雇佣，也被少数天赋者和野心家使用。但魔法从来不是免费的。越强大的魔法，越会向施法者索取代价：疲惫、疾病、疯癫、衰老、死亡，甚至波及周围无辜的生命。

所以在 Fallowton，人们并不否认魔法。他们只是尽量不说那个词。

比起 magic，他们更常说 black craft、old work，或者 wrong tongue。那些词里没有学术，也没有敬畏，只有一种农民世代相传的警惕：凡是向魔法借来的东西，迟早都要偿还。

不过，在炉火快要熄灭、雨夜漫长得像没有尽头时，老人们偶尔会提到一个更古老的传说。

他们说，在很久很久以前，世界曾经不是现在这样。

那是 The Dawnweave 的时代。传说那时魔法不像今日这般稀少、危险、令人不安。魔法曾像雨水、阳光与风一样自然地流淌在世界中。河水可以治愈伤口，树木会为迷路的人弯下枝条，星光甚至能落入人间，停在孩子摊开的掌心里。

老人们总在这里停下。

因为他们也知道，凡是太美的传说，往往只说了一半。

Scene1: The Collapse in the North Field
最近，Fallowton 连续下了数周大雨。

一天清晨，北边山脚下的 barley field 忽然塌陷。泥水、碎石和断裂的树根一起滑入地下，露出一处巨大的空洞。最先发现它的人，是老农夫 Henric Voss。

他原本只是去查看被雨水冲坏的田埂，却在泥浆之间看见了一条向下延伸的石阶。

那条石阶不是自然形成的。

它太整齐，太平滑，也太古老。每一级台阶都像被某种耐心的手反复打磨过，边缘没有风化的缺口，表面却覆着厚厚的黑土。它仿佛在地下沉睡了几千年，却从未真正腐朽。

村民们聚在洞口，举着灯，谁也不愿第一个走下去。

从上方望去，塌陷处内部出奇地空旷。那不是普通的岩洞，而像是一座被大地吞下的厅堂。空洞中央矗立着一座半埋在泥土中的阶梯状建筑。它像一座小型金字塔，又像某种巨大的祭坛。上半部分从黑土与碎石中露出，苍白的石阶层层向上收拢；下半部分却继续向地下延伸，仿佛整座建筑原本不是为了通向天空，而是为了深入大地。

它的表面刻满细密的碑文和环形纹路。那些刻痕不像装饰，也不像普通文字，更像某种仪式化的计算。

村民们称它为 temple。

但没有人真觉得那只是神庙。

有人说应该立刻把洞口填上。有人说不能动，因为谁也不知道下面埋着什么。还有人只是站在雨里，盯着那座苍白的石质金字塔，一句话也说不出来。

Fallowton 的人知道旧山下面藏着故事。

他们只是从没想过，有一天，故事会从地底睁开眼睛。

Scene2: The Wizard Arrives
就在洞口出现后的第三天，一个陌生人来到了 Fallowton。
他的名字是 Mordren Vale。

Mordren Vale 是一名 wizard。对许多村民来说，他是他们这辈子见过的第一个真正的 wizard。

他穿着沾满旅尘的黑袍，手持一根 pale wood staff，手指上戴着几枚 silver rings。他说话礼貌，声音平静，但他的存在让人不安。每当他进入房间，人们都会下意识地停止交谈，仿佛炉火也因此变小了一些。

Mordren 声称自己只是一个研究 ancient ruins 的 scholar。他说地下的东西不应该被立刻摧毁，而应该被调查。

但村民们并不信任他。

他们私下议论：
“He came because of the hole.”
“He can smell buried magic.”
“Don’t let him look at your child too long.”
“Wizards always collect debts.”

Mordren 向村民提出，希望有人能下去查看地下到底有什么。

没有人答应。

没有农夫愿意去。
没有猎人愿意去。
甚至村里的守卫也拒绝进入那条石阶。

于是，Mordren 开始寻找外来者、旅人、佣兵、冒险者，或者任何胆子足够大、又需要报酬的人。

Why You Are Here
你们因为不同的原因来到了 Fallowton。

也许你是被大雨困在这里的旅人。
也许你是护卫、佣兵、学徒、流浪者，或者只是需要钱的人。
也许你听说了地下可能有 treasure。
也许你对 magic、ancient ruins，或者 forgotten history 有自己的兴趣。
也许你只是刚好在错误的时间，来到了错误的村庄。

Mordren Vale 找到了你们，并提出了一份工作。

他会付钱。

任务很简单：进入北边田地下的 stone stairway，确认下面到底是什么，然后活着回来。

当然，在这样的故事里，简单的任务往往只是门口的第一盏灯。
```

## Campaign truth (DM only)

Long ago, **the Dawnweave** was real. Magic was natural, abundant, and woven through rivers, stone, weather, starlight, and living things. People did not "cast" magic the way modern spellcasters do; they stood inside a world already full of it.

That golden memory is true, but incomplete.

### The hidden cost

The Dawnweave did not make magic free. It made the cost invisible.

At its height, Dawnweave civilization built ritual engines called **Converters**. Common folk remembered them as temples, altars, or holy houses, but they were magical machines wrapped in religious language.

Converters consumed **living memory**: life force, names, grief, blood, personal history, and the shape of a soul inside the world. A victim might die, but the deeper horror was erasure. Families forgot faces. Records lost names. Statues became blank. The pain of the few became the effortless wonder of the many.

The old saying is true:

> Magic did not become costly because the gods were cruel. It became costly because the old world spent its debt on everyone else.

### The wound beyond the world

Dawnweave rulers and high ritualists eventually tried to reach the **world magic core** directly. They wanted magic without limit, without distance, without even the old machinery.

They did not create evil itself.

They created a door.

Something from outside the world noticed the wound and entered. Working titles until named in play: **The Nameless Hunger**, **The Eater Beyond the Weave**, or **The Hollow Star**. It fed on magic, memory, and meaning. Dawnweave fell when this outside presence reached the world magic core and took hold of it.

### The divine seal

The gods could not simply destroy the invader without risking the core of magic itself. Instead, they sealed the world magic core away.

The seal saved the world, but it changed magic forever. Modern spellcasting still works, but magic is now conserved: power drawn through the broken, sealed Weave must be paid from the caster, the nearby world, a relic, or old stored debts. This is the truth beneath **Weave Strain**.

Peasant belief in Fallowton is therefore partly right. Magic is dangerous and can lead to horrible things, but not because magic was born evil. It is dangerous because the old world broke the channel, and something hungry still holds the source.

### Campaign endgames

1. **Reclaim the core:** Kill, banish, or sever the outside entity from the world magic core.
2. **Open the seal anew:** Use recovered Dawnweave knowledge and divine authority to reopen magic safely, without returning to the sacrificial Converter system.
3. **Bad ending — the Age of Ash:** If the party fails, the seal collapses inward. The entity devours what remains of the core. Magic thins into weak echoes, miracles fade, relics go cold, and the world enters a final age without true magic.

### This ruin

The buried structure beneath Fallowton is not only a temple. It is a **Dawnweave Converter**: a pyramid-like ritual engine once known as **The House of First Light**.

It was built to turn living memory into gentle, abundant magic for the surrounding region. After the fall, survivors buried and warded it to hide the evidence of what Dawnweave had become. The recent sinkhole means the old earth-seal is weakening.

**The ruin now:** Not a walking dungeon — **half-dead, half-dreaming**. Decayed Dawnweave wards act like a wounded animal: it senses intruders, blood, magic, greed, goblins, relics moved. See Module 2 for the **Ruin Awakening Clock**.

## Fallowton (surface)

A small walled farming village. Barley, turnips, rye, gray wheat. Unimportant on any map. People fear the forest and the old hills. They do not deny magic exists; they mistrust its price.

They say “old work,” “black craft,” “the wrong tongue,” “things better left buried” — not “magic” aloud.

**Old legend (villagers know fragments):**

> Before the black age, the world had no torches, because every stone remembered light. No one paid blood for miracles. Magic flowed like rain, and even children could speak to stars.

---

## Opening hook

**Trigger:** After weeks of rain, the **north barley field** collapses into a sinkhole. Stone stairs lead down. Carvings predate any kingdom — **Dawnweave**.

**Ominous discoveries (use these instead of village-haunting events):**

- The cavern is too open and too empty, like a chamber prepared for something vast.  
- The exposed structure is pyramid-like: half above the collapsed soil, half descending below it.  
- The stone is pale gray, cold, and not quarried from anywhere near Fallowton.  
- Doorways and steps are almost human-sized, but not quite; the proportions feel wrong after a few seconds.  
- Inscriptions cover the walls in rings, spirals, and repeated blank-faced figures.  
- Some carvings show kneeling people whose shadows flow upward into a central altar.  
- At night, faint blue light leaks from seams in the stone, more like old machinery than a haunting.

**Day 3:** **Mordren Vale** arrives — black travel robes, pale staff, silver rings. Polite, quiet; rooms go silent when he enters.

**Opening line (optional):**

> A farmer runs into the square covered in mud: “The hole is **breathing**.”

## Key NPCs (story beats)

### Elder Bran Orwick

Wants the temple **sealed**.

Before anyone enters, Bran demands that the party justify why strangers should be allowed under Fallowton land. If they cannot win enough trust, he insists they pay a village bond before descending.

> “Nothing good comes from beneath old earth.”

### Old Henric Voss

Found the stairs; field destroyed. Wants **compensation** if there is gold.

> “If there’s gold down there, some of it is mine.”

**Secret hook:** Broken dagger in Blue Light tunnel may bear **H.V.** or **W.V.** — Henric’s family may have found this ruin **60–100 years ago** and reburied it.

### Sister Elra

Faith leader; temple is **cursed**; old magic waking means holy failure.

> “If old magic is waking, then something holy has failed.”

### Mordren Vale

Claims scholar of ruins. This is true. He knows enough about Dawnweave to fear what "free magic" may have cost, but not enough to prove it.

**Hides:** He suspects the Fallowton ruin is not a temple but a Converter. His private thesis is: **"Free magic was never free."** He does not tell villagers because they would seal the site; he does not tell the party at first because he is not yet certain and needs evidence.

**Deeper secret:** Mordren is also quietly connected to the king's government. He is not a simple wandering scholar; he has been sent, funded, or protected by a royal office that tracks dangerous ruins and magical disturbances. Do not reveal this until the end of the opening arc, when it can point the campaign toward the next major location.

**Offer to party:**

> “Bring me any writing, relic, or living flame you find below. Touch nothing you do not understand. And if you find an altar, do not pray at it.”

## Act 1 — Descent (read-aloud)

Use this **instead of** any older “three doors” version.

```text
你们沿着黑色石阶向下走。

雨声逐渐远去，取而代之的是一种低沉、潮湿的寂静。石阶两侧的墙壁上刻着古老的浮雕：农夫托着光，孩子仰望星辰，河流从大地流向天空，树木弯下枝条，好像正在听人说话。

但越往深处走，浮雕越残破。

有些人的脸被刮掉。
有些星星被凿成黑色的空洞。
有些手掌被刻意毁去，仿佛有人不希望后人知道他们曾经握着什么。
还有一些跪下的人没有名字。他们的影子被刻成细长的线，沿着地面流向同一个方向，像溪水流入井中。

最后，石阶结束了。

你们来到一片塌陷的地下大厅。这里不像完整的 temple，更像一座已经死去很久、又被泥土重新吞下的 ruin。断裂的石柱倒在地上，墙壁裂开，树根从天花板垂下。

大厅中央立着那座从地面上看见过的阶梯状建筑。它的上半部分像金字塔，苍白、冷硬、层层收拢；它的下半部分却沉入更深的黑暗，像一根石钉钉进大地。围绕它的地面刻着一圈又一圈的纹路，某些沟槽里积着灰色尘土，仿佛很久以前曾有什么东西在其中流动。

在金字塔前方，有一个干涸的石池。池底没有水，只有薄薄一层灰尘。灰尘中隐约能看见许多重叠的掌印，小的、大的、人的、几乎像人的。

大厅前方，有三条长长的黑暗隧道。

左边的隧道深处，漂浮着一些 tiny blue glowing lights。它们像萤火，又像有人在黑暗中慢慢眨眼。隧道入口前，插着一把 broken dagger。刀身已经断裂，但握柄上还残留着古老的纹路。

中间的隧道里，传来奇怪的声音。像是小东西在石头上爬行，像是骨头被拖过地面，也像是有人在很远的地方低声笑。

右边的隧道最安静。没有脚步声，没有低语声，只有一阵空洞的冷风从深处吹来。那风经过你们身边时，像是在寻找某个人的名字。

石池中的灰尘忽然轻轻震动了一下。

这座 ruin 好像在睡梦中，听见了你们。

而那座苍白的金字塔，像是一颗埋在世界骨头里的旧心脏，仍在等待下一次跳动。
```

**Then ask (English at table if preferred):**

> “Which tunnel do you want to examine first?”

## Tunnel summaries (story tone)

### Tunnel 1 — Blue Light (undead / protectors)

- Motes = fragments of old ward-magic near places defenders died.  
- **Broken dagger:** not Dawnweave-old; **60–100 years**; initials **H.V. / W.V.** **【confirm initials】**  
- Undead are **oath-bound guardians**, twisted because magic is cursed — not random evil.  
- Final guardian: **Edras, Last Guard of the Blue Flame** — broken armor, blue fire in ribs.

**Edras line:**

> “Thieves came after the fall. Goblins came after the dark. Which are you?”

**Reward (recommend one primary):** **Lantern of First Light** (lore + hidden writing); alternates: Star-Iron Dagger, Oath-Bound Shield.

**After tunnel (return to hall):** Blue motes sink into dry pool; in dust, shape of **black spring beneath white tree**.

---

### Tunnel 2 — Weird Sounds (goblins / gold)

**【DM DECISION: goblin tone A/B/C】** — Default in this handbook: **cruel scavengers**, not comic.

- Tribe name: **The Nail-Eater Goblins** (chew iron nails, bones, coins as ritual).  
- Leader: **Grask the Split-Ear** — elite, chain scraps, hooked blade, torn ear.

**Tone examples:** collect teeth; corpse-fat candles; imitate voices to lure; fear old wards but exploit broken zones.

**Nest room:** stolen Fallowton goods (wedding ring, children’s toys) — proves village threat.

**Grask line:**

> “Soft skins come down. Soft skins bring warm blood. Good. Good.”

**Gold pit:** mixed with bones and rot; **black coin** that never warms — future curse hook.

**After tunnel:** sounds stop; then **one wet laugh** echoes from all tunnels, as if something deeper was amused.

---

### Tunnel 3 — Empty Wind (traps / map)

- Wind = **circulating protection magic**, not natural air; closest to ruin’s “mind.”  
- Dead goblins in traps; one survivor may beg, flee, or betray **【evil goblins default: betrayal possible】**.

**Puzzle hint (three carvings):**

> “Gold feeds the living.”  
> “Steel arms the desperate.”  
> “Memory saves the world.”

**Trap (simple):** Floor of footprint carvings — careless steps replay violent memory. **Dex save DC 12**; fail = small damage; do not overtune for level 1.

**Final room — Breathless Archive:** silence; **Dawnweave Map** (silver-bark) floats above broken table. Touch → torches out 1 second → map shows **Fallowton**, **Black Spring**, **Crownless City**, black stain spreading under all three.

**After tunnel:** wind stops; ruin **silent**; stone pool fills **one inch of black water**.

## Session 1 endings (pick one or combine)

**Village reaction (Act 5):**

- **Ancient gear:** fear — “dead men’s tools”; Mordren very interested.  
- **Gold:** greed and arguments — who owns Fallowton’s past?  
- **Map:** villagers don’t understand; Mordren: “That is a map of the world **before it broke**.”

**Campaign hooks (choose one closing image):**

1. Everyone dreams of white towers under a black sun; by morning, **Mordren is gone**.
2. Village well full of **silver leaves** from trees that don’t grow here.
3. A villager forgets the name of her dead husband, then finds it carved inside the sinkhole.

---

## What players should learn in Session 1

Symbolic lesson — three corruptions:

- **Blue:** duty without wisdom  
- **Sounds:** hunger without sacredness  
- **Wind:** knowledge without safety

Magic was not evil at first. Dawnweave became corrupt when it made the cost invisible, and its hunger for perfect magic opened a door for something worse.

## DM voice notes

- **Not** a designed dungeon with doors — a **buried living ruin**: roots, cracks, collapsed hall.  
- Goblins: **evil occupants**, not misunderstood neighbors **【unless you choose B/C】**.  
- Respect vs loot sets undead tone in Blue Light tunnel.  
- Only run **one tunnel deeply** first session; other tunnels remain; clock and NPCs can change them later.
