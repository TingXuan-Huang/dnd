# Exhaustion

The only condition that **stacks** (1–6 levels). While you have the Exhaustion condition:

- **Exhaustion Levels.** Cumulative; each time you receive it, you gain 1 Exhaustion level. **You die when your Exhaustion level reaches 6.**
- **D20 Tests Affected.** Reduce the roll by `2 × your Exhaustion level` on every [[d20-test]].
- **Speed Reduced.** Your [[speed]] is reduced by `5 × your Exhaustion level` feet.

[BR-2024 §rules-glossary#ExhaustionCondition]

## Removal

- **Long Rest.** Finishing a [[long-rest]] removes **1 level**.
- The condition ends when your Exhaustion level reaches 0.

## Common sources

- [[long-rest|Skipping a Long Rest]] for too long
- Dehydration (drinking < half required water per day)
- Malnutrition (eating < half required food per day for 5+ days)
- Suffocation (1 level per turn out of breath)
- Some spells (*Sickening Radiance*, *Forcecage*)
- Some monster abilities (Banshee wail)

## Changes from 5e

**Fully reworked in 2024.** The 2014 version had a 6-level table with distinct effects per level (L1 = Disadv on ability checks, L2 = Speed halved, L3 = Disadv on attacks/saves, L4 = HP max halved, L5 = Speed 0, L6 = death). The 2024 version replaces all that with a uniform formula: **−2 per level on all D20 tests** and **−5 ft Speed per level**. Equally lethal at level 6, simpler bookkeeping.
