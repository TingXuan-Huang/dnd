# Fallowton Character Creation Guide

*Player-facing custom guide · Mostly D&D 2024 / 5.5e rules · Fallowton campaign overlay*

Use this guide first. It keeps character creation focused on **this campaign**. For exact rules text, use the official D&D Beyond 2024 Basic Rules or the longer [Character Creation Guide](character-creation-guide.md).

---

## The Short Version

You are making a level 1 character who has a reason to enter the buried ruin beneath Fallowton.

Follow this order:

1. Pick a **story concept**.
2. Pick why you are in **Fallowton**.
3. Pick a **class**.
4. Pick a **background**.
5. Pick a **species** and **languages**.
6. Assign **ability scores**.
7. Choose **equipment**.
8. If you use magic, read **Weave Strain**.
9. Answer three final roleplay questions.

At the table, your character should be willing to cooperate with the party. Secretive is fine. Party-sabotaging is not.

## Step 1 — Pick A Story Concept

Start with a simple sentence, not a build.

Examples:

- A local fieldhand who saw the ground collapse.
- A young acolyte who thinks the ruin is a divine warning.
- A hired sword trapped in Fallowton by the rain.
- A scholar's assistant who wants proof that Dawnweave was real.
- A desperate traveler who heard there might be treasure below.
- A spellcaster afraid of what their own magic costs.
- A wanderer whose family name appears in an old village story.

Good Fallowton characters have at least one of these:

- A reason to care about the village.
- A reason to need money.
- A reason to investigate old magic.
- A reason to prove themselves.
- A reason they cannot simply walk away.

## Step 2 — Pick Why You Are Here

Choose one hook, or write your own:

| Hook | What It Means |
|------|---------------|
| Local | You live in or near Fallowton. Someone you know is afraid. |
| Rain-Trapped Traveler | You meant to pass through, but the roads are mud and the inn is full of rumors. |
| Hired Guard | Elder Bran, Henric, Mordren, or a merchant hired you. |
| Mordren's Contact | You know the wizard, studied under him, or were sent to watch him. |
| Sister Elra's Acolyte | You worry the ruin is a spiritual wound. |
| Treasure-Seeker | You heard "old ruin" and thought "old gold." |
| Magic-Touched | You have personal experience with Weave Strain or black craft. |
| Sinkhole Witness | You saw the collapse and cannot forget what was under the earth. |

Write one sentence:

> I am in Fallowton because...

Then write one more:

> I will go into the ruin because...

## Step 3 — Pick A Class

All official Basic Rules classes are allowed. Choose the fantasy first. The numbers can follow.

For new players, "complexity" means how much you usually need to track during play. A complex class is still allowed; it just asks for more reading.

| Class | What You Do | Fallowton Story Fit | Complexity |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|------------|
| Barbarian | Fight up close, take hits, rage in battle. | Hill survivor, woodcutter, caravan tough, someone who distrusts soft scholarly answers. | Easy |
| Bard | Support allies, use magic, gather stories, talk your way through trouble. | Wandering singer, rumor collector, keeper of old songs about Dawnweave. | Medium |
| Cleric | Use divine magic, protect allies, heal, turn undead. | Sister Elra's circle, servant of a god, someone worried the ruin proves a holy failure. | Medium |
| Druid | Use nature magic, understand beasts, weather, land, and old growth. | Forest hermit, river watcher, keeper of the old hill's warnings. | Hard |
| Fighter | Use weapons well, survive danger, solve problems directly. | Village guard, mercenary, retired soldier, farmer with old training. | Easy |
| Monk | Move fast, fight with discipline, rely on body and spirit. | Wandering ascetic, monastery seeker, someone who senses imbalance below the earth. | Medium |
| Paladin | Fight with weapons and divine purpose, protect others, swear an oath. | Sworn protector, monster-hunter, knight errant, believer that some doors should stay shut. | Medium |
| Ranger | Scout, track, use weapons, survive wilderness, later use nature magic. | Forest scout, road guide, hunter, tracker of things moving near the sinkhole. | Medium |
| Rogue | Sneak, scout, pick locks, strike precisely, notice danger. | Smuggler, poacher, tomb-robber, desperate local, lockpicker hired for the ruin. | Easy |
| Sorcerer | Cast innate magic that comes from blood, soul, or strange power. | Weave-touched local, person born under a bad omen, someone whose magic hurts them. | Hard |
| Warlock | Cast magic gained through a pact with a patron. | Someone who made a bargain for survival, knowledge, revenge, or a way out. | Medium |
| Wizard | Study spells, prepare magic, solve mysteries through knowledge. | Scholar, failed apprentice, Mordren's contact, rival researcher of Dawnweave ruins. | Hard |

Spellcasters are allowed. They are not punished for existing. But in this campaign, visible magic has social consequences, and leveled spells create Weave Strain.

### If You Are New And Unsure

Pick based on the kind of turn you want to have:

| I Want To... | Good Class Choices |
|--------------|--------------------|
| Stand in front and protect people | Fighter, Barbarian, Paladin |
| Sneak, scout, and find traps | Rogue, Ranger |
| Heal or protect with divine power | Cleric, Paladin |
| Study the ruin and cast prepared spells | Wizard |
| Use strange personal magic | Sorcerer, Warlock |
| Be flexible and social | Bard |
| Be tied to nature and old land | Druid, Ranger |
| Fight with speed and discipline | Monk |

No class is required. A party without a healer can still play. A party with many spellcasters can still play, but Weave Strain will matter more.

## Step 4 — Pick A Background

In 2024 rules, a background is very important. It describes what shaped you before adventuring, and it gives real mechanics: ability score boosts, an Origin feat, skills, a tool, and equipment or gold.

For this campaign, choose one of three paths:

1. Use a **General D&D Background** from the free Basic Rules.
2. Use a **Fallowton Background** made for this campaign.
3. **Build Your Own Background** with DM approval.

### General D&D Backgrounds

The free 2024 Basic Rules include four backgrounds. These are useful for characters who are not deeply tied to Fallowton, or for players who want a clean official option.

| Background | Rules Package | What It Means | Fallowton Use |
|------------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Acolyte** | Abilities: Int/Wis/Cha. Feat: Magic Initiate (Cleric). Skills: Insight, Religion. Tool: Calligrapher's Supplies. | You served a temple, shrine, priesthood, or sacred tradition. You know prayers, rites, doctrine, and how religious people think. | Sister Elra's helper, village shrine student, wandering believer, someone who fears the ruin is a divine warning. |
| **Criminal** | Abilities: Dex/Con/Int. Feat: Alert. Skills: Sleight of Hand, Stealth. Tool: Thieves' Tools. | You survived through theft, smuggling, burglary, or other illegal work. You know how to move quietly and notice danger. | Poacher, smuggler, debt-runner, lockpicker hired for the ruin, someone who came because old ruins mean old valuables. |
| **Sage** | Abilities: Con/Int/Wis. Feat: Magic Initiate (Wizard). Skills: Arcana, History. Tool: Calligrapher's Supplies. | You learned through books, libraries, monasteries, tutors, or research. You know lore and want more. | Mordren's assistant, rival scholar, failed student, ruin researcher, someone chasing proof that Dawnweave was real. |
| **Soldier** | Abilities: Str/Dex/Con. Feat: Savage Attacker. Skills: Athletics, Intimidation. Tool: one Gaming Set. | You were trained for war, guard duty, patrols, or organized violence. You know weapons, discipline, and fear. | Village guard, caravan guard, old militia fighter, mercenary, someone hired because no farmer will enter the stairway. |

How to choose:

- Pick **Acolyte** if your story begins with gods, fear, duty, or faith.
- Pick **Criminal** if your story begins with hunger, debt, stealth, or bad choices.
- Pick **Sage** if your story begins with books, ruins, magic, or forbidden history.
- Pick **Soldier** if your story begins with training, violence, protection, or paid work.

### Fallowton Backgrounds

Fallowton backgrounds use the same 2024 structure, but they are written for this story.

Use [Sinkhole Witness](../campaign/background-sinkhole-witness.md) if you were there when the north field collapsed or grew up hearing that the old hills remember things.

Finished Fallowton backgrounds:

| Background | Use It For |
|------------|------------|
| **Fallowton Fieldhand** | Farmer, laborer, mill worker, stable hand, or someone whose family depends on the fields. |
| **Village Guard** | A local protector, watch member, old militia recruit, or practical fighter. |
| **Village Doctor** | A healer, bonesetter, midwife, or herbal caregiver. |
| **Mechanist** | A tinker, gearworker, millwright, or repairer of mundane and weakly magical devices. |
| **Forest Poacher** | Someone who knows the forbidden woods, traps, tracks, and quiet paths. |
| **Failed Apprentice** | Someone who studied magic briefly and learned that magic always has a cost. |
| **Ruin Scholar** | A researcher, scribe, translator, or amateur historian drawn to Dawnweave. |

Future optional backgrounds to build later:

- **Chapel Acolyte** once the role of the chapel and local religion is clearer.
- **Rain-Trapped Traveler** if the table wants a more general outsider background.

#### Fallowton Fieldhand

You worked Fallowton's soil: barley, rye, turnips, gray wheat. You know mud, weather, fences, tools, harvests, and bad winters. You may not know ancient magic, but you know when the land is wrong.

**Ability Scores:** Strength, Constitution, Wisdom

**Feat:** Alert

**Skill Proficiencies:** Nature, Survival

**Tool Proficiency:** Carpenter's Tools

**Equipment:** Choose A or B:

- **A:** Spear, Shovel, Carpenter's Tools, Traveler's Clothes, Rope, 3 Torches, 3 days of Rations, Backpack, and 12 GP
- **B:** 50 GP

**Fallowton Hooks:** Choose one or write your own.

- You work near Henric Voss's field and saw the collapse yourself.
- Your family's crops are close to the sinkhole. If the ruin spreads, you lose everything.
- Before the collapse, you noticed animals avoiding the north field, strange soil, or blue light in the mud.
- Bad harvest left you desperate. Mordren's coin is enough to risk the stairway.
- Someone you love wants to go down, or might be taken advantage of by Mordren.
- You do not understand magic, but you know soil, rain, and crops. Something under the field is not dead.

#### Village Guard

You served as one of Fallowton's few protectors. Most days, that meant breaking up tavern fights, watching the east road, helping during floods, and making sure no one wandered too deep into the woods. You are not a knight, but when the village is afraid, people look to you first.

**Ability Scores:** Strength, Dexterity, Constitution

**Feat:** Alert

**Skill Proficiencies:** Perception, Intimidation

**Tool Proficiency:** Vehicles (Land)

**Equipment:** Choose A or B:

- **A:** Spear, Shortbow, 20 Arrows, Quiver, Leather Armor, Signal Whistle, Traveler's Clothes, 2 Torches, Rations, and 10 GP
- **B:** 50 GP

**Fallowton Hooks:** Choose one or write your own.

- Elder Bran ordered you to keep people away from the sinkhole, but Mordren wants someone brave enough to go down.
- You were on watch the night before the collapse and heard stone moving under the rain.
- You know the village cannot defend itself if something comes up from below.
- You once escorted Mordren or another scholar through dangerous roads and do not fully trust them.
- A child, sibling, parent, or friend expects you to protect Fallowton.
- You are tired of being treated like a guard for fences and chickens. This is your chance to prove you are more.

#### Village Doctor

You are a village healer: bonesetter, midwife, sickroom watcher, herbal caregiver, or practical doctor. You may not have studied in a grand city, but people come to you when children fever, old wounds reopen, and bad weather brings sickness into the house.

**Ability Scores:** Wisdom, Constitution, Charisma

**Feat:** Skilled

**Skill Proficiencies:** Medicine, Insight

**Tool Proficiency:** Herbalism Kit

**Equipment:** Choose A or B:

- **A:** Quarterstaff, Dagger, Healer's Kit, Herbalism Kit, Traveler's Clothes, Blanket, 2 Vials, 3 Candles, Soap, Pouch, and 10 GP
- **B:** 50 GP

**Fallowton Hooks:** Choose one or write your own.

- Henric Voss came to you after the collapse, shaking, muddy, and unable to sleep.
- Since the sinkhole opened, old wounds ache, fevers behave strangely, or patients mutter forgotten words.
- You have seen what magic costs people's bodies. You do not trust a wizard who calls this "research."
- Your stores are low after weeks of rain. Rare herbs or old remedies might exist below.
- Someone sick, elderly, pregnant, or young in Fallowton may be affected if the ruin wakes.
- You believe the ruin may explain why magic harms the body.

#### Mechanist

Because magic is weak, costly, and mistrusted, people have learned to rely on machinery: gears, pulleys, pumps, locks, lenses, levers, waterwheels, pressure systems, and small devices. Some machines are purely mundane. Others are weakly powered by magic, old batteries, strange crystals, or recovered Dawnweave fragments.

You are one of the people who understands such things.

**Ability Scores:** Intelligence, Dexterity, Constitution

**Feat:** Skilled

**Skill Proficiencies:** Investigation, Arcana

**Tool Proficiency:** Tinker's Tools

**Equipment:** Choose A or B:

- **A:** Tinker's Tools, Light Hammer, Traveler's Clothes, 50 feet of String, Bell, Signal Whistle, Pouch, and 2 GP
- **B:** 50 GP

**Fallowton Hooks:** Choose one or write your own.

- You looked at the exposed stone and realized it was not just a temple. Something about it has design.
- Mordren does not only need swords. He needs someone who understands mechanisms.
- Since the collapse, Fallowton's mill, pump, or waterwheel has behaved strangely.
- You own or found a fragmentary diagram that resembles the pyramid structure.
- You believe machinery is how ordinary people survive in a world where magic hurts.
- If Dawnweave could build machines that moved magic, perhaps you can learn how.

#### Forest Poacher

You know the woods that villagers warn children not to enter. Maybe you hunted illegally, trapped rabbits, cut forbidden timber, smuggled food, or guided people through old deer paths. You are not necessarily evil; you just know that laws matter less when winter is long and children are hungry.

**Ability Scores:** Dexterity, Wisdom, Constitution

**Feat:** Alert

**Skill Proficiencies:** Stealth, Survival

**Tool Proficiency:** Woodcarver's Tools

**Equipment:** Choose A or B:

- **A:** Shortbow, 20 Arrows, Quiver, Dagger, Hunting Trap, Woodcarver's Tools, Traveler's Clothes, Cloak, Rope, 2 Torches, Rations, Pouch, and 9 GP
- **B:** 50 GP

**Fallowton Hooks:** Choose one or write your own.

- You have gone where villagers say not to go, and you know old paths near the hill.
- Animals fled the north field days before the sinkhole opened.
- You were caught poaching, and entering the ruin is your chance to avoid punishment.
- The rain ruined hunting and trapping. Mordren's pay is hard to refuse.
- You once found a carved stone, blue-lit root, or strange track near the old hill.
- The respectable villagers call the woods cursed, but you know the village keeps secrets too.

#### Failed Apprentice

You studied magic briefly under a village wise-person, traveling tutor, minor academy, hedge wizard, religious scholar, or stolen notes. You did not become a proper wizard. Maybe you lacked money, discipline, talent, or luck. Maybe the magic hurt you. Maybe you saw what it did to someone else.

**Ability Scores:** Intelligence, Constitution, Wisdom

**Alternate Ability Scores:** Wisdom, Constitution, Charisma for folk-magic or dream-touched versions

**Feat:** Magic Initiate (Wizard)

**Alternate Feat Source:** Magic Initiate (Cleric or Druid) for divine, folk, or nature-based apprenticeship

**Skill Proficiencies:** Arcana, Investigation

**Alternate Skill Proficiencies:** Arcana, Insight for a more intuitive or emotionally scarred apprentice

**Tool Proficiency:** Calligrapher's Supplies

**Alternate Tool Proficiencies:** Herbalism Kit, Alchemist's Supplies, or Tinker's Tools depending on mentor style

**Equipment:** Choose A or B:

- **A:** Quarterstaff, Dagger, Calligrapher's Supplies, Burned Notebook, Ink Pen, 5 sheets of Parchment, 3 Candles, Vial of Black Ink, Traveler's Clothes, Pouch, and 9 GP
- **B:** 50 GP

The Burned Notebook is a story item, not a Wizard spellbook unless your class gives you a real spellbook.

**Fallowton Hooks:** Choose one or write your own.

- Your first real spell left a scar, sickness, memory gap, or fear of casting again.
- The person who taught you magic disappeared, died, or went mad after researching old ruins.
- Mordren recognizes your notes, school, mentor, or failed experiment.
- You believe Dawnweave may explain how magic became dangerous.
- A school, temple, or tutor dismissed you after an accident.
- Since the collapse, your cantrips behave strangely, or your dreams contain symbols from the stone.

**Weave Strain:** You are magic-touched. Strain costs at levels 1–2 are often **ill luck**, **memory**, or **signs** (not just damage). Burnout save **DC +2** if you cast at strain 3. Details: [Who bears worse luck](../campaign/weave-strain-magic.md#who-bears-worse-luck-and-worse-death-risk).

#### Ruin Scholar

**Weave Strain:** Memory-themed strain costs count as **+1 Luck debt**. Activating seals at strain 2+ may trigger **Overreach** on a failed Arcana check. See [weave-strain-magic.md](../campaign/weave-strain-magic.md#who-bears-worse-luck-and-worse-death-risk).

You study old places: broken towers, buried roads, dead languages, sealed tombs, abandoned shrines, and the kind of ruins most sensible people avoid. You may be formally educated, self-taught, hired by Mordren, or obsessed with one unanswered question: what was the world before it broke?

**Ability Scores:** Intelligence, Wisdom, Constitution

**Alternate Ability Scores:** Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma for lecturers, negotiators, or patron-facing scholars

**Feat:** Skilled

**Skill Proficiencies:** History, Investigation

**Tool Proficiency:** Cartographer's Tools

**Equipment:** Choose A or B:

- **A:** Quarterstaff, Dagger, Cartographer's Tools, Map or Scroll Case, 5 sheets of Parchment, Ink Pen, Lamp, Flask of Oil, Chalk, Traveler's Clothes, Rations, and 10 GP
- **B:** 50 GP

**Fallowton Hooks:** Choose one or write your own.

- You exchanged letters with Mordren before the collapse.
- You found Fallowton marked on an old map, though it should be too small to matter.
- You want proof that Dawnweave was real, false, or misunderstood.
- Mordren thinks this is one kind of ruin. You suspect another.
- Other sinkholes, old roads, or ruined towers form a line leading here.
- Your research suggests ancient "temples" may have been machines, prisons, or worse.

### Build Your Own Background

If none of the above fits, you can make a custom background using the 2024 structure:

| Part | Choose |
|------|--------|
| Ability Scores | Pick three abilities that fit the background. Add +2 to one and +1 to another, or +1 to all three. |
| Origin Feat | Pick one approved Origin feat from the free Basic Rules. |
| Skills | Pick two skills. |
| Tool | Pick one tool proficiency. |
| Equipment | Pick a modest equipment package or take 50 GP. |
| Fallowton Hook | Write why this background makes you enter the ruin. |

Custom background examples:

| Background | Ability Scores | Feat | Skills | Tool |
|------------|----------------|------|--------|------|
| Fallowton Fieldhand | Str, Con, Wis | Alert | Nature, Survival | Carpenter's Tools |
| Chapel Acolyte | Int, Wis, Cha | Magic Initiate (Cleric) | Insight, Religion | Calligrapher's Supplies |
| Ruin-Touched Dreamer | Con, Int, Wis | Magic Initiate (Wizard) or Alert | Arcana, Insight | Calligrapher's Supplies |
| Road-Worn Hireling | Str, Dex, Con | Savage Attacker or Alert | Athletics, Intimidation | Gaming Set |
| Village Poacher | Dex, Con, Wis | Alert | Stealth, Survival | Thieves' Tools or Woodcarver's Tools |
| Failed Apprentice | Con, Int, Cha | Magic Initiate (Wizard) | Arcana, Investigation | Calligrapher's Supplies |

The DM can rename backgrounds freely. Mechanics should stay close to official 2024 background power.

Good custom backgrounds are specific but not overpowered. "Fallowton Fieldhand" is better than "Chosen God-Killer." The second might become true someday, but level 1 characters should begin small enough that the ruin can change them.

Custom background equipment should stay close to official background value. If one item is very expensive, like Tinker's Tools, the rest of the package should be modest.

## Step 5 — Pick Species And Languages

Use official 2024 species unless the DM approves something else.

Species is not your job or personality. It gives traits like size, speed, darkvision, resistance, or special movement. Pick something that supports the character idea, then decide how common or unusual you are in Fallowton.

| Species | Simple Identity | Fallowton Fit |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| Dragonborn | Draconic ancestry, breath weapon, elemental resistance | Usually an outsider or rare family line; memorable in a small village |
| Dwarf | Sturdy, long-lived, stone/forge flavor | Good for miners, smiths, mechanists, guards, ruin scholars |
| Elf | Long-lived, keen senses, fey/forest flavor | Good for forest ties, old songs, Dawnweave curiosity |
| Gnome | Clever, small, magical or inventive flavor | Excellent for mechanists, scholars, strange travelers |
| Goliath | Large, powerful, giant ancestry flavor | Usually outsider; good for guards, laborers, road protectors |
| Halfling | Small, lucky, practical, community-minded | Very easy local fit; fieldhands, doctors, inn workers, poachers |
| Human | Flexible, common, adaptable | Easiest fit for any Fallowton role |
| Orc | Tough, relentless, strong survival flavor | Usually outsider or edge-of-village family; good for guards, hunters, laborers |
| Tiefling | Fiendish legacy, unusual appearance, supernatural edge | Strong fit for mistrusted magic themes; likely noticed by villagers |

Quick DM guideline:

- Humans, halflings, dwarves, and elves can be common enough in Fallowton.
- Dragonborn, gnomes, goliaths, orcs, and tieflings are allowed, but the player should decide whether they are a rare local or an outsider.
- Do not punish unusual species constantly. Use it for flavor, first impressions, and occasional social tension.

### Languages

Everyone knows **Common** plus **two more languages** from the standard list.

Pick languages for story, not optimization:

| Language | Good For |
|----------|----------|
| Common Sign Language | Silent communication, travelers, guards, accessibility |
| Draconic | Ancient magic, old formulas, arcane scholarship |
| Dwarvish | Stonework, smithing, ruins, underground craft |
| Elvish | Old forests, songs, long memory, Dawnweave echoes |
| Giant | Old hills, goliaths, ancient stone legends |
| Gnomish | Mechanists, clever devices, technical notes |
| Goblin | Tunnels, raiders, scavengers, interrogation |
| Halfling | Local families, travelers, rural communities |
| Orc | Road clans, warriors, outsiders, survival cultures |

Recommended Fallowton picks:

- **Dwarvish** if you care about stone, machines, or underground ruins.
- **Elvish** if you care about the forest, old songs, or Dawnweave legends.
- **Goblin** if you expect trouble in tunnels.
- **Draconic** if you want arcane scholarship.
- **Common Sign Language** if you want useful silent communication.

## Step 6 — Assign Ability Scores

Recommended method: standard array.

Use these numbers before background boosts:

> 15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8

Put your highest number in your class's primary ability:

| Class | Primary Ability |
|-------|-----------------|
| Barbarian | Strength |
| Bard | Charisma |
| Cleric | Wisdom |
| Druid | Wisdom |
| Fighter | Strength or Dexterity |
| Monk | Dexterity and Wisdom |
| Paladin | Strength and Charisma |
| Ranger | Dexterity and Wisdom |
| Rogue | Dexterity |
| Sorcerer | Charisma |
| Warlock | Charisma |
| Wizard | Intelligence |

Then apply your background boosts:

- Add **+2** to one background ability and **+1** to another background ability, or
- Add **+1** to all three background abilities.

### What Each Ability Does

| Ability | Matters For |
|---------|-------------|
| Strength | Heavy melee attacks, grappling, climbing, jumping, carrying |
| Dexterity | Armor Class for light/no armor, initiative, stealth, bows, finesse weapons |
| Constitution | Hit Points, endurance, resisting poison/disease, surviving strain |
| Intelligence | Arcana, History, Investigation, Wizard magic |
| Wisdom | Perception, Insight, Survival, Cleric/Druid/Ranger magic |
| Charisma | Persuasion, Deception, Intimidation, Bard/Sorcerer/Warlock/Paladin magic |

### Where Should My Lowest Score Go?

There is no official rule for your lowest score. But for new players:

- Usually safe low scores: **Strength**, **Intelligence**, or **Charisma**, depending on class.
- Risky low scores: **Constitution**, **Dexterity**, or **Wisdom**.

Why:

- Low **Constitution** means fewer Hit Points.
- Low **Dexterity** can mean worse Armor Class and Initiative.
- Low **Wisdom** can mean poor Perception and weaker resistance to fear or mental danger.

Fallowton recommendation:

> Do not put your 8 in Constitution unless you really want a fragile character. Put the 8 in Strength, Intelligence, or Charisma if that fits your class and story.

### Fast Examples

| Character Type | Put 15 Here | Good Low Score |
|----------------|-------------|----------------|
| Fighter with sword | Strength | Intelligence or Charisma |
| Archer / Rogue | Dexterity | Strength |
| Cleric / Druid | Wisdom | Dexterity or Charisma |
| Wizard / Scholar | Intelligence | Strength or Charisma |
| Bard / Sorcerer / Warlock | Charisma | Strength |
| Tough local survivor | Constitution as 14 or 15 | Intelligence or Charisma |

## Step 7 — Choose Equipment

Use your class and background equipment, or take gold if the rules allow.

For new players, use the listed equipment packages. Buying every item one by one is slower and easier to mess up.

Make sure you have:

- A weapon you are proficient with.
- Armor you are trained to wear, if your class uses armor.
- A light source.
- Food and basic travel gear.
- A spellcasting focus or component pouch if you cast spells.
- Ammunition if you use a bow or crossbow.
- One personal item that says something about your past.

### What Comes From Class vs Background?

Your **class equipment** usually gives your combat kit: weapons, armor, spellcasting focus, pack, and adventuring basics.

Your **background equipment** gives your old-life kit: tools, personal items, work gear, or trade goods.

If you are unsure, do this:

1. Take your class's suggested equipment.
2. Take your background's Equipment A package.
3. Ask the DM to check for duplicates or missing basics.

### Dungeon Basics

Before entering the sinkhole, the party should collectively have:

- At least two light sources.
- At least one rope.
- Someone who can heal or use a Healer's Kit.
- A way to open, break, or work around stuck doors/chests.
- Someone with decent Perception or Investigation.

Fallowton-friendly personal items:

- A muddy field knife.
- A prayer cord from Sister Elra.
- A silver ring from Mordren's notes.
- A child's drawing of the sinkhole.
- A broken farm tool repurposed as a weapon.
- A blank coin that never warms.
- A page copied from an unreadable inscription.

## Step 8 — Magic And Weave Strain

Read [Weave Strain Magic](../campaign/weave-strain-magic.md) if your character can cast spells.

Player-facing summary:

- The world knows magic exists; Fallowton knows it **always costs** (exhaustion, sickness, madness, harm to others).
- Villagers say **black craft**, **old work**, **wrong tongue** — “anything borrowed from magic must be repaid.”
- Cantrips usually do not cause strain (weak echoes of the old Dawnweave).
- Leveled spells add **Weave Strain** (+1 per cast): damage, signs, Weakened, **bad luck** (disadvantage on rolls), social fear, memory slips, or the ruin waking up.
- At **strain 3**, you must rest before more leveled magic; casting anyway can **knock you out or kill you** (Constitution save).
- **Ill luck** strain costs steal fortune (disadvantage on d20s, optional **Luck debt** tracker).
- Some **classes and backgrounds** bear worse luck or higher burnout risk — see [Who bears worse luck](../campaign/weave-strain-magic.md#who-bears-worse-luck-and-worse-death-risk) (Sorcerer, Warlock, Failed Apprentice, Sinkhole Witness, etc.).
- Underground, near the buried **Converter** (House of First Light), strain and **Overreach** are worse.
- **Purification** milestones let you cast more safely **without** repeating the Converter’s evil (no stealing others’ memories).

### If You Are A Spellcaster

Write these on your sheet:

- Spellcasting ability
- Spell save DC
- Spell attack bonus
- Cantrips known/prepared
- Level 1 spells known/prepared
- Current Weave Strain: 0

Ask the DM if your background spell from Magic Initiate causes strain. Default: cantrips usually do not; leveled spells usually can.

Magic-related custom backgrounds (strain + luck/death hooks):

- **Failed Apprentice:** magic-touched; strain 1–2 often **ill luck / memory**; burnout save **DC +2**.
- **Mechanist:** no strain for normal machines; Dawnweave-powered casts may add **+1 Luck debt**.
- **Ruin Scholar:** memory strain counts extra for Luck debt; seal activation can trigger **Overreach**.
- **Village Doctor:** treats strain; **Medicine DC 14** on short rest lets ally **reroll one burnout save** per session.
- **Sinkhole Witness:** worse luck and burnout **underground**; disadv. on Survival/Perception near the field after casting.
- **Magic-Touched** (any): start with **Luck debt 1** if you have any spellcasting.

Character ideas that fit this rule:

- "I only cast when I must."
- "I believe magic is holy, but wounded."
- "I think villagers are ignorant and afraid."
- "I have seen magic save a life and ruin one."
- "I want to prove Dawnweave magic can be healed."

## DM Notes For Customization

Use official 2024 rules as the foundation. Add custom features only when they create campaign flavor without raising raw power too much.

Safe additions:

- Custom background names.
- Custom background equipment.
- A personal omen, dream, rumor, or family tie.
- A harmless magical scar or sign.
- A secret linked to Mordren, Henric, Sister Elra, or the sinkhole.

Use caution:

- Extra feats.
- Extra spells.
- Better armor or weapons than the class normally gets.
- Ignoring Weave Strain.
- Homebrew species or classes.

Best rule:

> If one player gets a mechanical advantage, either everyone gets something comparable, or it becomes a story reward later.
