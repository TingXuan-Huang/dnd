# Background: Failed Apprentice

*Homebrew · Fallowton campaign · Formatted like D&D 2024 Basic Rules backgrounds*

Use this background if your character was once being trained in magic — by a master, a college, a hedge witch, a temple — and that training ended badly. You left, or were sent away, or were warned off before you ever cast a real spell.

---

## Failed Apprentice

You were taught the price before you paid it.

For a year, or three, or seven, someone took you in and tried to teach you. They showed you the cantrips first — the small, safe echoes — and then the warnings. They made you copy the same diagram of the broken channel until your hand cramped. They told you what happened to the apprentice before you, and the one before her. They wanted to be sure you understood.

You understood. That was the problem. The day you were supposed to cast your first leveled spell, you either could not, or would not, or did and something went wrong that everyone agreed was your fault. You were sent home with the warnings still in your head and no proof you had earned them.

Now you live among people who do not know what you almost became. You catch yourself watching wizards. You catch yourself counting strain on strangers like you can still read it.

**Ability Scores:** Intelligence, Charisma, Wisdom  

**Feat:** [Magic Initiate](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024/feats#MagicInitiate) (one of the few things you actually finished learning — pick Wizard, Cleric, or Druid based on who taught you)  

**Skill Proficiencies:** [Arcana](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/free-rules/playing-the-game#Skills), [Insight](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/free-rules/playing-the-game#Skills)  

**Tool Proficiency:** [Herbalism Kit](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/477-herbalism-kit) (component prep, the bottom rung of every curriculum you ever sat in)  

**Equipment:** Choose **A** or **B:**  
- **(A)** [Quarterstaff](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/13-quarterstaff), [Herbalism Kit](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/477-herbalism-kit), a half-finished [Spellbook](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/536-spellbook) or [Holy Symbol](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/478-holy-symbol) (the one item your old teacher let you keep), [Ink](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/482-ink-bottle), [Traveler's Clothes](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/541-travelers-clothes), [Scholar's Pack](https://www.dndbeyond.com/equipment/528-scholars-pack), and **12 GP**

## Optional personality prompts

- Who taught you? Name them. What did they look like the last time you saw them — angry, sad, relieved, or already turning away?
- What was the spell you were supposed to cast, and what actually happened? You can lie to other characters about this. You cannot lie to yourself.
- You still know one warning your teacher gave you that you have never told anyone. What is it, and who would you tell first if you broke?

## Weave Strain (if you gain magic later)

You start play knowing exactly what Weave Strain is. The mechanic is not new information. Your **Magic Initiate** cantrip is usually free; the 1/day leveled spell from the feat causes strain like any other leveled cast. If you take a full caster class on level-up, all normal rules apply — *and* the following:

- On strain **1 or 2**, when you cast a leveled spell, the DM may **require** your strain cost to be **ill luck**, a **memory** option, or a **black-craft sign** — not raw damage. You were warned. You knew the price options before you ever spent a slot, and the channel knows you knew. This restriction lifts once you earn **Purification I**.
- Your **burnout** save (Constitution save at strain 3) is **DC +2** higher than standard. You cast knowing what could happen. The seal does not grade on the curve.
- Optional roleplay: the first time per session you cast a leveled spell in front of an NPC who knew you as "the one who couldn't finish," the DM may give you **Heroic Inspiration** — see the [DM Operating Manual §3](../00-dm-operating-manual.md). The cost of casting includes everyone watching you finally do it.
