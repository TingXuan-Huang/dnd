# Multiclassing

When you gain a level, you may take it in a new class instead of advancing your current class. **Total character level** (sum across classes) determines your [[xp-and-advancement|XP cost]] and [[proficiency-bonus]]. [BR-2024 §08-supplements#Multiclassing]

## Prerequisites

You need **13+** in the primary ability of your **current** class(es) **and** the **new** class. Example: a [[barbarian]] → [[druid]] multiclass requires Strength 13 and Wisdom 13. [BR-2024 §08-supplements#Multiclassing]

## Hit points and hit dice

- Use a class's "Level 1 HP" formula only when your **total** character level is 1.
- For each subsequent class level, roll (or take the fixed average for) that class's hit die. See [[hit-points]].
- Maintain hit-dice pools **separately** when die types differ (e.g., d8 from Cleric + d10 from Paladin).

[BR-2024 §08-supplements#Hit Points and Hit Dice]

## Proficiency Bonus

Based on **total character level**, regardless of class split. See [[xp-and-advancement]].

## Proficiencies you gain from multiclassing

When you take your **first** level in a new class, you gain **only** the proficiencies listed in that class's "Multiclassing" entry — NOT the full starting proficiencies you would have received at character creation. [BR-2024 §08-supplements#Proficiencies Gained]

## Stacking rules

| Feature type | Rule |
| --- | --- |
| Armor Class | Use only one calculation (e.g., Monk Unarmored Defense OR Sorcerer Draconic Resilience — not both) |
| Extra Attack | Does not stack; max two attacks per Attack action unless a feature says otherwise |
| [[spellcasting]] | See [[multiclass-spellcasting]] (combined caster levels, separate prepared lists) |

[BR-2024 §08-supplements#Stacking Rules]
