# Module 1 — Checks Catalog

Every roll the DM may call for in Module 1, in one place. Two columns matter most:

- **OPEN** — player rolls, knows the result. Use for gates, combat-adjacent skill use, and any time success/failure changes what happens visibly.
- **SILENT** — DM rolls behind the screen using passive scores or a hidden d20. Use for atmosphere, dread, hidden information. On a fail, narrate **nothing** — the player simply doesn't notice. No anticlimax.

**Design rule of thumb:** every important clue should be reachable by **at least two different checks** (e.g., Investigation OR History, Perception OR Insight). If only one path exists, that path becomes a gate — gates are for permission scenes, not clue scenes.

**Fail-forward principle:** No check kills momentum. A failure either (a) costs a resource, (b) advances the Awakening Clock, (c) gives partial info, or (d) reveals the same info through a worse path (an NPC tells them later, embarrassed).

## Open checks — detailed

### Act I

**Insight DC 12 · Mordren's pitch · Bent Plow Inn**
- **Trigger:** A PC asks "is he telling the truth?" or studies him while he speaks.
- **Success:** "He is telling part of the truth. He believes what he says, but he is holding more back." (Players know he's hiding something but not what.)
- **Fail:** "He seems sincere." (True, just incomplete.) No clock advance.
- **Fail-forward:** Bessa drops a hint later if asked (DC 12 Persuasion).

**Persuasion DC 12 · Bessa for Mordren rumor**
- **Trigger:** PC asks Bessa what she knows about the wizard.
- **Success:** She mentions the "House of First Light" sleep-mutter. +1 clue (write on player log).
- **Fail:** "He's just a quiet customer. Pays his coin." Try again next visit at DC 14.
- **Fail-forward:** Spend 4 cp on two rounds for the farmers — they spill the same rumor for free.

**Persuasion DC 13 · Elder Bran · waive the village bond**
- **Trigger:** Party tries to descend without paying.
- **Success:** Bond waived. Village gives 2 torches, 50 ft rope, 1 small loaf. +Advantage on one future Fallowton social check.
- **Fail:** Pay 10 gp total OR leave one useful item as collateral. No clock advance.
- **Fail-forward:** Henric will pay half the bond in exchange for a share of any gold (he is right there).

**Intimidation DC 15 · Elder Bran · force passage**
- **Trigger:** PC threatens or publicly humiliates Bran.
- **Success:** Bran lets them pass, but village trust drops. Disadvantage on all future Fallowton social rolls until the party brings home something visibly heroic.
- **Fail:** Two villagers physically bar the sinkhole stairs. Party must persuade, pay, or wait — no descent tonight.
- **Cost reminder:** Intimidating an elder in his own village is a real social cost. Make the room go quiet.

**History DC 14 · cite Dawnweave to Bran**
- **Trigger:** PC tries to convince Bran that the descent is necessary because of what the ruin <em>is</em>.
- **Success:** Bran sits down. Bond waived without question. He says, very quietly: "Tell me you understand what you are saying." This is also the trigger that lets him reveal his grandfather Wendel's letter (if a Voss dagger has surfaced).
- **Fail:** "Books in a city. Words for scholars. My fields are real."

**Religion DC 12 · catch Sister Elra's prayer phrase**
- **Trigger:** PC listens while she chants at the shrine.
- **Success:** One phrase translates: "Keeper of the first light, do not wake." Direct connection to Mordren's sleep-mutter.
- **Fail:** Comforting cadence, words unknown. Try Arcana DC 15 to recognize it as not any modern liturgical language.
- **Fail-forward:** Comprehend Languages spell or a PC with Old Common automatic.

**Persuasion DC 13 · request a blessing from Sister Elra**
- **Trigger:** Player asks her to bless a torch, weapon, or flask.
- **Success:** She blesses it. The bearer has advantage on the first save vs. fear or charm in the ruin.
- **Refusal context:** She will not bless a leveled-spell focus (staff, holy symbol used as arcane focus). She will bless a torch even if asked rudely — torches are for the dark, and the dark is the dark.

**History DC — (free) · Tamsin Bellows IDs the sinkhole stone**
- **Trigger:** A PC brings her a chip or rubbing of the pale stone.
- **Success:** Automatic. She volunteers: "Not from here. Not within fifty miles. I'd say west — past the forest. Maybe the old crown country."
- **Why free:** Tamsin's whole identity is shaped metal. Rolling for this would be like rolling for the smith to recognize iron.

**Persuasion DC 12 · Tamsin · haggle on sharpening**
- **Trigger:** A PC tries to lower her 5 sp / 1 gp pricing.
- **Success:** 4 sp / 8 sp.
- **Fail:** She raises the price by 1 sp out of irritation.

**Investigation DC 12 · Grain Store · the tooth marks**
- **Trigger:** PC examines a chewed sack.
- **Success:** Three-toed, clawed teeth on the burlap. Goblin-sized. Cross-reference to Tunnel 2 mouth: advantage on first social roll vs. captive goblins in Module 2.
- **Fail:** "Rats, maybe. Big ones." Lose the cross-reference bonus.

**Persuasion DC 11 · Grain Store keeper opens up**
- **Trigger:** PC convinces him to show the back of the storage.
- **Success:** He shows them the chewed sack and the broken board where something got in.
- **Fail:** "Nothing to see. Just grain. Move along."

**Insight DC 10 · Garret is telling the truth (mill)**
- **Trigger:** PC studies Garret as he describes the blue light.
- **Success:** "He's not lying. He's not drunk. He's terrified." Note: this is intentionally easy. Garret needs to be believed.
- **Fail:** Even on a 1, narrate doubt but not certainty of lie. He is too earnest to read as deception.

**Arcana DC 13 · Garret's flour drawing**
- **Trigger:** Garret traces a shape on the mill floor in flour.
- **Success:** Recognized as a Dawnweave invocation seal — same family of symbols as the sinkhole carvings. Advantage on next Arcana roll in the ruin.
- **Fail:** "A pattern. Maybe meaningful. You'd want a scholar." Mordren can confirm if shown.

**Persuasion DC 14 · invite to Mordren's cottage**
- **Trigger:** Polite, respectful approach. Best in private.
- **Success:** He shows them his cottage himself. The sealed letter is on the desk but he does not open it; if asked, he says "later, perhaps." No clock advance.
- **Fail:** "I do not entertain. Forgive me."

**Stealth DC 14 (group) · break into Mordren's cottage**
- **Trigger:** Party decides to sneak in.
- **Success:** They get inside. They can read titles, see candle smoke, find the sealed letter.
- **Reading the sealed letter:** Investigation DC 13 to peel without breaking → they see only the salutation: "To the bearer of the Recovery." Breaking the seal entirely → **Mordren knows** (clock +1, and he will confront them at the inn the next time they meet).
- **Fail:** Mordren returns mid-burglary. The scene becomes a standoff, not a fight. He demands an explanation. If they have any cover story at DC 13 Deception, he pretends to accept it — and the party loses his trust silently (Insight 14 to notice).

### Act II — Descent

**Athletics DC 10 · wet stone stairs**
- **Trigger:** Descent.
- **Success:** Keep footing.
- **Fail:** 1d4 bludgeoning, drop one held item one step (recoverable). No clock advance.
- **Fail-forward:** A second PC can grab them with a Reaction (Dex 12 free check).

### Act III — The Sampling

**Investigation DC 12 · Blue mouth · the broken dagger**
- **Trigger:** PC examines the dagger half-buried in dust.
- **Success:** Reads the initials — H.V. or W.V. (DM picks). History DC 12 piggyback (free if Investigation passed): "Local make, 60–100 years old. Not Dawnweave."
- **Fail:** "A dagger. Old. Broken." Initials not seen. Henric does not connect.
- **Fail-forward:** Showing the dagger to Henric will still trigger his reaction even without the initials read.

**Perception DC 11 · Sound mouth · the tracks**
- **Trigger:** PC looks at the floor before the mouth.
- **Success:** Three-toed clawed prints, several days of two-way traffic. Cross-reference to grain-store cue.
- **Fail:** "Dust. Some scuff marks."

**Arcana or Nature DC 13 · Wind mouth · the heartbeat**
- **Trigger:** PC studies the wind.
- **Success:** "This is not weather. It pulses at the rhythm of a sleeping heartbeat. Something is breathing slowly, far below."
- **Fail:** "Cold air. Strong draft. Strange but not unnatural."

**Group Constitution save DC 10 · climb out under the ruin's eye (after Pool Shudder)**
- **Trigger:** Pool Shudder fires, party retreats.
- **Success (each):** Climb out cleanly.
- **Fail (each):** Reach the surface trembling. Disadvantage on the first Charisma roll back in the village (their hands shake; villagers see it).
- **No HP damage.** This is mood, not danger.

## Silent checks — detailed

Silent checks are **DM rolls or passive comparisons**. Players do not know one was made. On success, narrate the detail to the highest-passive PC only. On fail, narrate nothing.

### Why silent?

1. **Atmosphere without anticlimax.** A failed Perception roll the player knows about ruins dread. A silently-failed one just leaves the dread.
2. **Reveals character.** High-Wisdom PCs naturally sense the world's breathing. Low-Wisdom miss it. Both feel correct.
3. **Lets you seed clues at the right pace.** If you want them to catch something three scenes from now, hold the clue silent here.

### Act I silent rolls

**Passive Perception 13 · Mordren's ring flicker · Bent Plow**
- **When:** Three farmers say "cave" loud enough across the room.
- **Reveal (highest passive PC, quiet aside):** "His ring on the third finger flickers — blue, very quick. He notices you saw, but does not look at you."
- **Fail:** No mention. The detail is gone.

**Insight 13 · Bessa is scared**
- **When:** Bessa makes a joke that lands flat.
- **Reveal (highest Insight bonus):** "Her humor is armor."
- **Fail:** Nothing.

**Insight 12 · Bran is afraid (not angry)**
- **When:** Bran refuses the first request.
- **Reveal (highest Insight):** "Underneath the refusal — he is frightened. Not of you. Of being the one who said yes."
- **Fail:** He reads as stubborn.

**History 14 · Sister Elra's chant is Dawnweave**
- **When:** Elra finishes a prayer.
- **Reveal (highest History — Arcana is acceptable substitute):** "The rhythm of her chant matches the inscription rhythm on the sinkhole steps. She is praying in their language."
- **Fail:** Nothing. (This can fire later when the player rereads the inscription. Hold it.)

**Insight 14 · Tamsin's grief**
- **When:** Tamsin mentions "five winters ago."
- **Reveal:** "Her husband died last winter. The five-winters mark was when something happened — when he stopped being whoever he was."
- **Fail:** Nothing.

**Perception 12 · Grain-store · faint goblin smell**
- **When:** Examining the chewed sack.
- **Reveal:** "Under the burlap, a smell — sweet rot and iron."
- **Fail:** Nothing.

**Perception 12 · Mill stone is warm**
- **When:** A PC stands near the mill stone for more than a few seconds.
- **Reveal:** "The stone, even from a few feet away, is warmer than the air. Not hot — warm. Like a person's chest."
- **Fail:** Nothing.
- **If they touch it:** Wisdom save DC 12 (still silent, DM rolls vs. their passive Wisdom). On fail, they linger longer than they meant to. The urge passes.

**Investigation 13 · Mordren's royal seal (in his cottage)**
- **When:** PC sees the sealed letter on the desk.
- **Reveal:** "The wax bears a sigil — three nested rings, a key through them. You have not seen this seal before, but it does not look like any merchant or temple insignia."
- **Fail:** "A sealed letter. Wax. Unfamiliar mark."

**Insight 14 · Mordren's relief at "wait"**
- **When:** Party tells Mordren they want to wait until dawn.
- **Reveal:** "Something in his face softens. He covers it quickly. He <em>wanted</em> you to wait."
- **Fail:** "He nods. 'As you wish.'"

### Act II silent rolls

**Wisdom 12 · the ruin notices**
- **When:** Halfway down the stairs.
- **Reveal (highest Wisdom):** "The stones at your back feel like they are turning their faces toward you. They are not. But the feeling is real."
- **Fail:** Nothing.

### Act III silent rolls

**Wisdom 11 · Blue mote feeling**
- **When:** PC stands at Blue mouth.
- **Reveal:** "Not malice. Vigilance. They are watching for something specific, and you are not it — yet."
- **Fail:** Motes feel cold and indifferent.

**Insight 13 · the laughing thing**
- **When:** PC hears the imitation laugh from Sound mouth.
- **Reveal:** "That is not a child. Something has been listening to children, and learned the shape."
- **Fail:** "A child's laugh. Echoing strangely."

**Wisdom 12 · the wind knows your name**
- **When:** PC stands at Wind mouth.
- **Reveal (to one PC only — the highest Wisdom):** "The wind passes your face and seems, for half a heartbeat, to know your name. Then it lets go."
- **Fail:** "Strong cold draft." No mechanical effect either way.

### Act IV silent rolls

**Wisdom 12 · which ending fits**
- **When:** End of session, before the cliffhanger lands.
- **This is the DM's roll only.** Use it to break ties if two endings could fire — let the dice pick. Or override the dice and pick the one that hurts most.

## Awakening Clock — when it advances

The clock starts at **0 / 6**. Hidden from players. Advance silently.

| Trigger | Stage advance |
|---|---|
| Party visits Mordren's cottage and breaks the royal seal | +1 |
| Party casts a leveled spell anywhere (village or ruin) | +1 each cast |
| Option C fires (refuse to descend) | +1 |
| Each tunnel mouth peered into | +1 each |
| PC touches a Blue mote with bare skin | +1 |
| PC shouts in the Sound tunnel mouth | +1 |
| PC speaks their own name aloud in the Wind tunnel mouth | +2 |
| Party insists on entering a tunnel past the mouth | +2 |
| Any blood spilled in the ruin | +1 per 10 HP |

### Stage effects (Module 1)

| Stage | What players experience |
|---|---|
| 0 | Nothing. The ruin sleeps. |
| 1 | Dust shifts on its own. A torch flutters with no wind. |
| 2 | The carvings flicker blue, briefly, in the corner of someone's eye. |
| 3 | **Warning.** One PC: Wisdom save DC 11 or **Frightened** until end of next turn (whispered name from the wall). |
| 4 | **Defending.** Stage-4 floors creak before the party steps on them. The ruin warns them away. |
| 5 | **Calling.** Surface manifestation: a Fallowton bell rings once with no hand. Mordren feels it from miles away if alive. |
| 6 | **Waking.** **End the session here, even if the script isn't done.** The pool fills an inch with black water. Force them out. |

**Module 1 should end no higher than stage 3.** If it's climbing toward 4–5 by Act III, slow down — describe the Pool Shudder earlier, have Mordren bark "Out, now," and pull the party.

## DC ladder reference (level 1)

| DC | Difficulty | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Trivial | Almost never roll — just succeed |
| 10 | Easy | Most stress-free checks; in-class skill |
| 12 | Moderate | The default for "this is interesting" |
| 13 | Tested | Cross-skill use; out-of-class but possible |
| 14 | Hard | A skilled PC's signature attempt |
| 15 | Very hard | Whole party's specialist on a stretch |
| 17 | Heroic | Only if failure is also exciting |
| 20 | Legendary | Do not gate Module 1 content behind this |

**Level-1 reminder:** A level-1 PC with proficiency + +3 ability bonus rolls 1d20+5. Average roll is 15.5. DC 13 succeeds ~60% of the time for a proficient PC. DC 15 succeeds ~50%. **If a check fails too often at your table, lower it by 1 — players remember being told "you don't notice" three times in a session as railroading, even when it isn't.**

## DM principles for this module

1. **Roll fewer dice than you think.** When in doubt, hand the clue to the player who tried.
2. **Silent failures don't get narrated.** That is the entire point. Resist the urge to say "you don't see anything special" — it's a tell.
3. **The Awakening Clock is the only timer that matters.** Day-of-week tracking, food, water — set aside until Module 2. Today is one day.
4. **Every NPC remembers.** Note who the party ignored, who they bribed, who they were rude to. That memory becomes Module 4.
5. **Endings are flavors, not branches.** Whichever cliffhanger fires, Module 2 begins at sunrise with the descent. The flavor changes; the door does not.
