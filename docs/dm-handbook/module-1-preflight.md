# Module 1 — Pre-Flight Checklist

For first-time DMs running Session 1. Read morning-of. Print if you can.

---

- [ ] Read `module-1-play.html` once, front to back. **Read the Chinese paragraphs aloud at least once.** Stumbling at the table sounds worse than stumbling alone in your bedroom.
- [ ] Read `module-1-npcs.html`. For each NPC, **say one of their sample lines aloud in their voice.** Don't memorize — just rehearse the cadence.

  - **The H.V./W.V. dagger initials.** Pick now. (Recommended: W.V. for Wendel Voss — the grandfather connection is stronger.)

## 30 minutes before

- [ ] Sticky note 1: write `AWAKE 0 / 6` — keep face-down at your right hand.

## At the table — before any narration

Say this to the players, in your own words but hitting all three beats:

1. **The contract.** "You have one day in Fallowton before the village forces a decision about the sinkhole. Use it however you like — but the village will not wait for you forever."
2. **The signal.** "I will sometimes describe things you may or may not notice. If I describe something quietly only to one of you, that's because of a passive check. Don't ask why. Just play your character."
3. **The promise.** "Tonight is the setup. There will be no boss fight. There will be one moment when I tell you to leave the ruin — when that happens, I'm asking, not railroading. Trust me."

Then: ask each PC, around the table, **"Why are you in Fallowton?"** Use the d8 hook table on the npcs page if anyone hesitates. Spend no more than 60 seconds per PC. This warms the table.

---

## Voice warm-up · 3 minutes

Before you start narrating, run these three in your head or under your breath:

1. **Mordren** — soft, paused, polite. Try the line: *"Touch nothing you do not understand. And if you find an altar — do not pray at it."* Pause before "altar." Hold the pause.
2. **Bran** — slow, weight forward. Try: *"You want to walk under our fields, disturb our dead earth, and call it courage?"* Land hard on "courage." Let the room go quiet after.
3. **Sister Elra** — quiet, patient. Try: *"Keeper of the first light, do not wake."* Whisper this one. Almost too quiet to hear. That's the voice.

If you can hold three distinct voices, you can hold seven. The other four (Bessa, Henric, Garret, Tamsin) live in the same register as you, just shifted — Bessa quicker, Henric slower, Garret tighter, Tamsin flatter.

## The first five minutes — verbatim opening

Read aloud:

> 清晨，雨终于停了。
>
> The Bent Plow Inn 的窗户结着薄薄一层水汽，木门半开着，让一缕灰白色的晨光斜斜地落在地板上。屋里有炉火残留的烟味、湿羊毛味，还有 Bessa Thorn 正在揉面的声音。

Pause. Look at the players. Then continue:

> 角落里坐着三个农夫，已经在低声争吵。

Voice the farmers (use three slightly different pitches, doesn't have to be perfect):

> "那个洞自己会闭上的。" — *Theo, hopeful*
>
> "它会闭上的吗？我可没听见它闭上。" — *Jord, skeptical*
>
> "老 Henric 的麦田全毁了——他这把年纪还能再种一季吗？" — *Old Marn, low*

Then narrate Mordren entering frame:

> 窗边那张桌子边，独自坐着一个穿着黑色旅袍的男人。他的手指上戴着几枚银戒，面前摆着一盏不曾点燃的 pale wood staff。他听见你们进门的脚步，抬起头——眼神平静，却让屋里其他几张脸不约而同地转开。

Now Mordren speaks. **In his voice, not yours:**

> "早安。我叫 Mordren Vale。坐下吧——如果你们今天有空，我有一份工作给你们。"

**Stop.** Look at the players. Wait. Let them speak first. The session has begun.

---

## Three emergency moves — if you forget everything else

1. **If you don't know what to do next:** Ring the Sunset Bell. Bran is standing under it. He demands a decision. The session moves forward.
2. **If a player does something you didn't plan:** Ask "what does that look like?" and let them describe it. You don't have to know — they don't either.
3. **If a roll feels wrong:** Don't ask for it. Just hand them the information they were trying for. Failed checks are for stakes, not for clue-gating.

---

## Common opening-session pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why it happens | What to do instead |
|---|---|---|
| You over-explain Mordren's offer | New DMs want to be sure players "get it" | Give the offer once, in his voice. Move on. If they didn't catch it, Bessa will explain over a beer. |
| You read every read-aloud paragraph word-for-word | The text feels like a contract | Read the *italicized* / parchment blocks word-for-word. Paraphrase the rest. The voice is yours, the atmosphere is theirs. |
| You let one player monopolize the inn | The talker is having fun | After 3 minutes on one PC, turn directly to another: "[PC2's name], while [PC1] is talking to Mordren — what are you doing?" |
| You announce silent checks | Excitement about the trick | If you've said "passive perception" out loud, you've broken the device. Just whisper or pass a folded note to the high-Wisdom PC. |
| You forget the Awakening Clock | It's behind the screen | Glance at the sticky every scene transition. Tick if any trigger fired. It does not have to be precise — it has to feel inevitable. |
| You let combat happen in the village | A player wants to draw a sword | "You can. But Bessa will throw you out, and Tamsin will refuse to sharpen your blade for the rest of the campaign. Are you sure?" 95% of the time, they're not sure. |
| You give all three cliffhangers | Wanting players to feel like all their choices mattered | **Pick one.** Players sense the difference between a single hard truth and three soft hooks. The single hard truth lands. |

## Mid-session pulse check (look at the clock at Sunset Bell)

- If table-time is **under 90 min**: take a 10-minute break before descent. Players are fresh; ride the energy.
- If table-time is **90–120 min**: brief stretch, water, then descent. You're on rails.
- If table-time is **over 120 min**: skip directly to descent. The free-explore was generous; don't milk it.

---

## Ending the session

You will *want* to keep going after the cliffhanger lands. Don't.

The cliffhanger is the session. After you deliver the ending image, say:

> "We end there."

Then close the laptop / put the screen down. Players will protest. That's correct. Protest means the next session has a built-in audience.

Take five minutes to fill the log at the bottom of `module-1-dm-flow.md` while it's fresh. You will thank yourself in two weeks.

---

## After the players leave

Three questions to ask yourself:

1. **Who at the table was the quietest?** Plan a moment in Module 2 just for them — an NPC who asks specifically for their character.
2. **Which clue did the party miss that I was excited about?** Put it on a different NPC's lips in Module 2. Clues are not consumed by going unfound.
3. **Which moment, in your gut, did the table go still?** That is the tone of this campaign. Lean into it.

Good luck.
