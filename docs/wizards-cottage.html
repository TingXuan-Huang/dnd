The Wizard's Cottage

Rented · currently occupied by Mordren Vale · approach with caution

Floor Plan — The Wizard's Cottage · The arcane circle is fresh

The door is unlocked. Inside, the air is wrong — it smells like ash, old paper, and something metallic that has no name. Books are stacked against two walls with no apparent order. In the center of the floor, drawn in what looks like chalk and silver dust, is a large circle with rings inside it. The lines are precise. Whatever it is, it was not done carelessly.

Mordren Vale

Mordren Vale
Wizard · ruin scholar · arrived three days after the collapse · Age: apparent 40s, actual unknown
Polite and correct in all things. Never raises his voice. Has an answer ready for every suspicious question and the answers are always just plausible enough. Claims to be documenting pre-Dawnweave ruins. Pays rent in advance. Speaks to no one casually.

What Is Here

  • Arcane circle (center floor): a Sending circle, recently used. It points roughly north-northeast — toward the cave.
  • Bookshelves: texts on ruin architecture, several in languages that predate the current calendar, one book with no cover whose pages feel warm.
  • Writing desk: contains correspondence in cipher, blank parchment, silver ink, and a strip of black cloth used as a bookmark.
  • Locked chest (DC 18 to pick): contains a brass cylinder sealed with dark wax, a folded map with the pyramid labeled "The House of First Light," and two vials of something that smells like burnt copper.
  • Under the bed: three spent candles arranged in a triangle, a smear of ash, and a single pale gray stone chip — same material as the buried structure.
  • Windows: all sealed from inside with strips of blackened cloth. Light does not enter.

DM Only

Mordren Vale is not lying about being a ruin scholar — that is exactly what he is. He has studied the Dawnweave for thirty years and suspects the buried structure is not a temple but a Converter. He does not know the full truth. The sealed brass cylinder contains royal-backed research papers and a coded warrant from an office that tracks dangerous ruins and magical disturbances; breaking the seal too early hints at his government connection without explaining it. Mordren came to Fallowton after the sinkhole was confirmed, but his papers show he had been watching reports of strange resonance in the region before then. If confronted with evidence, he negotiates rather than fights. His goal is knowledge and control of the investigation, not random evil.
