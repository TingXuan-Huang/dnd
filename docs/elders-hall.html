Elder's Hall

Seat of village authority · Council chamber of Fallowton

Ground Floor Plan — Elder's Hall

The hall is cold even in summer. Stone floor, bare rafters, a long table of dark oak worn smooth by generations of elbows. Shields hang above the weapons rack — old ones, unfamiliar heraldry. The Elder's chair at the north end sits on a raised platform, and behind it, a map of Fallowton's farmland has been pinned flat. Most of the land parcel names have been crossed out and rewritten.

Who's Here

Elder Bran Orwick
Village Elder · appointed for life · 60s, grey beard, careful hands
Bran has governed Fallowton for twenty-two years by being slow to act and hard to surprise. He is not slow now. He wants the cave sealed, the wizard gone, and outsiders watched closely. He is afraid, and he knows that everyone can tell, and that makes him angrier.
Councilwoman Hilde Voss
Farmer-representative · practical, blunt
Henric Voss's daughter. She was the one who pulled her father away from the sinkhole that first morning. She thinks the Elder is stalling and says so, loudly, at every meeting.

Notable Details

  • A hand-drawn map of the north barley field is pinned to the east wall — the sinkhole location is marked with a red X.
  • The land ledger on the west shelf shows that Fallowton's grain surplus has been declining for three years.
  • A village bond agreement, half-written, sits on the table — Orwick is demanding strangers pay it before they may enter the cave.
  • The old shields on the east wall are unidentifiable — no kingdom currently uses those sigils.
  • A locked iron strongbox under the Elder's chair contains the village's coin reserve: 38 gold pieces.

DM Only

Elder Orwick knows more than he says. His grandfather's diary — kept behind the records shelf — references "the pale stair" and "the light that should not be disturbed." He inherited this knowledge and buried it. The village bond he demands is 10 gp total for the party, or useful collateral, framed as compensation for risk to Henric's field and village labor. If the party wins his trust (Persuasion DC 13, or by promising to protect Fallowton and return village goods), he waives the bond and may quietly share the diary. The diary mentions "First Light" and "what sleeps between the steps."
