# Fallowton House Rules

> This page is for **you, the player.** It is the short list of campaign rules that sit *next to* BR-2024 — what we tweak, what we add, and what stays untouched. If a rule is not on this page or in [`docs/campaign/weave-strain-magic.md`](../docs/campaign/weave-strain-magic.md), assume BR-2024 RAW. Tape this to the inside of your character folder; pair it with [`docs/player-guide/quick-reference-combat-skills.md`](../docs/player-guide/quick-reference-combat-skills.md).

**Two other rule documents you already have:**

- **Magic** — [`docs/campaign/weave-strain-magic.md`](../docs/campaign/weave-strain-magic.md). The Weave Strain system. Read it if you cast.
- **Combat & skills cheat sheet** — [`docs/player-guide/quick-reference-combat-skills.md`](../docs/player-guide/quick-reference-combat-skills.md). One-page BR-2024 reference.

## Section 1 · Already locked in the DM Manual

These house rules are spelled out in detail in [`homebrew/00-dm-operating-manual.md`](00-dm-operating-manual.md). Linked here so you know they're in play. Click through for full text — they're not restated.

- **Heroic Inspiration economy** — See [§3](00-dm-operating-manual.md#3--when-to-roll). You earn Heroic Inspiration for (1) playing in-character even when it's mechanically suboptimal, (2) handing the spotlight to another player, (3) showing up prepared (notes, art, music). **Max 1 award per player per session.** Spend it to **re-roll any d20 before the result is read — you take the SECOND roll, not the better one.** [ON BY DEFAULT]
- **Nat 20 / Nat 1 on skill checks** — See [§7](00-dm-operating-manual.md#7--ruling-on-the-fly). Unlike RAW, a natural 20 on a non-attack d20 **succeeds and grants one extra benefit** (free info, advantage on the next related check, a small gift). A natural 1 **fails and adds one complication** (a tool breaks, an NPC notices, time slips). Story events, not math events. [ON BY DEFAULT]
- **Creative skill + ability combos** — See [§3](00-dm-operating-manual.md#3--when-to-roll). You can ask for **Strength (Intimidation)**, **Charisma (Athletics)**, **Wisdom (Performance)**, etc. If the pairing makes fiction sense, the DM says yes. You bring the skill proficiency; the DM picks the ability that fits the moment. [ON BY DEFAULT]
- **Help action — physical proximity required** — See [§7](00-dm-operating-manual.md#7--ruling-on-the-fly). To grant advantage via Help, you must be **within 5 ft** of the ally OR be able to **physically participate** in the task. Shouted advice from across the room does not grant advantage (the DM may award a flat +1 or +2 for genuinely useful info). [ON BY DEFAULT]

## Section 2 · New rules introduced by this campaign

Each rule below is a deliberate tweak to BR-2024. The default is [ON]; ask the DM if you want any one of them off for a specific scene.

- **Critical hits — max + roll** [ON BY DEFAULT]. When you land a critical hit, **take the maximum value of your damage dice, then roll those dice again and add the result.** A longsword crit (1d8+3) becomes `8 + 1d8 + 3` instead of `2d8 + 3`. This makes crits feel like crits — a guaranteed floor with a swingy ceiling — and removes the "I crit but rolled two 1s" deflation. Sneak Attack dice, smite dice, and any other dice tied to the attack follow the same rule (max one set, roll one set). Monsters crit the same way against you; the dice are sacred to both sides.

- **Death saves — only the DM sees the roll** [ON BY DEFAULT]. When your character is dying, you do not roll death saves yourself. The DM rolls them in secret. Instead of watching the math, you **describe what your character feels** each round — *cold creeping up your fingers, a voice you can't quite hear, the warmth of someone holding your hand, the room narrowing.* The DM will tell you only when you **stabilize**, when you **wake up**, or when you have **died**. You'll still know roughly how bad it is from how the DM narrates. The point is fear and presence, not arithmetic.

- **Exhaustion — RAW (cumulative 1–6)** [ON BY DEFAULT]. We use BR-2024 exhaustion exactly as written: levels stack 1–6, each level imposes **−2 on every d20 test** and **−5 ft Speed**, you **die at level 6**, and a long rest removes 1 level. See [`wiki/conditions/exhaustion.md`](../wiki/conditions/exhaustion.md). Forced marches, missed long rests, dehydration, and certain spells/effects can give you levels; the DM will name the trigger when it happens.

- **Rest schedule — standard with teeth** [ON BY DEFAULT]. Short rest = **1 hour**; long rest = **8 hours**. Same as RAW. **But:** in confirmed-dangerous areas (the Converter ruin, an active dungeon, anywhere a clock is ticking), a long rest can be **interrupted by the world**, and the Awakening Clock keeps ticking through your rest. You won't always get the full benefit just because you closed your eyes. If you want a guaranteed safe long rest, get yourselves somewhere safe first.

- **Encumbrance & weight — four-tier load system** [ON BY DEFAULT]. We **do** track weight, using a four-tier carrying-capacity system based on your Strength score. See [`physicality.md`](physicality.md) for the full system. Quick rule: **Light Load** (≤ STR×5 lb) no penalty; **Standard Load** (to STR×10) −1 initiative; **Heavy Load** (to STR×15) −10 ft speed + disadvantage on DEX; **Overload** (to STR×20) heavy penalties + no reactions. Hard cap at STR × 20 lb. Also covers weapon-weight tiers and armor drawbacks (heavy armor: −5 ft baseline, half HP on long rest if you sleep in it, optional heat-exhaustion check in summer).

- **Survival realism — the body keeps score** [ON BY DEFAULT]. We track hunger, thirst, sleep, disease, falling, and **drug/magic/save tolerance** (your body adapts to repeated exposures). See [`survival-realism.md`](survival-realism.md). Key takeaway: healing potions get less effective with repeated use in a 24-hour window; magic buff stacks adapt; the body remembers.

- **Sanity & spirit — the mind doesn't stay in the head** [ON BY DEFAULT]. We track PTSD/Trauma triggers (with mechanical effects you pick from when triggered) and **mind-body bleed** (your mental state shapes your physical stats — Trauma raises your Concentration DC; Despair counts as 1 level of exhaustion until addressed; Joy gives +1 HP per long rest for 3 days). See [`sanity-and-spirit.md`](sanity-and-spirit.md).

- **The Notice — the campaign's central tracker** [ON BY DEFAULT]. **One unified tracker** (replaces the old Awakening Clock + Hollow Star Corruption Tracker). Personal Notice (your PC, 0-10) and World Notice (the region, 0-10) feed each other in a feedback loop. At Personal Notice 3+ you unlock the Forbidden Choice (consult the Hollow Star for info, +1 Notice cost). At Personal Notice 7+ revival from death = automatic Permanent Vision. At 10 your PC becomes an NPC. **This is the campaign's central horror mechanic.** Read [`the-notice.md`](the-notice.md) before play.

- **Healing potions — bonus action to drink yourself** [ON BY DEFAULT]. Drinking a healing potion **on yourself is a bonus action.** Administering a potion **to another creature is a full action** (you have to find their mouth, tilt the head, pour without spilling). RAW makes both an action; we speed this up because a downed ally and a held potion shouldn't cost you your entire turn. You still roll the potion's healing dice normally.

- **Reaction sharing — declare in advance** [ON BY DEFAULT]. You can state your reaction intent **before** initiative comes back to you. *"I'll use my reaction to Counterspell if anyone casts an attack spell at us"* or *"If anything moves out of my reach, I'll opportunity-attack it."* The DM tracks whether your trigger fires and will tell you when it does. You can change your declared reaction at the start of any other player's turn (before their action resolves). If your reaction fires, you've used it for the round; if it never fires, you still have it.

- **Weapon swap — generous object interaction** [ON BY DEFAULT]. **Dropping** a weapon is always free. **Drawing OR sheathing one weapon** per turn is free (your one object interaction). **Swapping** weapons (drop one, draw another) is also free — the drop is free and the draw uses your object interaction. **Drawing two weapons in one turn** costs your object interaction (first weapon) plus your action OR a feature (second weapon). Two-weapon-fighting builds: drawing both at once happens naturally on the turn you start the fight; subsequent draws follow the rule above.

## Section 3 · Everything else is RAW

If it isn't on this page and isn't in `weave-strain-magic.md`, **we use the 2024 Basic Rules as written.** That means:

- **Action economy** (1 action, 1 bonus action *if a feature grants one*, 1 reaction per round, movement up to Speed split however you like) — RAW.
- **Advantage / disadvantage** — RAW. Sources do **not** stack; any advantage + any disadvantage cancels to a flat d20.
- **AC, saving throws, attack rolls, spell save DC** — RAW. See [`docs/player-guide/quick-reference-combat-skills.md`](../docs/player-guide/quick-reference-combat-skills.md).
- **The 18 skills** and their default abilities — RAW. See [`wiki/`](../wiki/) for any specific skill. (The creative skill+ability combo rule above adds to this — it does not replace it.)
- **Conditions** (Blinded, Charmed, Frightened, Grappled, Poisoned, Prone, Restrained, Stunned, Unconscious, etc.) — RAW. See [`wiki/conditions/`](../wiki/conditions/).
- **Spell rules** (slots, components, ritual casting, concentration, upcasting) — RAW. *Weave Strain is layered on top — it does not change how spells work; it changes what casting them costs you.*
- **Levelling up, multiclassing, feats** — RAW.
- **Initiative** (d20 + Dex mod once per fight) — RAW.

If you're unsure: assume RAW, ask the DM, and they'll either confirm or point you back to this page.
