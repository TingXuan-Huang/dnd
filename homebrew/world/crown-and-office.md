# The Crown and the Office of Sealed Practice

The political machine that runs the province Fallowton sits in. Most villagers will never see the king. They will see the Office. Once a year, in Stillmonth, with sealed warrants and four horses.

> See also: [[weave-strain-magic]] §"Magic law and the crown" — the doctrine the Office enforces. [[calendar]] §holidays — the Crowning (Sownmonth 14) and the Office's Walk (Stillmonth 1). [[pantheon]] (unbuilt) — the official faith the crown patronizes.

## The Crown

**Kingdom:** the **Sealed Realm** (formally; commoners just say *the crown* or *the realm*). **Dynasty:** **House Verant**, founded by the first sealed-age monarch crowned 247 years ago on Sownmonth 14, Year 1 — a date the calendar still observes. The reigning monarch is **King Edrik III Verant**, fifty-one, twelve years on the throne.

**Form:** **hereditary monarchy** with seven regional **dukes** who govern by writ of the crown. Fallowton sits in the **Duchy of Hollowmarch**, ruled from the cathedral-town of **Carrowfen** about eight days' ride east. The duke is a Verant cousin; the title is older than the family.

**Official faith:** the **Concord of the Sealed Light** — the post-Sealing pantheon the crown patronizes and funds. Village clerics like Sister Elra answer to a bishop in Carrowfen. (Specific gods deferred to [[pantheon]] — leave dangling.)

**Stance on magic:** controlled. Restricted. Mistrusted by doctrine. The crown licenses individual casters by writ, charters a small number of approved colleges, and treats every other source — apprentice, hedge-witch, blood-touched child — as *unsealed practice*, a crime against public safety.

**The official line.** *"The realm protects its subjects from the harm of borrowed magic. Only the trained, examined, and sealed may draw on the broken Weave. This is mercy."*

**The actual pattern.** The licensed practitioners are, almost without exception, the children of nobles and the scholars nobles fund. The few commoners who pass examination do so under noble patronage and serve at noble pleasure. Magic in the Sealed Realm is legal — for the people who already had power before the law existed.

## The Office of Sealed Practice

**Official name:** the **Office of Sealed Practice for the Public Safety of the Realm.** Bureaucratic, deliberate. Nobody says the full name. Common usage: *the Office*, *Sealed Practice*, *the sealed coats*, or — among villagers who have seen one ride past — just *the four*.

**Founded:** Sownmonth 14, Year 1 of the Sealed Age — the same day House Verant was crowned. The Office is older than the kingdom's first tax law and older than its standing army. The founding writ is read aloud every Crowning.

### Hierarchy

| Rank | What they do | Where they sit |
|---|---|---|
| **High Sealkeeper** | Single position. Reports to the king directly. Sets doctrine. | Capital |
| **Provincial Wardens** (one per duchy) | Authorize warrants. Manage caseloads. Answer to the High Sealkeeper. | Duchy seat (Hollowmarch: Carrowfen) |
| **Circuit Captains** | Run a district of ~20 villages. Sign warrants for their squads. | Market towns |
| **Warrant Officers** (foot-squad lead) | Lead a four-person field squad. Carry the sealed warrant. | The road |
| **Arcane Sensitives, Enforcers** | The other three of the squad. | The road |
| **Clerks, Inspectors, Examiners** | The licensing-and-paperwork wing. Score apprentice exams. | Provincial offices |

A village sees the field squad and nobody else.

### The four-person squad

This is the unit Fallowton fears. Every squad is exactly four:

- **The Warrant Officer.** Lead. Carries the **sealed scroll** for that circuit — a wax-and-cord roll bearing the crown's blue-and-gold ribbon and the Office's mark (a closed eye over an open hand). Their word in the field is the warrant's word. They speak; the others act.
- **The Arcane Sensitive.** Robed, not armored. Trained at one of the licensed colleges. Carries a focus — usually a small **bone-and-silver pendulum** that twitches near recent casting. Their job is to *find*. They do not arrest; they point.
- **Two Enforcers.** Mailed. Crossbows and short swords. They restrain, they escort, they kill if the warrant authorizes it. The Office prefers them old — younger soldiers ask too many questions.

The four ride together, eat together, and sleep in shifts. They are reassigned every two years to prevent local entanglements. None of the four is required to be from the duchy they police. Usually none of them is.

### Warrant authority

**The sealed warrant lets them:**

- Enter any commoner dwelling on a circuit without further leave.
- Detain any person on suspicion of unsealed practice, up to ten days without charge.
- Compel a licensed cleric or wizard in the area to assist.
- Search and seize foci, components, written magic, and ritual implements.
- Execute on the spot for resisting arrest or for *unsealed casting in the act* (rare; usually escorted to Carrowfen).

**The sealed warrant does NOT let them:**

- Enter noble property without the lord's consent.
- Detain a licensed practitioner without their college's warden present.
- Take goods unrelated to the magical charge.
- Cross provincial lines (a Hollowmarch warrant dies at the duchy's edge).

The gap between the two lists is the heart of the politics.

### Visible tells

- **Coat.** Deep blue, gold piping at cuff and collar. A practical riding coat — the Office is not parade dress.
- **Badge.** A small enameled disc at the throat — closed eye over open hand.
- **Sealed scroll.** Carried at the warrant officer's belt in a cylindrical lacquered case. Visible. Heavy.
- **Pendulum.** The sensitive's focus, often worn outside the robe so commoners see it swing.
- **Crossbows, not bows.** The Office does not skirmish; it executes warrants. Crossbows shoot the unprepared.
- **Four horses, matched dun.** Issued from the same provincial stables. A village child can spot the Office from a half-mile by the colour of the horses.

### Perception

- **Nobility:** a tool. Useful for keeping commoners from accidentally setting their own fields on fire and for keeping rivals' bastards from learning to read seals. Nobody invites the Office to dinner, but the duke pays for their oats.
- **Licensed casters:** professional ally. The Office protects the licensing monopoly that protects their living. Most cooperate willingly when a sensitive comes asking. A few — the ones who remember unlicensed teachers fondly — comply slowly and lie when they safely can.
- **Commoners (city):** the law. Annoying, occasionally cruel, mostly someone else's problem.
- **Commoners (Fallowton and the like):** the four. The bag by the door. Children kept inside. A week of fear for an hour of riding.

### Relationship with clerics and licensed wizards

The official line is *collaboration*. Sister Elra holds a low-grade license to invoke the Concord — her healing is sealed practice and lawful. When the Office's Walk comes through, she is supposed to step forward and confirm no unsealed casting occurred in the village. She does. She also doesn't volunteer the things she hasn't been asked about.

Licensed wizards are colleagues — until a warrant names one. Then the warden of their college is summoned, and the matter moves into a different room with different chairs. Commoners do not see that room.

## Named NPCs

Pre-stocked, per DM Manual §6.

- **Warrant Officer Halsten Crebb.** Tired. Taps the warrant-case against his thigh when annoyed. Secret: failed his own son's licensing exam two years ago — boy ran north. Rank: warrant officer, Hollowmarch circuit 4 (covers Fallowton). Appears: the Office's Walk; also any in-session arrival.
- **Sensitive Ysmae Tann.** Watchful. Hums a six-note tune under her breath when the pendulum twitches. Secret: she was unsealed-born; her own mother smuggled her into the college at six and died of it. Rank: arcane sensitive, same squad. Appears: with Crebb.
- **Enforcer Old Brask.** Silent. Cleans his crossbow's nut with a thumbnail constantly. Secret: served under the previous duke; remembers a purge in '32 he will not discuss. Rank: enforcer, same squad. Appears: with Crebb.
- **Enforcer Ren Pell.** Eager. Smiles before he means to. Secret: writes letters home to a sister who thinks he's a coachman. Rank: enforcer, same squad. Appears: with Crebb.
- **Circuit Captain Maerea Voll.** Polished. Adjusts her gloves one finger at a time before speaking. ## The Office's Walk — Stillmonth 1

The cold, quiet holiday. Once a year. Late autumn. The fields are stubble; the gray wheat has just been sown; the village is between hard work and hard winter. The four arrive an hour after dawn.

**What happens, mechanically.** The squad rides the length of Fallowton at a walk. They stop at the inn and at Sister Elra's chapel. The warrant officer reads the founding writ aloud (forty-three seconds; the same forty-three seconds for 247 years). The sensitive walks once around the village green with the pendulum out. The enforcers stand by the horses. If the pendulum doesn't twitch, they ride on toward the next village before noon. An hour, end to end.

**What happens, in practice, from the village.** Goodman Tamsin packs the bag by his door three days before. Goodwife Bessa scrubs the inn floor twice the night before — Sealed Practice does not stay for a meal, but if they did, they would notice dust. Henric is told to be at the forge and to keep his head down and not to look at the horses. Sister Elra is up before dawn, vestments cleaner than they have been since Crowning. Children are not allowed outside until the dust settles. Any household with a known caster — even a licensed one — has a packed bag and a story they can tell in one breath. Some have two stories.

**From the village's point of view:** the year's longest hour. Nobody breathes properly until the dun horses are past the south road. Then somebody — usually the inn — pours a single round, and nobody toasts.

**From an unlicensed caster's point of view:** you do not cast for the week before. You do not cast for the day of. You stand somewhere visible, doing something boring, while the pendulum passes. Then you wait three weeks before you cast again, because pendulums sometimes remember.

## DM eyes — the Dawnweave parallel

The crown's logic is the Converter's logic, told in modern words. The Converter said: magic is too dangerous to be free; channel it through trained hands; the cost will be invisible because the people paying are not the people benefiting. The crown says: magic is too dangerous to be unsealed; license it through trained hands; the cost falls on commoners stripped of safe access to their own gift, on apprentices driven north into the woods, on children whose mothers smuggled them into colleges and died of the smuggling. Both systems are *not wrong about the danger.* Both systems are run by, and for, the people who do not pay. House Verant does not know it has rebuilt a Converter without the pyramid. There is no architect alive who could tell them. There is only Sister Elra, who knows the old prayer for the Lampset Vigil and will not say it during the day.

## DM use

Drop the Crown / Office into play like this:

- **Travel.** *"A half-mile east of the road, four dun horses ride single-file. Blue coats. They don't turn their heads."* — atmosphere; tells the party the Office exists; no scene required.
- **At the Crowning** *(Sownmonth 14, eight days before Module 1 opens — already happened in the fiction).* The granary still flies the blue-and-gold. Bessa hasn't taken it down. Nobody has.
- **The bag by the door.** When the party enters a house with a known caster, the bag is there. Players may or may not notice. If they ask, the householder will lie clearly.
- **Henric or another apprentice cast in front of the wrong person.** Three weeks later: the four ride into Fallowton off-schedule. *That* is a session. Use Crebb (tired) and Ysmae (watchful, secretly sympathetic). The PC casters have to choose what to do with their magic and their honesty.
- **The Office's Walk itself.** If Module 1 or 2 stretches into Stillmonth, run the Walk. One hour of in-game time. Twenty minutes of table time. Whoever in the party casts magic must decide whether to be visible doing nothing during the pendulum's circle.
- **Sister Elra's silence.** She is licensed. She also knows what the village won't say. A high Insight against her around the Office is worth one truth she's never said out loud.
