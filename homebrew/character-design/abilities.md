# Abilities in Fallowton

Where [`wiki/core/ability-scores.md`](../../wiki/core/ability-scores.md) tells you what the six abilities *do mechanically*, this file tells you what they *mean in this village*. Same math, same modifiers, same range — but the social weight of each score is local. A CHA 18 in the capital is a courtier; in Fallowton it is a stranger to watch.

This file is for **players** at character creation and for the **DM** when an NPC reacts to a score.

> **Mechanically:** we use BR-2024 RAW for the six abilities and for ability score generation. See [§B](#b--ability-score-generation-raw) below. The creativity in this campaign lives in the *interpretation* (§A) and in the **Lore sub-specialties** (§C).

---

## §A · The Six Abilities — Fallowton gloss

For each ability:

- **What it does (RAW)** — one-line mechanical reminder.
- **In Fallowton** — what the village reads this ability as.
- **High (16+)** — how a Fallowton villager sees a stranger or PC with this score.
- **Low (≤8)** — how a Fallowton villager sees a stranger or PC with this score.
- **Worth rolling for** — example checks specific to this campaign where the ability matters.

---

### Strength

- **What it does (RAW):** lifting, pushing, melee attack, Athletics. See [[strength]].
- **In Fallowton:** hands that *produce*. The smith's arms, the plowman's back, the woman who carries her own water from the well rather than ask.
- **High (16+):** respected when paired with usefulness. Suspected when paired with restlessness. *"Strong men who don't farm have somewhere else to be."*
- **Low (≤8):** forgiven, especially if your Constitution or Wisdom shows. Not pitied. The mill stones do the heavy lifting in this village; not everyone needs to.
- **Worth rolling for:** wet stone of the descent stairs (Athletics DC 10); shoving a tunnel-mouth stone aside; grappling Garret when he panics.

### Dexterity

- **What it does (RAW):** AC, initiative, finesse weapons, ranged attack, Stealth, Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand. See [[dexterity]].
- **In Fallowton:** the midwife's fingers, the poacher's footwork, the bowman's eye. Quiet competence.
- **High (16+):** valued for craft work, midwifery, hunting. *"Quick"* — both a compliment and a soft accusation. Quick hands have a way of holding what isn't theirs.
- **Low (≤8):** clumsiness; you trip on dry ground. The village ribs you about it; nobody trusts you with the lambs.
- **Worth rolling for:** descent stairs without slipping (Acrobatics DC 10); slipping past a guard during the Office's Walk; pickpocketing a sealed warrant.

### Constitution

- **What it does (RAW):** HP, Concentration saves, exhaustion resistance. See [[constitution]].
- **In Fallowton:** the **most respected** ability. *"That one's got bottom"* is the highest compliment a Fallowton elder pays a stranger. Surviving the winter, the field, the childbirth.
- **High (16+):** trusted automatically. Bran trusts a high-Con stranger faster than a high-Cha one. People volunteer information.
- **Low (≤8):** pitied, gently. Villagers quietly carry your share. You don't have to ask.
- **Worth rolling for:** **every Weave Strain Con save** (the strain cost, the burnout DC); resisting cold on the descent; pushing through the second day's ride.

### Intelligence

- **What it does (RAW):** wizard spellcasting, Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion. See [[intelligence]].
- **In Fallowton:** **suspect.** The wizard's ability. *Mordren is unsettling because his intelligence is open.* "Too clever by half" is the common phrase. Schoolteachers and outsiders.
- **High (16+):** a stranger with high Int gets watched. Bessa serves them politely. Bran does not invite them to weddings. Sister Elra does invite them to the shrine, once.
- **Low (≤8):** trusted. Not loved, not pitied — just trusted. Fallowton's economy doesn't reward cleverness; it rewards consistency.
- **Worth rolling for:** reading the Dawnweave inscriptions; recalling Voss-family history; cross-referencing the pale stone to the Wealdmark; activating a seal.

### Wisdom

- **What it does (RAW):** Perception, Insight, druid/cleric spellcasting, most saving throws against fear/charm. See [[wisdom]].
- **In Fallowton:** *"having sense."* Reading the weather. Reading the herd. Reading the village. **Sister Elra is high-Wis.** After Constitution, the most-trusted ability.
- **High (16+):** village elders consult you informally. You're not asked to vote, but your silence is noticed when others are speaking. Children quiet down when you sit down.
- **Low (≤8):** "missing the cue." You're the friend who shows up late. People wait for you, with affection.
- **Worth rolling for:** Sister Elra's prayer translation (Religion DC 12 + Old Common); whether Mordren is telling the whole truth (Insight DC 12); whether the ruin is *looking back* (silent Wis save vs the Awakening Clock).

### Charisma

- **What it does (RAW):** Persuasion, Deception, Intimidation, Performance; sorcerer/bard/warlock spellcasting. See [[charisma]].
- **In Fallowton:** distrusted in strangers, loved in your own. A bard from outside is *a charmer* — suspect. The same skills in Bessa Thorn make her the heart of the inn.
- **High (16+):** a stranger with high Cha is **doubly suspect** — the village suspects you're selling something even if you're not.
  - **[HOUSE]:** **Disadvantage on the first Persuasion or Deception check against any Fallowton elder by a stranger with CHA 16+, until trust is earned.** Earned trust unlocks normal rolls. Trust is earned through CON, WIS, or showing up.
- **Low (≤8):** "an honest face." Often genuinely trusted, in a way high-Cha NPCs are not. Some of the best diplomatic results in Fallowton come from low-Cha characters speaking plainly.
- **Worth rolling for:** convincing Bran to waive the bond (Persuasion DC 13 — see [`module-1-checks.md`](../../docs/dm-handbook/module-1-checks.md)); calming Garret; haggling with Tamsin.

## §B · Ability Score Generation (RAW)

We use **BR-2024 RAW** for ability score generation. No homebrew overlay on the math.

Three methods are available — pick one per player:

| Method | How it works | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard array** | Distribute `15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8` as you choose | Players new to D&D, or sessions where chargen needs to be fast |
| **Point buy** | 27 points to spend; each ability starts at 8; costs from [BR-2024](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024/character-origins#OptionalRule:CustomizingYourOrigin) | Players who want to optimize; lets you minmax cleanly |
| **4d6 drop lowest** | Roll 4d6 six times, drop the lowest die from each set; assign as you like | Players who want chaos and roll-driven storytelling |

See [`wiki/concepts/ability-score-generation.md`](../../wiki/concepts/ability-score-generation.md) for the full RAW.

> **[HOUSE] Re-rolls:** A player may request a single re-roll of one ability if their starting total (sum of all 6 scores) is below 65, OR if their highest score is below 13. DM may grant; if granted, narrate why (you were sick that day; the village priest blessed you wrong; the road shaped you differently than you expected).

> **[HOUSE] Background gives the mark.** Whichever generation method you pick, your **background's listed Ability Scores** (e.g. Sinkhole Witness lists STR, CON, WIS) carry **+1 to ONE of those three** at chargen, on top of your generated scores. This is RAW-compatible — backgrounds already grant a +2 / +1 or +1×3 in BR-2024. We use the version where you pick one of the three to get +1, on top of your roll/array/buy.

## §C · Sub-specialty Lores (the creative add-on)

A **Lore** is a narrow factual specialty your character has acquired through training, family, or experience. Lores are gained from specific backgrounds, specific feats, or DM-granted via story.

Lores are **not skills.** They sit on top of skills. When the topic of your Lore comes up at the table, you get two benefits:

### How Lores work mechanically

1. **The free question (1 / long rest per Lore).** When a topic in your Lore is relevant in the current scene, you may ask the DM **one specific factual question** about it. The DM answers honestly within your character's knowledge. If the answer is unknown to your character, the DM narrates *what your character knows to be uncertain about* — you get the shape of the gap.
2. **Advantage on related checks.** When making an Investigation, History, Religion, Arcana, or Nature check whose subject falls within your Lore, you have **advantage** on the roll. This does not stack with other sources of advantage; it just gives you the advantage if you didn't already have one.

### How many Lores you can have

| Tier | Max Lores |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 (levels 1–4) | 1 |
| Tier 2 (levels 5–10) | 2 |
| Tier 3 (levels 11–16) | 3 |
| Tier 4 (levels 17–20) | 4 |

You gain your first Lore at character creation (from background or feat). Additional Lores are gained as you tier up, **only by acquiring them in fiction** — a year of study, an apprenticeship with an NPC who has it, finding and reading a specific book. The DM gates this; you don't unlock Lores automatically by leveling.

### The four Lores at launch

#### Lore (Dawnweave)

The ancient pre-Sealing era — its architects, its Converters, the Sealing event, the script carved into ruin walls. **NOT the full DM truth** (that's gated behind discovery in play), but everything a Religion DC 17 or Arcana DC 17 would reveal under normal study.

- **Granted by:** [Ruin Scholar](backgrounds/ruin-scholar.md) (free, automatic); [Failed Apprentice](backgrounds/failed-apprentice.md) (free, automatic); a future Custom Feat "Initiated Reader" (planned).
- **Sample free questions:** *What kind of architect built this seal? Is this Converter still operating? Is this an active seal or a memorial one? What was this room originally for?*
- **Advantage on:** Arcana, History, Religion checks tied to Dawnweave architecture, language, machines, or theology.

#### Lore (Hedge)

Village folk-knowledge. The grandmother's-knee curriculum: which mushroom is which, which sheep is sick, how to tell a real fever from a turned stomach, how to mend a tool with what you have on the road. The unwritten survival manual of rural life.

- **Granted by:** [Sinkhole Witness](../../docs/campaign/background-sinkhole-witness.md) (free, automatic); [Village Doctor](backgrounds/village-doctor.md) (free, automatic); [Mechanist](backgrounds/mechanist.md) (optional choice); the Outlander or Folk Hero background (when those are written).
- **Sample free questions:** *Is this mushroom safe? What did kill that animal? Will this ford hold tonight? Can this tool be repaired with what's in my pack?*
- **Advantage on:** Nature, Survival, Medicine checks tied to rural Fallowton-region life.

#### Lore (Crown Law)

How the kingdom and the Office of Sealed Practice actually work. What a warrant officer can do, what they cannot (without a writ from a Provincial Warden). When a magistrate's judgment binds, when it doesn't. The procedure of an arrest. The procedure of escaping one without becoming a criminal.

- **Granted by:** Criminal background (free, automatic); Soldier background (free, automatic); a future Noble or Magistrate background (planned).
- **Sample free questions:** *Can the Office search my pack without a warrant? Is this writ valid? Does this magistrate have authority here? What's the lawful penalty for what I just did?*
- **Advantage on:** Investigation, History checks tied to crown law, the Office's procedures, or the official structure of the Sealed Realm.

#### Lore (Voss-Family)

A specific Fallowton bloodline. The Voss line back four generations — the marriages, the inheritances, the disappearance of **Wendel Voss** sixty-to-a-hundred years ago into the House of First Light, the dagger he carried (the W.V. dagger that may surface in Module 1), and the rumor that the line carries "old blood."

This is the narrowest Lore — most useful in this campaign specifically, less so in other campaigns.

- **Granted by:** Sinkhole Witness *with* a specific Henric connection (DM-gated; not automatic — your character must have been close enough to Henric's family to have heard the stories); a future "Voss-Blooded" feat (planned, requires DM approval).
- **Sample free questions:** *Whose line did Wendel marry into? Where is his dagger now? Was his disappearance reported to the Office? Who in the village remembers him personally?*
- **Advantage on:** Investigation or History checks tied to the Voss family specifically. Some Religion checks involving Lampset Vigil (the Voss line is connected to it).

### What Lores are NOT

- They are NOT skills. You still need the underlying skill proficiency for non-advantage uses of Investigation, History, etc.
- They are NOT feats. They are a smaller, more thematic unlock that fits with feats rather than replacing them.
- They are NOT class features. Any class can have any Lore (subject to background/feat access).
- They do NOT scale into raw damage, AC, or save numbers. The mechanical effect is **advantage + free question**, not stat bumps.

### Adding new Lores later

The DM may introduce additional Lores as the campaign expands — *Lore (Office Politics)*, *Lore (Saltspeech Trade)*, *Lore (Calder Vault Diocese)*. Any narrow factual specialty earned in fiction can become a Lore. The campaign starts with these four; more accumulate over time.
