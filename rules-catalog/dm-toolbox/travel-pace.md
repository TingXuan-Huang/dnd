# DM Toolbox: Travel Pace

Source: [D&D Beyond 2024 DM’s Toolbox](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024/dms-toolbox)

Overland travel pace, terrain limits, extended travel, and vehicles.

<a id="TravelPace"></a>
## Travel Pace

A group of characters can travel overland at a Normal, Fast, or Slow pace, as described in the “ [Playing the Game](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024/playing-the-game#TravelPace) ”. During any journey stage, the predominant terrain determines the characters’ maximum travel pace, as shown in the Maximum Pace column of the [Travel Terrain](#TravelTerrain) table. Certain factors can affect a group’s travel pace.

<a id="GoodRoads"></a>
### Good Roads

The presence of a good road increases the group’s maximum pace by one step (from Slow to Normal or from Normal to Fast).

<a id="SlowerTravelers"></a>
### Slower Travelers

The group must move at a Slow pace if any group member’s Speed is reduced to half or less of normal.

<a id="ExtendedTravel"></a>
### Extended Travel

Characters can push themselves to travel for more than 8 hours per day, at the risk of tiring. At the end of each additional hour of travel beyond 8 hours, each character must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or gain 1 [Exhaustion](../rules/conditions.md#ExhaustionCondition) level. The DC is 10 plus 1 for each hour past 8 hours.

<a id="SpecialMovement"></a>
### Special Movement

If a party can travel at a high Speed for an extended time, as with a spell such as [Wind Walk](https://www.dndbeyond.com/spells/2619211-wind-walk) or a magic item such as a [Carpet of Flying](https://www.dndbeyond.com/magic-items/9228381-carpet-of-flying), translate the party’s Speed into travel rates using these rules:

**Miles per hour** = Speed ÷ 10

**Miles per day** (Normal pace) = Miles per hour × number of hours traveled (typically 8)

**Fast pace** = Miles per day × 1⅓ (round down)

**Slow pace** = Miles per day × 2/3 (round down)

If the characters are flying or their special movement allows them to ignore [Difficult Terrain](../rules/movement.md#DifficultTerrain), they can move at a Fast pace regardless of the terrain.

<a id="Vehicles"></a>
### Vehicles

Characters traveling in a vehicle use the vehicle’s speed in miles per hour (as shown in “ [Equipment](https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024/equipment#AirborneandWaterborneVehicles) ”) to determine their rate of travel, and they don’t choose a travel pace.

<a id="TravelTerrain"></a>
### Travel Terrain

| Terrain | Maximum Pace | Encounter Distance | Foraging DC | Navigation DC | Search DC |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Arctic | Fast* | 6d6 × 10 feet | 20 | 10 | 10 |
| Coastal | Normal | 2d10 × 10 feet | 10 | 5 | 15 |
| Desert | Normal | 6d6 × 10 feet | 20 | 10 | 10 |
| Forest | Normal | 2d8 × 10 feet | 10 | 15 | 15 |
| Grassland | Fast | 6d6 × 10 feet | 15 | 5 | 15 |
| Hill | Normal | 2d10 × 10 feet | 15 | 10 | 15 |
| Mountain | Slow | 4d10 × 10 feet | 20 | 15 | 20 |
| Swamp | Slow | 2d8 × 10 feet | 10 | 15 | 20 |
| Underdark | Normal | 2d6 × 10 feet | 20 | 10 | 20 |
| Urban | Normal | 2d6 × 10 feet | 20 | 15 | 15 |
| Waterborne | Special† | 6d6 × 10 feet | 15 | 10 | 15 |

*Appropriate equipment (such as skis) is necessary to keep up a Fast pace in Arctic terrain.
†Characters’ rate of travel while waterborne depends on the vehicle carrying them; see “ [Vehicles](#Vehicles).”
