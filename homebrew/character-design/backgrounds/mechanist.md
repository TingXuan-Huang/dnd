# Background: Mechanist

*Homebrew · Fallowton campaign · Formatted like D&D 2024 Basic Rules backgrounds*

Use this background if your character grew up around mills, pumps, locks, clockwork, siege engines, or the kind of mining gear the crown likes to keep quiet about — and knows the difference between a machine that runs on gears and a machine that runs on something else.

---

## Mechanist

You think in linkages. A pulley is not a mystery; a sluice gate is not a mystery; a lock is just a problem someone wrote out in iron. You worked the mill, or your father's smithy, or a crown-licensed pump crew on the canal four towns over. You can tell which axle a barn-roof needs by the sound it makes in wind.

Then someone showed you a Dawnweave panel, or you saw one alone, and your hands itched. The geometry was wrong on purpose. The notches did not transfer force — they transferred something else. You could *almost* see what it would take to make it turn again, and that is the part that scares you, and the part you cannot stop thinking about.

In Fallowton you are useful and trusted, until the day you reach for the wrong handle.

## Optional personality prompts

- What is the most complicated machine you have built or repaired with your own hands? Be specific about the part that went wrong before you fixed it.
- The first time you saw a Dawnweave panel up close, what did your hands want to do *before* your head caught up?
- You have a private theory about how the buried Converter works — something no one taught you. Are you proud of it, or do you wish you had never thought of it?

## Weave Strain (if you gain magic later)

You do not start as a spellcaster, and most mundane gears are exactly that — mundane. Your background is designed so the everyday work you love does not count against the broken channel.

- Operating, repairing, or improvising with **mundane machinery** — mills, locks, traps, siege engines, normal Mechanist devices — does **not** cause Weave Strain and does **not** add Luck debt. The crown's licensed pumps do not bite back.
- If a spell **powers** Dawnweave machinery — turning a crystal, energizing a panel, completing a circuit in a Converter chamber — treat the cast as a **Sorcerer** for luck purposes for the rest of that day: **+1 Luck debt** the first time it happens, and the day's strain options favor blood-and-bloodline costs over memory ones.
- Standard **burnout** rules apply, **unless** the spell that pushed you to strain 3 was powering **Converter** machinery in the House of First Light. In that case the DM should favor the **Overreach Backlash** table (see [Weave Strain Magic](../../../docs/campaign/weave-strain-magic.md#overreach-backlash-converter-logic)) — the machine spills what you tried to channel, and your allies pay alongside you. This is the part the crown does not put in the licensing paperwork.

See [Weave Strain Magic — Mechanist](../../../docs/campaign/weave-strain-magic.md#custom-fallowton-backgrounds).

---

Spellcasters should read [Weave Strain Magic](../../../docs/campaign/weave-strain-magic.md). Any class is allowed. Artificer-flavor Wizards, Eldritch Knights, and Sorcerers with an inherited gift all fit — but the background's whole point is that *mundane competence* protects you. Do not give that up lightly.
