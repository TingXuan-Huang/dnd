# Regional Geography — The Fallow Reach

The country around Fallowton. Not a kingdom map — a walking map. What's a day off, what's a week off, what direction the trouble comes from. Distances are in the units villagers actually use: a day's walk (24 miles), a day on horseback (32), a wagon-day on the post road (18, if the road's dry).

> See also: [[weave-strain-magic]] (the old crown country to the west, the "fifty miles" trade radius), [[background-sinkhole-witness]] (pale stone "unlike anything quarried within fifty miles"), [[calendar]] (the work the land does month by month), [[crown-and-office]] (unbuilt — the Office's provincial routes).

## Fallowton, anchored

Fallowton sits in temperate farm country — barley, gray wheat, turnips, hedgerows — on the **Slowmere**, a brown river that takes its time getting east. The village straddles the **Fallow Road**, the spur off the King's East Way that runs from Marrowford (the nearest market town, a day's ride east) through Fallowton and dies in the hedges of the hill country a half-day west. North field, south field, mill, inn, chapel, smithy. Past the hedgerows in every direction: more farms like it, smaller, scattered, until the land changes. The whole region is called **the Fallow Reach** by people in cities and "around here" by everyone in it.

## Five regions within a week's walk

### 1 · Mereswood (the forest west of town)

- **Distance:** half a day's walk west. You're in it by noon.
- **Description:** mixed oak-and-beech forest on the rising ground between the Slowmere's headwaters and the old crown country. Charcoal-burners, swineherds, two woodward families. Some of the trees are old enough that the swineherds will not strike them.
- **Feature:** the **Stoneford** — a river crossing where the Slowmere narrows over a slab of the same pale stone as the sinkhole. Nobody quarried it. It was here first. Wagons cross dry-shod in summer.
- **L1–5 hook:** the forest is the front door to the old crown country. Anything Tamsin or Mordren needs from "past the forest" comes through Mereswood first. Also: something has been spoiling traps near the Stoneford since the rains stopped.

### 2 · Marrowford

- **Distance:** one day east by horse, day-and-a-half on foot. Down the Fallow Road, then the King's East Way.
- **Description:** a real market town on a bend in the Slowmere where the river widens enough for a stone bridge. Tanners, smiths, a Wednesday cattle fair, a chapter house of the Office. (See *Nearby town* below.)
- **Feature:** the **Three Bells** — the town's coaching inn, where the post is posted and the magistrates drink.
- **L1–5 hook:** anyone wanting a real smith, a real letter sent, or news that travels further than gossip goes to Marrowford. The Office's outpost makes it a place to be careful in.

### 3 · The Bracken Downs

- **Distance:** two days' walk south-southwest. Past the last hedgerow, the ground rises into open sheep country.
- **Description:** rolling chalk downs, gorse, sheep, kites overhead. Half a dozen shepherding hamlets the crown taxes by counting the flock. **Ralla's Cairn,** the highest hill, has a ring of standing stones that nobody plants near.
- **Feature:** the **Shearing Pens** at Westfold hamlet — where the wool from forty miles of downs is sorted in late Brightleaf. Drovers, packmen, three days of noise.
- **L1–5 hook:** sheep are getting their throats opened at night and nothing's eating them. The shepherds blame wolves. The wolves are afraid of whatever is doing it.

### 4 · Cinderfen

- **Distance:** three days' walk north along the Slowmere, then east at the fork.
- **Description:** sedge fen, peat, slow water, sky. Fishers, fowlers, reed-cutters. The crown collects little from Cinderfen and visits less. People there speak with a flatter vowel and bury their dead in the peat.
- **Feature:** **the Drowned Mill** — a ruined Dawnweave-era waterwheel half-sunk in the fen, with pale stone in its foundations. Local children dare each other to spend the night on its boards. None has, since the rains.
- **L1–5 hook:** another exposed piece of the old machinery, less guarded than Fallowton's. If the House of First Light has cousins, the Drowned Mill is one. Good for a level-3 expedition with consequences they can carry home.

### 5 · The Hollowing Hills

- **Distance:** four days' walk north-northeast. The road peters out; you follow drovers' trails.
- **Description:** broken upland country of slate and heather. Old mine workings — tin and silver — most played out two generations back. **Banthorp,** the largest mining village, still works the deeper seams. Crown reeves visit twice a year for the assay.
- **Feature:** the **Black Adit** at Banthorp — a shaft that goes farther down than the silver ever did. The miners stopped digging in 232 SA. Nobody quite remembers why.
- **L1–5 hook:** the Adit's air smells faintly of the same iron-and-old-smoke as the Converter stairs. Mordren has heard about it but has not been. A party finishing Module 1 in good order could walk here in Module 2 and find the second piece of the lattice.

## The nearby town — Marrowford

- **Population:** ~1,400. Town, not city. A magistrate, not a lord.
- **Three Bells inn** — coaching stop, post hub, where the King's Messenger changes horses every fourth day. The taproom has news older than rumor and newer than letters.
- **Office chapter house** — a low stone building on the bridge approach with the Office's sealed-knot painted on the lintel. Two clerks, four riders, one captain. They are correct and quiet. They are not friends.
- **Market square** — Wednesday cattle, Saturday general. The pillory is still there but hasn't been used in this magistrate's time.
- **Saint Veyne's chapel** — the chapter Sister Elra reports to. The senior priest, Reverend Tassen, has known her since she was an oblate and does not entirely trust her any more.
- **Political tone:** **grudgingly tolerant.** Crown-loyal in public, weather-loyal in private. The magistrate prefers a quiet province to a correct one. The Office captain notices this and is patient.

## The regional city — Calder Vault

- **Population:** ~14,000. Walled. Real stone, four gates, a cathedral with a copper dome gone green.
- **Distance:** four days east of Fallowton by wagon on the King's East Way, three by hard horse.
- **The Vault proper** — the cathedral undercrofts, where the diocese keeps its records and the city its grain reserve. Lit day and night. Tour for a copper on holy days.
- **The Sealed Court** — the Office's regional headquarters, a black-brick compound on the south wall. Twenty riders. A standing magister. This is where warrants are signed.
- **The Long Bridge** — five spans over the Slowmere where it's finally wide enough to matter. The dye-works are downstream of it; the wine-shops are upstream.
- **Why Fallowton folk visit:** to find a daughter who left for work, to buy iron the village smith can't, to petition the diocese about a chapel matter, or — once or twice in a lifetime — to be summoned. Nobody from Fallowton visits Calder Vault for fun.

## The old crown country (west, past the forest)

Tamsin's "fifty miles, west, past the forest" is a real direction. Walk through Mereswood, climb the long ridge on its far side, and the land changes. The hedgerows stop. The ground goes up in hard steps to a region the maps call the **Wealdmark** and old people call the **old crown country**.

It's broken hill country, not mountains — chalk and limestone shoulders, blind valleys, the bones of three burnt villages, and ruined keeps with their gates open and their wells dry. The pale stone in Fallowton's sinkhole comes from here; the quarries are still findable, mostly choked. Two hundred and forty-seven years ago this was the seat of the **pre-Sealing crown** — the line the gods' Sealing ended. After the seal, the surviving claimants moved east to found what is now the modern kingdom; the Wealdmark was left to scrub and weather and whatever the old courts left behind.

**Walkable from Fallowton in:** three days hard on foot to the first ruined keep (**Greycairn**), five days to the high quarries.

**Danger for level 1:** **doable, but only the edge.** Greycairn's outer baileys are picked over and the wolves are ordinary wolves. Past Greycairn into the deeper Wealdmark — old wards, things that fed on the Sealing's aftermath — is **suicide** until tier 2. Tamsin and Mordren both know this. So does whoever else has been quietly mapping it.

## The capital

**Highmoor-on-Anvel.** Three weeks east by wagon, ten days hard riding. The seat of the sealed-age crown — the line that crowned itself 247 years ago this Sownmonth. Political meaning: the Office answers there. The diocese answers there. Every magistrate in the Fallow Reach has, in their desk, a sealed letter from Highmoor they hope never to open. Nobody in Fallowton has ever been. Sister Elra has been once. She does not talk about it.

Players will not reach Highmoor in chapter 1. It exists on the map because it explains why everyone behaves the way they do.

## Travel times reference

Distances Fallowton → destination.

| Destination | On foot | On horseback | By wagon |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mereswood (forest edge) | half day | 2 hours | 3 hours |
| Stoneford (within Mereswood) | 1 day | 4 hours | 1 day |
| Marrowford | 1.5 days | 1 day | 1.5 days |
| Bracken Downs (Westfold) | 2 days | 1 day | 2.5 days |
| Cinderfen (Drowned Mill) | 3 days | 1.5 days | impassable past fork |
| Hollowing Hills (Banthorp) | 4 days | 2 days | 3 days |
| Greycairn (Wealdmark edge) | 3 days | 1.5 days | 2 days to forest, then no road |
| Calder Vault (regional city) | 6 days | 3 days | 4 days |
| Highmoor (the capital) | 4 weeks | 10 days | 3 weeks |

A wagon-day assumes the post road is dry and the wagon is loaded — Fallowton-to-Marrowford is faster on foot in late spring after eighteen days of rain. The road *is* dry now. It has been for one morning.
