# Getting Started — Fallowton Campaign

**This is the only file you need to read before session 1.** Everything else is a deep reference for when you want more. Plan: ~30 minutes to read; ~30 minutes to build a character; show up to session 1 ready.

---

## What this campaign is (60 seconds)

- **Setting:** **Fallowton**, a small farming village. Something is buried under the north field — a ruin from an ancient era called the **Dawnweave**, when magic moved freely through the world. It does not move freely anymore.
- **Tone:** grim but humane. The campaign is about people who *care for each other* in a world where magic has a price. Death is possible. Joy is possible too.
- **System:** D&D 5.5e (2024 Basic Rules) + Fallowton-specific homebrew. The math is RAW; the texture is ours.

## Character creation in five steps (the short path)

### Step 1 — Pick a class

One of the 12 RAW classes from D&D Beyond: Barbarian, Bard, Cleric, Druid, Fighter, Monk, Paladin, Ranger, Rogue, Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard. (Artificer is also available; ask the DM.)

Don't agonize. Pick what sounds fun. Class is the *shape* of how you fight and what magic you have.

### Step 2 — Pick a background

Backgrounds give you skill proficiencies, a tool proficiency, an Origin feat, equipment, and a story hook.

**Fallowton-specific backgrounds** (5 to choose from):

| Background | Vibe |
|---|---|
| **[Sinkhole Witness](../campaign/background-sinkhole-witness.md)** | Lifelong villager. You were there when the ground collapsed. |
| **[Ruin Scholar](../../homebrew/character-design/backgrounds/ruin-scholar.md)** | Outsider. You came to Fallowton *for* the seals. |
| **[Failed Apprentice](../../homebrew/character-design/backgrounds/failed-apprentice.md)** | You were taught the price of magic before you paid it. |
| **[Mechanist](../../homebrew/character-design/backgrounds/mechanist.md)** | Hands-on across mundane and Dawnweave-touched devices. |
| **[Village Doctor](../../homebrew/character-design/backgrounds/village-doctor.md)** | Healing without paying strain. The party's hands. |

Or pick one of the 4 RAW backgrounds (Acolyte, Criminal, Sage, Soldier) — they work fine. Ask the DM.

### Step 3 — Pick a species

One of the 9 RAW species: Dragonborn, Dwarf, Elf, Gnome, Goliath, Halfling, Human, Orc, Tiefling. Each grants traits like darkvision, breath weapons, etc.

### Step 4 — Generate ability scores (RAW)

Three methods (pick one):

| Method | How |
|---|---|
| **Standard array** | Distribute `15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8` across the six stats |
| **Point buy** | 27 points to spend; 8 minimum, 15 maximum |
| **4d6 drop lowest** | Roll 4d6 six times, drop the lowest die from each set |

**Background bonus**: your background lists 3 abilities (e.g., Sinkhole Witness lists STR, CON, WIS). Pick ONE of those three and give it **+1** at chargen. [HOUSE rule]

If your total of all 6 scores is below 65, OR your highest score is below 13, the DM may grant ONE re-roll. Just ask.

### Step 5 — Equipment

Your background's Choice A is a themed kit + small cash. Choice B is **50 GP** to buy from the equipment list. Pick.

Full reference: [`equipment-reference.md`](equipment-reference.md) and [`weapons-reference.md`](weapons-reference.md).

## The 5 things to know mechanically (read this carefully)

### 1 · Magic has a cost (Weave Strain)

If you cast spells, you carry **Weave Strain**, a number 0-3 that increases each time you cast a leveled spell. Higher strain means you take damage and bad luck. **Read [`../campaign/weave-strain-magic.md`](../campaign/weave-strain-magic.md) if you're playing a caster.**

If you don't cast, skip this. You'll still encounter it in NPCs.

### 2 · Wounds happen when you go down (the body remembers)

When your HP hits 0 and you survive, **you take one Wound** from a list (broken rib, twisted ankle, bleeding gash, etc.). The Wound stays until properly healed (Medicine DC 12 + kit + long rest). Multiple Wounds make subsequent fights more dangerous.

There's also one special Wound called **Hollowed** — only caused by Dawnweave-touched damage. It needs cleansing magic or Sister Elra's blessing.

### 3 · Carry weight matters (four-tier system)

Track your gear weight. Your load tier (based on STR × multiplier) gives:

| Load tier | Effect |
|---|---|
| Light (≤ STR × 5 lb) | No penalty |
| Standard (to STR × 10) | −1 to initiative; fatigue check on long marches |
| Heavy (to STR × 15) | −10 ft speed; disadv DEX checks |
| Overload (to STR × 20) | Most things have disadvantage |

A typical Fighter in chain mail + greatsword + pack is at **Standard**. A typical Wizard is at **Light**. Plan accordingly. Full system: [`../../homebrew/physicality.md`](../../homebrew/physicality.md).

### 4 · The Hollow Star is watching (The Notice)

The campaign has a single tracker called **The Notice** — how aware an ancient cosmic horror is of you and the world. Touching Dawnweave magic, surviving close-calls with death, reading certain texts → Notice ticks up.

At low Notice (1-3): bad dreams; NPCs sometimes act strange around you.
At mid Notice (4-6): real penalties; you might lose a memory, take an extra Strain, take physical-stat damage.
At high Notice (7-10): your character starts becoming something else.

**The Notice is the single most important mechanical tracker in this campaign.** Full system: [`../../homebrew/the-notice.md`](../../homebrew/the-notice.md).

### 5 · Joy heals you (the one positive system)

Genuine joy at the table — a holiday, a romance milestone, a roleplayed reunion — gives you **+1 HP per long rest for 3 days** and heals one Wound at half time. The grim systems are real; so is this one. Lean into it.

## What to expect at the table

### The DM's contract with you

- **The DM is your editor, not your adversary.** The dice are the judge; the DM keeps the camera on the right person.
- **No "rocks fall."** If the DM has to break the rules to make you fail, it's the DM's bad, not yours.
- **Free explore + fixed pivots.** Most of the session you choose what to do. A few story rails fire on time (the bell rings; the night falls). The DM controls those.
- **Open vs Silent checks:** Some rolls are open (you roll, you see the result). Some are silent (the DM rolls behind the screen, narrates only on success). Don't ask why; just play your character.

### Your contract with the DM

- **Show up prepared.** Character sheet ready. Dice on hand.
- **Don't railroad each other.** If a PC wants to be the talker in this scene, let them. Your turn is coming.
- **Speak up when you're lost.** If you don't know what to do, ask. The DM will set the scene.
- **Lean into the texture.** This world's grim mechanics work because *players engage with them*. If you treat Wounds and Trauma as roleplay-only flavor, the campaign is less.

### Death and consequences

- Death is **possible**. The DM will telegraph it (you'll know when it's serious).
- The DM **does not fudge dice against you**. Ever.
- The DM **may fudge once per campaign FOR you** in two narrow cases: (a) a TPK from a single unlucky roll on a setup combat; (b) a new player's first character dying in session 1.
- If you die and are revived, you carry a **Permanent Vision** — a fact about your character that lives on the sheet forever.

## Session 1 cheat sheet (rip this out, tape to your sheet)

```
HP: ______  /  ______ (current/max)
Wounds carried: _________________________
Weave Strain (if caster): 0  1  2  3
Notice (campaign-shared tracker): 0-10
Load tier: Light / Standard / Heavy / Overload
Active spells: _________________________

ATTACK ROLL: 1d20 + proficiency (if proficient) + ability modifier
SKILL CHECK: 1d20 + proficiency (if proficient) + ability modifier
SAVE:        1d20 + proficiency (if listed) + ability modifier

DC ladder: 10 easy / 13 default / 15 hard / 18 very hard

ACTIONS PER TURN:
  · 1 Action (Attack, Cast, Dash, Disengage, Dodge, Help, Hide, Magic,
              Ready, Search, Study, Utilize, Influence)
  · 1 Bonus Action (if feature grants)
  · 1 Reaction between turns (Opportunity Attack, etc.)
  · Movement up to your speed (split across the turn)
  · 1 Free Object Interaction (draw, sheathe, open door, drop)

CRITICAL HIT: max damage dice + roll damage dice (homebrew)
NAT 20 SKILL: succeed + one extra benefit (story event)
NAT 1 SKILL:  fail + one complication (story event)

HEROIC INSPIRATION: earn by playing in-character / supporting tablemate /
                   showing up prepared. Spend = re-roll any d20,
                   TAKE THE SECOND ROLL (not the better).

ROLL TO STAY ALIVE:
  · 0 HP + stable → take 1 Wound, get back up
  · 3 successful death saves → stable at 0 HP
  · 3 failed death saves → die (your final Vision lives on)

YOUR DM IS ON YOUR SIDE.
```
